## 2. #1: Churn Là Lỗ Hổng — Hãy Chẩn Đoán Nó

Think of **churn** like a **leaky bug net** — then diagnose what's actually happening.

Your **MR** (monthly recurring revenue) grows when **new members > churn members**.

**Examples:**
- Sell 5, lose 4 → MR grows
- Sell 5 new, 5 churn → growth is flat
- New members < churned members → declining **Tactical:** Track two numbers monthly — **joins vs. cancels** — then force yourself to label the business as **growing**, **flat**, or **declining** based on that.

## 3. #2: Benchmark Tỷ Lệ Giữ Chân

Thing two, you want to use **retention benchmarks** — so you can know if you're good.

**Context:** This isn't churn for something like an insurance premium or an alarm system. But if you sell anything that is a consumer service — weight loss, remote service, or any online service — these metrics will probably be much more correlated to what you're at.

**School platform averages:** - **Platform average:** 80% monthly retention (20% churn)
- **Best groups:** less than 10% churn — aka 90% retention month over month
- **Not good groups:** less than 70% retention — aka 30% or higher churn **Tactic:** If less than 7 out of 10 people want to stay on a monthly basis, you probably need to work on **retention** more than anything else — it'll be the highest **ROI lever**.

### Tác Động Của Việc Giảm Churn

**Impact:** Taking churn from 20% → 10% — or said differently, retention from 80% → 90% — doesn't just give you a 10–12% boost. It **doubles LTV per customer**, meaning customers stay twice as long when you make that change.

**Key insight:** One of the big things people get confused about is these small numbers. The smaller the number, the bigger the change by percentages when you make these small tweaks.

**Note:** This is all monthly in comparison to others — not in comparison to having a sellable business or anything like that.

## 4. #3: Churn Giảm Mạnh Theo Thời Gian

**Key rule:** **Churn drops dramatically with time.** It's easy to think *"my average churn is 10% a month"* — but when you look at **cohorts of churn** (people at their 6th month vs. 2nd month vs. 3rd month), the **averages across platform differ significantly by month**.

**Churn by milestone:** - **Month 1–3:** Highest churn — **20%+**
- **Day 90:** Drops to **~10% or less** — roughly half *"Huh, that's kind of interesting."* - **Month 6:** Drops to **~2%** — a **5x decrease** in churn, or a **5x increase in LTV** **Implication:** A lot of people obsess over "how do I get my monthly churn down." An easier reframe — not *how do I make this number go down*, but:
- How do I get someone to stay past **day 90**?
- How do I get these people to stay past **month 6**?

These become much more **tactical** in terms of what you can actually do.

**Caveat:** If your community or recurring revenue is less than **90 days old**, your churn will always be inflated — you haven't gotten customers who've made it past that point yet. Same logic applies if it's less than **6 months old**, just a little less pronounced.

Don't overhaul your entire business because your **month 1 churn** is high — that's normal for it to be higher than other months. Unless basically everyone is leaving, in which case, yes, go fix that.

### Chiến Thuật Cho Từng Mốc Thời Gian

Focus on **three tactics**, one per milestone: 1. **Month 1 — Activation:** Biggest month of churn. Strategy comes from **activation**, **onboarding**, **expectation setting**, selling the right promises on the front end, showing up every day, making sure you're providing content in that environment.

2. **Day 90 — Outcome:** Needs to deliver some sort of **outcome** — some sort of **change in behavior** for them.

3. **Month 6 — Community:** Usually about them **connecting with other people** within that community.

## 5. #4: Hỏi Người Hủy Tại Sao Họ Rời Đi

Now, number four, **ask people why they canceled** and then look for the patterns.

Earlier on in my career, when someone says, *"Oh, it cost too much,"* I would just ignore them. I'd be like, *"Ah, they just don't understand the value."* But over time, I've learned to appreciate the feedback.

**Example —** Sam has the head of customer support sit right next to him in the whole office. The reason for that is because he wanted a very **tight feedback loop** between the problems customers are experiencing, the reasons they're canceling, and the things that are getting built. That's why School is such a strong platform.

**Tactically:** 1. **Message people who are canceling** — ask *"Hey, why are you canceling?"*
2. **Don't kneejerk** on one response — look for **patterns**, collect enough feedback to see them
3. **Create a simple DM script** to reach out consistently
4. **Tag the reason** in an Excel sheet — categories like:
   - Price
   - Overwhelm
   - Not using it
   - Missing a feature
   - Not what I expected
5. **For every ~20 cancels**, review the top 1–2 reasons and fix those Please do this.

## 7. Giá Trị Cốt Lõi Và Retention Engine

**Key insight:** What's the number one value that you get here? Whatever shows up repeatedly becomes your **retention engine** — and everything else after that is really optional.

Nếu chỉ có một thứ giữ người ta ở lại, tại sao lại làm năm thứ? Đây là bài học mất quá lâu để học, vì ta thường tin rằng để sửa thứ gì đó, phải sửa bằng cách *thêm vào*.

**Solution:** Thực ra cần sửa bằng cách **subtraction** — loại bỏ và tinh gọn, tức là làm tốt hơn thay vì thêm nhiều hơn. Nếu đơn giản là fulfil đúng lời hứa ban đầu, người ta có lẽ sẽ ở lại.

**On improving product quality:**
1. Đưa ra một **promise**
2. Loại bỏ toàn bộ **friction** liên quan đến việc đạt được điều đó **Principle:** Những business, product, và service thắng là những cái đơn giản làm cho mọi thứ tệ liên quan đến việc đạt được điều tốt *biến mất*.

**Case study:** School về cơ bản giúp người ta bắt đầu online. Tất cả những gì đã làm là tối ưu để làm cho nó dễ nhất có thể — không phải bằng cách thêm features, mà bằng cách làm cho mọi thứ ở nền tảng *biến mất* để nó chỉ đơn giản là hoạt động.

## 8. Price Objections: Định Giá Dựa Trên Bằng Chứng

**Key insight:** Đừng đoán mò — hãy **định giá dựa trên bằng chứng thực tế**.

Tại School, họ nhận thấy nhiều người hủy đăng ký với lý do *quá đắt*. Đây là case study điển hình về cách xử lý **price objection** bằng dữ liệu.

**Case study: School — tạo lower tier** Họ giữ nguyên tier cao với các tính năng nâng cao:
- Additional admins
- Unique URLs
- No sidebars quảng cáo competitor groups Và tạo thêm tier rẻ hơn **11 lần**: từ **$99/tháng** xuống còn **$9/tháng**.

**Why it worked:** Nhiều nhóm hobby và người mới bắt đầu chưa kiếm được nhiều tiền. Chênh lệch giữa $100 và $9 là rất lớn — tier mới giúp họ ở lại, tiếp tục dùng tool, và cuối cùng thành công về lâu dài.

---

**Hack — dùng giá như một bộ lọc:** 1. Nếu **ideal customers** nói giá là vấn đề → thêm **lower tier** hoặc **standard plan**
2. Nếu **non-ideal people** nói quá đắt → **bỏ qua** — giá đang hoạt động đúng như một bộ lọc **Example —** Bạn muốn cộng đồng dành cho **business owners $1M+**. Nếu người chưa đạt $1M nói "đắt quá" → *bingo*, đó là dấu hiệu pricing đang filter đúng đối tượng.

## 9. Overwhelm: Làm Ít Để Giữ Nhiều Hơn

Which leads to number seven, **overwhelm**. So let's talk about this. The **number one reason people churn** outside of price is **overwhelm**.

It is the **churn driver**. And this is why **doing less can often retain more**.

The core idea behind this: *"I don't have the time. I can't keep up. I can't utilize it."* — usually it just means there's **too much stuff**.

**Case study: Planet Fitness —** Planet Fitness is a $10 a month gym subscription. They have some upsells, whatever. They were able to enter the market in a place where there's already tons of gyms, health clubs, etc.

When they surveyed customers, people were paying $59 a month — basketball courts, racket courts, cardio, pool, and all this other stuff. But **the vast majority of people just used the cardio equipment and a little bit of weights** — only 10–15% of the facility.

> "Wait, so these have these massively built out things, but people are only using 10 or 15% of the facility."

**Solution:** > "What if we just took out that 10 or 15%, put it over here, and then charge way less for it?"

By doing that, they had a **superior value proposition**.

Now, here's what gets really interesting.

### Nghịch Lý Giá Trị Cảm Nhận

When you have **five things** that you pay for, but you only use **one**, you feel like you're only getting **20% of the value**. If for the same price you just offer that one thing, people feel like they're getting **100% of the value**.

So someone is more likely to **churn** in this scenario because you added more stuff — because now they feel like they're not using enough of it.

Wild. So us as business owners, a lot of times we're thinking, *"Oh, I'm going to add all this extra stuff."* But the reality is all we did is we gave them more reason to cancel because they never used it.

**Case study —** A community on School found out about this. They deleted content they had and took their churn from **30% month-over-month to 5%** — a **6x increase in LTV** by simply having **fewer things**.

**Hack —** Make a little form and simply ask your customers: 1. *"If I were to delete everything, what one thing would you fight for?"*
2. *"If I were to keep everything and just remove only one feature, which one thing would you not mind seeing go?"* Chart those on a graph → you'll find the **#1 and #2 most valuable things**. Often times it's really outsized. Most of the time, just do those things and forget about everything else.

**Key insight:** People using your services are **not full-time** using your stuff. They have lives.

- Think **value per second**, not **seconds of value**
- In the digital world: one post, one call recording, or one single action

If someone just gets that **one valuable thing** every week from you, they're in.

## 10. Design For The Extremes: Bận Rộn Và Chuyên Sâu

Which leads to number eight. Design for the extremes.

Have two paths:
- **Busy path** — the dabbler dipping their toe in the water
- **Power user depth** — the hardcore person who can go deep all day **Busy member:** should be able to get value from a distance. They should be able to keep up and not feel like they're left behind.

**Hardcore person:** should be able to go deep all day.

**Tactically:**
- Make an explicit *"start here"* weekly cadence flow for the **busy people**
- Leave optional side quests and depth for the **heavy users**

## 11. Move The Ladder Closer: Frontload Điều Người Dùng Yêu Thích

**Core idea:** Move the ladder closer to the front — **frontload the thing that people love**.

Dùng **snakes and ladders** như một framework: - **Snakes** = friction, confusion, overwhelm → cần loại bỏ
- **Ladders** = thứ khiến người dùng stick → cần đưa vào **onboarding** **Tactic:** Nếu có một thứ sticky nhất — calls, một service cụ thể, implementation, activation, best call replay, pin post — hãy đặt nó **lên đầu**.

## 13. Show Up Hàng Ngày và Quan Tâm Thực Sự

**10. Show up daily and care.** So, this is something that we saw across all communities: **owners who showed up in the community on a daily basis** had significantly higher **retention** than those who didn't. Big surprise.

Now, we've basically never seen this fail.

**Key insight:** If a member feels like the owner isn't active or doesn't care, they leave.

**Tactile minimum:** Put an alarm, make it part of your daily routine. On a small level: - Clear notifications and DMs
- Report or deal with content that people might have flagged **Application:** This is talking about School, but you can apply this to your own business — what am I doing every day to **show up for my customers**, and can I make that a **repeated, high-value, high-leverage action**?

**On complaints:** Read the complaints every morning of customers who are dealing with issues. Because if you want to hide your head in the sand — your head will be in the sand, it'll get in your ears and your nose. That's a tough life.

Encourage the good behavior — recognize the people who follow what you want: - People leave nice reviews, people leave nice testimonials → tell them *good job* On the other hand: - **Delete bad stuff**
- **Delete bad people** **Key insight:** I cannot tell you how powerful **deleting cancers** is. Like if they do it in your body, you should do it in your community or membership.

## 14. Weekly Call và Recording Giữ Nhịp Cộng Đồng

**Which leads me to number 11:** **weekly call** và **recording**.

- **Live voice/face** deepens relationships
- **Recordings** let busy people keep up

Các **weekly podcasts** thường có **retention** rất cao — *just enough* để người dùng theo kịp. Hãy nghĩ đến TV shows: Netflix tạo ra binge culture, nhưng phần lớn các show vẫn ra **một tập mỗi tuần**.

Quá nhiều **add-ons** sẽ khiến mọi người quay về *one thing* duy nhất — vì vậy hãy đảm bảo *one thing* đó là thứ tốt nhất họ tiêu thụ.

**Default:** Nếu không chắc về **communication cadence**, hãy chọn **one big thing per week** + **best of post**.

**Example —** Cấu trúc đơn giản nhất cho cộng đồng: - **Weekly call**
- **Weekly post** Chỉ vậy thôi. Rất đơn giản trong **community structure**.

## 15. Gói Annual Giảm Churn Hiệu Quả

**Core insight:** **Annual plan** luôn có **churn thấp hơn monthly** — vì khách chỉ cần quyết định *một lần* thay vì 12 lần.

**Tại sao annual buyer churn ít hơn:** - **Không thể cancel** — đã trả tiền rồi
- **Cam kết cao hơn** — những người này thường có ý định nghiêm túc hơn
- **Nhận được giá thấp hơn** — có động lực ở lại
- **Qualified hơn** — trả nhiều tiền upfront = filter tự nhiên **Discount chuẩn:** 16–17%, tương đương công thức *buy 10 get 2*.

**Action:** Nếu bạn chưa có **annual component** trong dịch vụ hiện tại, hãy implement ngay hôm nay — announce nó và kỳ vọng một phần đáng kể khách sẽ chuyển sang. Kết quả: **retention ổn định hơn đáng kể**.

## 16. Xây Dựng Belonging Qua Kết Nối 1-1

**Goal:** Build **belonging** via **1-on-1 relationships** and **10 true regulars**. This applies to any business with a real, in-person community element.

**Core mechanic — fostering 1-on-1 relationships:** Think about the unique aspects of people coming in and pair them with others who are similar.

**Key insight:** Leila shared this data point from the employee side — but it transfers directly: > "If an employee has a friend they are close with outside of work, they stay five times longer at their job." — Leila Just *one* real friend makes that difference. One person they're genuinely close with.

**Hack:** Get them to make **one real friend**. Easier said than done — but the good news is everyone already has at least one thing in common: they're all patrons of your business.

**Application:** Introduce **true regulars** to new members. Otherwise, everyone just feels brand new — stuck in an *in-group / out-group* dynamic.

**Example —** Think of it like hosting a party. When someone new walks in: 1. Proactively introduce them — *"John, meet Tina. Meet Jesse. Meet Trish."*
2. Give them a hook — *"John's really good at X, Y, Z. Trish is great at..."*
3. Help them find **overlap** — that's all rapport-building is.

---

### 10 Regulars: Nền Tảng Cộng Đồng Digital

Within a digital community, you can **anchor engagement** with just **10 regulars**. That's it.

**Process:** 1. **Identify —** Find the top 10 people: your **model citizens**, best customers, best avatars
2. **DM them —** Learn their goals
3. **Spotlight them —** Give them **public kudos**
4. **Invite them to contribute —** Not just watch; make them active participants
5. **Publicly recognize —** Reinforces the behavior further
6. **Matchmake —** Introduce new members to the regulars

## 17. Triết Lý: Nhất Quán Từng Bước Nhỏ

Sometimes **tactics** can be very overwhelming — but sometimes they're exactly what you need.

If you have a **recurring revenue business**, don't expect to implement all the stuff at once. Just take **one thing** off the list, do it, add it in consistently, and then once you get consistent with that one, add another one off the list.

**Key insight:** Conquering **churn** — or mastering **retention** — just comes down to doing a hundred small things consistently over a very long period of time.

Most people think that growth looks like a spike, when in fact growth really just looks like a **flat retention curve** — and what happens is it just keeps growing.

Keep crushing.
