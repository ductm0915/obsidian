I use both the context-based Space dashboards and Focus Zone daily. Focus Zone helps me prioritize, the context Spaces help me concentrate. The visual environment reinforces the mental mode I want to be in (especially Deep Work mode).

My typical flow:

I start the day in Focus Zone. I take a quick glance at my top priorities and scan through my C2 action view to get a sense of what's in my various action queues. This gives me the big picture of what needs attention across all areas of my life.

Then I "go to work" — literally, I switch to my Work Space, which I treat as my virtual office. This is where I spend the bulk of my deep work time, executing work actions in the context of other work-related knowledge items. What makes this powerful is that I'm surrounded _only_ by work content — my projects, my reference materials, my work-specific actions. This keeps me in Deep Work mode without the mental noise of seeing personal actions or other commitments in my peripheral vision.

When I take a break, I intentionally switch to Personal Space or Focus Zone to see if there's anything quick I can knock off or errands I need to run. This context switch tells me I'm no longer in "work brain."

Throughout the day, I periodically return to Focus Zone to check in, reorient myself, see what's shifted in priority, and make quick adjustments. I need to make sure I'm still heading in the right direction.

After the break, I dive back into Work Space for my afternoon deep work block. When I'm done with work for the day, I hang out in Personal or Connection Space. This creates a clear psychological boundary: work stays in the Work Space. The compartmentalization helps me balance.

Then, before I fully sign off, I circle back to Focus Zone to recap the day, plan tomorrow, and close out any loose ends. Same place I started.
