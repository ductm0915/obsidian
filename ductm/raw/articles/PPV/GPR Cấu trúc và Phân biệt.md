## Projects versus Routines

So next we'll talk about projects versus routines. The difference between projects and routines are much simpler compared to a distinction between goals and projects because these two types of GPR entries behave very differently in your system.

A project is a timebound package of work. Like we mentioned earlier, it needs a clear end date. It needs a plan, scope, rough timeline, and it's a bundle of coordinated actions that together produce a result.

A routine is different. A routine is a repeating action. It doesn't need to have a fixed end date in GPR. It is something you do again and again to compound progress and maintain gains. And each instance is small, but over time it creates momentum and stability.

### Ba loại Routines trong GPR

In GPR, we work with three types of routines.

The first type is goal-driven routines. Now, these routines belong to a specific goal and conclude when that goal is achieved. They're concrete recurring actions toward a measurable outcome. They live in GPR under a specific goal and you execute them through actions. When the goal is achieved, these routines can end or be redesigned.

The second type of routine is ongoing routines not tied to goals. These are routines you want to keep doing, but they're not attached to any particular goal. They're managed directly in the actions database using the routine action type. You organize them with type and property, those two properties in the actions database. So they do not blur with the goal-driven routines. And here you might also use recurrent templates in notion to generate them on a schedule.

Then the third type is foundational practices to maintain indefinitely. These are the habits and practices that you want as part of your life for the long term. Things like stretching or meditation. These will be foundational practices to maintain indefinitely. So for these you might use the daily template or a specialized tracking database. The focus is on maintenance and consistency not on reaching a specific end state.

So in this session in the context of GPR we're only going to be talk about goal driven routines and for the others and for the rest of the implementation we'll talk more about action implementation in the focus of training.

### Protocols của Goal-Driven Routines

And lastly the protocols of goal-driven routines. When you're designing goal-driven routines in GPR database follow a few simple rules.

Number one, leave the date empty. Routines do not need a fixed end date in GPR. You control them mainly through status and actions.

Number two, phrase the routine as an action. So, you're going to write the title as something you actually do. For example, read 30 minutes and optionally add a frequency in the title as well. That's optional.

And number three, design in GPR do in actions. You design and store the goal-driven routines in the GPR database linked to a specific goal and you actually execute the repetitions in the actions database. So we'll talk about implementation in focus on training.

## Organizational Protocols

Next we'll talk about organizational protocols. Since we now have the concept in place, I want to give you a few simple rules for how to organize GPR so the system stays clean and predictable.

### Kết nối từ Life Aspirations

First, the connection from life aspirations. In PPV, as August mentioned earlier, life aspirations connect only to goals, not to projects and routines. Pillars and purpose guide what goals you choose, and those goals then guide projects and routines. We do not link aspirations directly to projects or routines because that would blur the hierarchy and make it harder to see which goals are actively carrying your long-term direction.

### Dates

Second, dates. Goals and projects must have dates. They're finite. You're deciding when you want the outcome or phase to land. Routines in GPR do not need dates. They're recurring behaviors. You manage them by status and by how they show up in your actions.

### Creation Flow

Number three, this is the creation flow. So, we recommend the flow to be you start in life aspirations and decide which goal matters now and then you create those goals. Under those goals, you add the projects and routines that will move you forward. You can create them directly in the GPR database from the goal page which August will demonstrate next. This top-down flow keeps everything aligned aspirations, goals, and then to projects and routines.

### Visual Cues

And the fourth rule is visual cues. In the templates, you will see color-coded icons that are specifically chosen for goals, project routines. We have the target and the blue arrow and the brown arrow for routine. So use these consistently. They become quick visual anchors. So you can scan a view and instantly see which items are goals, which are projects, and which are routines without reading every title type.
