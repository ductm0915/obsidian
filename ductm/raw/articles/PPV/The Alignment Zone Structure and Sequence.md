

## Overview

Before we dive into the top, let's get a sense of the sequence and structure of the alignment zone page. So, this is essentially a pyramid. We'll jump into alignment zone from the homepage.

---

## The Pyramid Structure

At the top of the pyramid is the narrowest, most focused, highest level, most conceptual elements of our lives. These are our purpose, our meaning, our values.

And then as we go down, it becomes more specific, but still quite narrow and still quite conceptual. These are our life aspirations. The more specific things we want to achieve or outcomes we want in our life and these are things that are very dear to our hearts but they're a long time. These are things that will happen over the course of a lifetime.

Then we break those aspirations down into goals. Goals are more structured, more specific, more measurable steps on the way to achieving the life aspirations.

Within the goals, we will have projects and or routines. So a goal is advanced by either a project or a routine or a combination of both. And so those are even more specific, more measurable, more tangible, more actionable, more time bound in a nearer time frame.

And within the projects and within the routines, there will be actions. Actions will be the most specific, most tangible, most measurable. These are the literal things that appear on your daily task list, which we call action list, will be the actions day by day.

---

## The Power of Connection

But because they're connected through this hierarchy from the broadest, most numerous, most tactical at the bottom up through various layers to the most narrow, fewer but most conceptual and aspirational. You have a hierarchy that connects everything and it remains connected as you move through any level.

As you're planning your goals, it's always connected. As you're planning your projects and routines, they're always connected. As you're doing your actions, they're connected. As you're doing your annual review and your highest level aspirations, it's always connected.

That is the beauty and power of the alignment zone and that's why it's the backbone of the system because it connects across it and it also pulls in information and resources we're collecting in our knowledge zone. So it's a beautiful central hub that has this directional organization.
