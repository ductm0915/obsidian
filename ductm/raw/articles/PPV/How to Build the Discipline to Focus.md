## The Only Time Management Plan That Works

One of the best insights I've ever come across on focus was presented in an article I read about 10 years ago by Oliver Berkman, a journalist for The Guardian, and I can't find this article online anymore, I just have my notes on it. I entered these notes as the top pinned post in my Evernote long before I had Notion under the heading, HOW TO GET SHIT DONE all caps, and I reread my condensed notes on it almost weekly. For years, it made a transformative impact on my life.

And here it is the only time management plan that works:

1. Decide what matters most.
2. Decide when you'll do what matters most.
3. Squeeze all the other stuff around it in the remaining time.

That's it.

---

## The Problem

There will always be too much to do. Most of the time, we do the pressing urgent things, hoping there will be time afterward for our big life advancing projects.

These little urgent seeming things are usually other people's agendas, making claims on our time, or tiny busy work items that just nag at us. They often have a deadline. And that's what gives them urgency. But it's a false urgency. And in the greater scheme of life, they just don't matter.

In most cases, a few of them do—get your taxes done—but most of them don't matter. They will be forgotten and make no impact in your life. They provide no propulsion.

Yet, we typically plow through these little pressing items. And then if we have time left over, squeeze in some of our bigger life advancing projects. So you don't advance in life, you just hold your place, year after year, treading water, not gaining much ground. If any.

---

## Flip It Around

This is not how super achievers do it, they do the opposite. They first do their big ambitious projects, then the leftover time is for everything else.

You first define a zone of time each day or several days a week for your personal top priorities. Projects that will change your life and move you toward your dreams. You give these your best time slots where you have the most energy, then, no matter what you must defend the zone, defend the zone. This is where the titling of action zone comes from in the pillars pipelines and vaults life operating system.

You don't find time for priority work, you make time for it, and squeeze the rest in or let it slip and deal with the consequences for everything else. If all the little pressing things slide then so be it. You've been letting your dreams and aspirations slide for years. Now flip it around, stop letting dreams and aspirations slide. Let the busy work and endless little outside claims on your time slide.

How many of those things will you even remember in a week? Say no more often, reschedule as needed, do the central work of your life first, then squeeze in the rest or not.

---

## Plan Ahead

Now that we've changed the whole protocol to how we approach our day, plan and schedule the priority work ahead of time.

If you wait until 9am Wednesday, to decide what you'll do at 9am Wednesday, you will do what is the most immediately gratifying. This is no good. Plan the next day, the evening before. I map out the key projects for the week, at the beginning of the week.

Then when the scheduled time arrives, do the work. You don't have to feel like it to sit down and do it. No need to change your inner mindset. Just do the work in whatever mindset you're in. No inspiration is required.

There's a great quote by artist Chuck Close: "Inspiration is for amateurs, professionals show up and get to work." Be a professional. Amateurs and wannabes sit around waiting for inspiration to strike.

There are mind framing approaches to help with this and we'll explore them in future videos. But start by recognizing that the people who achieve their dreams show up and do the work regardless of their mood. Be one of those people. It is entirely within your power and it is your choice to make.

---

## Steps to Make It Happen

So here are the steps to make shit happen:

1. Decide what matters most.
2. Decide when you'll do what matters most. And protect the zone.
3. Squeeze all other stuff around it in the remaining time.
4. Schedule the time for what matters most ahead of time.
