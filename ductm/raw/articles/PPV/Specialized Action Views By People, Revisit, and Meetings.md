## Revisit View

So next we'll talk about revisit. These are actions to come back to. The revisit view is filtered to action with status being revisit and these are not abandoned items. Just want to clarify that these are actions you want to step away from for now but come back to at the right time or with the right trigger.

### Use Cases for Revisit

So revisit is especially useful for waiting on which is actions where you're waiting on someone or something and there isn't a clear next date yet. So for waiting on type of revisit actions you don't even need a due date if you don't have a clear due date next. So the action stays out of your main list. So it doesn't create noise but you can still see it in revisit and decide when to move it forward. You don't want them in your main execution view but you don't want to lose track of them.

So for instance, this is an action you might be waiting on and you don't know how long it's going to take. So you don't need a due date here, but you do want to change the status to revisit and this will always be here in your revisit view to check back on later.

And it could also be used for done for now and check back or review. So actions where the current round of work is done, but you want to come back later to review results or confirm something that is still working or decide whether or not to take a next step. So you can apply a due date or not. Again, it depends on if there's a specific date that you want to set to return here and to take a look at the action.

It could also be used for a very important function is snooze and resurface on a set date. So actions you intentionally push into the future. You set a new due date and then they quietly sit in revisit view until that due date. Then they will come back to your main action view.

### How Revisit and Due Date Work Together

So how do revisit and due date work together? Revisit tab. This tab we're in. This is a catchall view for all of the actions regardless of due date. Any action with a status being revisit will always appear in this view. This is the home base for every action you've marked revisit.

Due date makes a big difference in that it controls whether or not a revisit action shows up in the main action list. If a revisit action does not have a due date, it will not appear in your main action list. It only lives in the revisit view. so it doesn't clutter your day-to-day execution.

If a revisit action has new due date set in the future, it will appear in the main action list on that specific day. It's quite like a snooze feature in a lot of software.

So, you have flexible control over where and when you see revisit items. You can keep them out of sight when they're simply on hold and bring them back automatically on a chosen date so they show up in your main action list again before a meeting, before a client call appointment to review questions you have or any other moments when the action becomes relevant again.

For example, if you have a series of meetings with someone, you can put previous meetings on revisit and then the new due date you can set to the day before your next meeting. So you can review what you discuss with this person and your notes in advance to help you prepare for the next meeting.
