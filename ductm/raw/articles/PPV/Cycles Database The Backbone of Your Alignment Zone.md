## Understanding the Cycles Database Structure

So now let's look at the cycles database itself.

Cycles is a core alignment zone database that holds all of your time-based review units in a single place. We have the daily cycles, weekly cycles and the dual cycles which are two month reviews. Each row in this database is a cycle page. Each page has a reflection content in the body and a set of properties that track metrics and summaries and connections to your goals, actions, and resources.

So, structurally, there's a hierarchy within the same database. Daily pages are nested within the weekly page they belong to, and weekly pages are nested under the dual cycle page they belong to. So this gives you three levels that you can zoom in and out a single day or when you want to look at the week or when you look at the two-month period. So these are the layers that with this one database you can achieve and for organizing all the smaller units of cycles.

## How the Three Cycles Work Together

So how these three cycles work together.

**The daily cycle** drives your micro reflection. So they capture your effectiveness rating improvements and any simple optional metrics you're tracking.

**The weekly cycles** pull together daily patterns, actions, and outcome that help you summarize the last week and turn all of that into meaning, decisions, and next steps.

**Dual cycle**, these integrate eight to nine weeks of weekly insights. This is where you zoom out, recognize bigger patterns, reset your GPR focus, make structural changes, and figure out where you should shift your focus over the next two months.

## Best Practices for Cycle Reviews

So before we go into each cycle workflow, I want to lay out the best practices that make cycle reviews work well over time.

### 1. Create Cycles Progressively

So the first best practice is create cycles progressively as you need them and don't precreate the cycles. What that means is you'll create a daily where when you need to use it and you'll create a weekly when you sit down to do a weekly and then you'll create a dual cycle when you do a two-month review. Do not pre-create the weeklies or dual cycles ahead of time. You want to keep your cycles filled with live meaningful entries rather than empty shelves that create clutter and sometimes even guilt because you created the page but you didn't do it.

### 2. Restart From Where You Are

And that leads us to the second best practice and principle is restart from where you are and accept your missed cycles. It's completely normal that you skipped or missed some cycles. Just re-engage from the current cycle forward. Do not try to catch up and backfill the missed dailies or the missed weeklies. We want to build long-term consistency and what we're not trying to achieve is perfection.

### 3. Follow the Three Tier Structure

So the third principle and best practice is follow the three tier structure for organization. You'll use the current year view to organize your cycles. You'll nest daily pages under its correct weekly and nest weekly pages under its correct dual cycles.

So I'm going to demonstrate how to organize the entries. So, we have the dailies from this week. After you create a weekly, you will click on this little toggle, expand it, and then you'll select the five dailies. Drag them under the weekly. Do not remove sorting. And voila, we have a weekly organization of all your dailies. And then when it's time to create a dual cycle, after you create a dual cycle page, it's time to organize all the weeklies under this two-month period. Then same idea we will click on this toggle next to the dual cycle. We will select the weeklies and we'll nest them. Don't remove sorting. And now when you come to this view you have a very clean organization of the three tier structure.

### 4. Review Both Progress and Resources

So going back to our best practices the next one is you want to review both progress and resources in your weekly and dual cycle review. When you review, you're not just asking what did I do this week or the last two months, but also what did I learn and what is worth carrying forward. So go through the outcomes and the insights. And the good news is the most valuable information is always already teed up for you through the notable highlights and the high value rating. We've talked about this in focus zone and knowledge zone. So we will not repeat that here.

### 5. Focus on Lessons Learned

And then finally, we want to focus on lessons learned, not just data collected. Metrics are there to prompt reflection. You know, the data that you're tracking or the checkboxes, but they're not the end goal. As you move on in life, what you take with you aren't numbers, but the lessons and insights you've gathered from the reviews. So, recognize the real long-term value in the thoughtful written reflections. The more you go inside the pages to do meaningful reflections and write down your thoughts and fill in the white matters and improvement notes, the more useful your cycles become over time.
