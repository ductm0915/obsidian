## The Resurfacing and Deletion Mechanism

So, when that day comes, there's one of two things to do. One option is to do it. Of course, the evening before we'll have assessed this is when we're going to make the judgment. The evening before, are we going to do this the next day? Does it fit into today? Is it viable? And is it important enough to do now over everything else? If it makes the cut, then we do it that day.

If not, its resurfacing is still valuable because it tells you it's there. It needs to be done. and needs a home for a time slot to do and you will then schedule it into a future date. You can always bump things forward, but there's a built-in intentional friction here. If you keep bumping something forward over and over again, you're going to see it resurface and then reschedule and it'll resurface when that day comes and you'll reschedule it over time. You're going to notice I'm constantly rescheduling this one.

And this is friction designed to make you make a judgment. If you never feel it's important enough to do in any given day, should this still be on your action list? You need to start considering deleting it. Or if it is important enough, you need to get it done. So, it's going to put either pressure on you to get it done or pressure on you to make the call, not worth my time. I'm going to delete it.

So, that resurfacing is part of the design and part of when you get to it the night before, the question is really, is this worth the next day? Is this important enough? Is the time slot fit naturally or is this something I can naturally do later? So that is a judgment that is going to be fed to you over and over as you push things forward and eventually by default a lot of these things will just not be that important after you bump them a few times and it's going to trigger that deletion rather than GTD and the traditional systems.
