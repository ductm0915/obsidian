## The Top Control Strip

So moving down to the top control strip. The top control strip of focus zone has three panels. Quick ad, your priority actions list and your action analytics. You can capture, choose, and review in a very lightweight.

### Quick Ads Panel

So first the quick ads. This left column is for instant capture. You don't go hunting for any databases. You click a button and the system does the tagging for you. Anything in your head should be just two clicks away from becoming an action, a daily entry or a neurobbit.

So the action by category button, these buttons create actions already tagged as personal, work or connection category and use it when you know what slice of life an action belongs to.

The action by type buttons create actions pre-typed as meeting, routine, errand, content, etc. Use it when you know what kind of action this is and you want consistent structure filtering by type.

The daily area. This area lets you click the new page button to create today's daily page in the cycles database with the correct type and the title autofilled.

And lastly, we have the new bits buttons. These buttons capture ideas and insights without leaving focus zone. The point of this section is that you capture first and organize lightly in the meantime. The buttons does the light categorizing and classifying with with type for you.

### Top Priorities Action List

Then we have the top priorities action list. This middle panel here is a filter view of the actions database that pulls together only the most important actions on or for today. This is not where you do heavy planning. This is a list that's like a mini billboard of your most important actions. So every time you open focus zone, you're reminded what matters the most. August will talk more about this in a little bit.

### Analytics Panel

And then we have the analytics. These are optional analytics views. And on the right we have category analytics. This is actions grouped by category to visualize the load between personal work and connection.
