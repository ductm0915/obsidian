## Day 3 at a glance

**Theme:** Three Levels of Wisdom (Văn–Tư–Tu Tuệ / Suta–Cinta–Bhāvanā-maya-paññā)

**Context:** Evening discourse before Day 4 — the day Vipassana practice officially begins.

**Why it matters:** Day 3 is the **philosophical keystone** of the entire course. Goenka explains why Vipassana is not religion, devotion, or intellectual exercise — it is direct experiential wisdom.

**Key topics covered:**
1. Three Levels of Wisdom — Suta (heard), Cinta (reasoned), Bhāvanā (experienced)
2. Why only experiential wisdom liberates — the restaurant parable, the medicine prescription parable
3. Dangers of blind faith (Suta) and intellectual pride (Cinta)
4. [[anicca-impermanence]] at the experiential level — subatomic particles arising and passing
5. Four parts of mind introduced: Viññāna, Saññā, [[vedana|Vedanā]], Saṅkhāra
6. Transition announcement: Vipassana proper begins tomorrow (Day 4)

**Core chain:** Sīla → Samādhi → Paññā → Liberation
