## 7. The 100 Units of Time Exercise

I encourage you to be more mindful of where your **time** goes. If you are not feeling very productive, and if you're kind of getting frustrated and annoyed because you can't deliver on the projects that you want to deliver — one exercise I highly recommend is just called the **100 Units of Time**.

All you do is for **one week**, just track where your time goes: 1. Every single day, chunk it out into **thirty-minute** blocks 2. Every time you do something, just **write it down** 3. Track from when you wake up in the morning to when you go to bed at night — **every day for seven days straight** 4. At the end of the week, look back over that 5. Look at the different **buckets** that it goes into — create some buckets or categories 6. Start to work out as a **percentage** how much time you spend doing all of these different things ---

## 8. Eliminate, Don't Optimize

Once you're aware of where your time is going, the real trick is **not** to just try and become more efficient or more productive. The real trick is not to get more apps on your phone, and it's not to get some to-do list software or anything like that — that's the **stupid way** to do it.

**Key insight:** You don't try to **do more** — you try to **do less**.

The real masterful way of dealing with time — once you've been made aware of it and where your time goes — is to just start **eliminating the wasteful practices**. Just get rid of it.

There is nothing better than just **eliminating** something. Creating a system or a process, hiring someone, delegating it — these are all things that **aren't true fixes**. They're kind of just patches, putting a bandaid on something. But **eliminating** something — this is gone.

That's why I encourage you to do the **100 Units of Time** exercise all the time. Once a month, do this exercise — just one week out of the month, every single month: - See where your time's going - Identify the wasteful practices - **Eliminate them** ---
