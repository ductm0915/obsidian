# Continuity of Practice

**Continuity of practice** (satatam — Pāli: continuous, unbroken) is the principle that Vipassana's benefits are proportional to the consistency and continuity of daily practice, not to episodic intensity.

## Goenka's Teaching (Day 7)

> "Continuity of practice is the secret. It is not the long retreat that matters — it is the two hours every morning and evening, without break, day after day."

The pañca bala (five strengths) develop through continuity. Each day of practice reinforces the sankara-reduction process; each day skipped allows old patterns to reassert.

## Daily Practice After the Retreat

Goenka prescribes:
- **2 sessions per day** — 1 hour morning + 1 hour evening
- **No skipping** — even 10 minutes is better than nothing
- **Annual retreat** — 10-day course once per year to reset and deepen
