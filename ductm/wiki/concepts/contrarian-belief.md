# Contrarian Belief

A **Contrarian Belief** is a genuine conviction that goes against the dominant or mainstream belief in a specific industry or niche. In [[brand-building]], it is considered the "80% lever" for standing out from the competition.

## Contrarian vs. Controversial
[[caleb-ralston]] distinguishes between these two:
- **Contrarian**: Standing against the dominant belief because you genuinely believe it to be true at your core. It attracts respect and creates high-trust alignment.
- **Controversial**: Taking a position specifically to trigger a reaction or "clout chase." This often attracts "noise" and can make a brand appear unstable or untrustworthy to established players.

## The Two-Column Approach
To find a contrarian belief, use this exercise:
1. **Left Column**: List things people in your space say or do that you disagree with (industry tropes).
2. **Right Column**: Your unique view or what you would do differently.

## Test and Double Down
Caleb recommends starting with multiple contrarian takes, testing them in content, and then doubling/tripling down on whichever resonates most. He started with 3: (1) trust > virality, (2) sustainable system > omnipresence, (3) psychological safety > fear. Trust optimization caught fire, so he doubled down.

## Examples
- **Patagonia** (Yvon Chouinard): Prioritizing environmental stewardship over maximizing profit.
- **Netflix** (Reed Hastings): The belief that people don't want to leave their house to rent movies (vs. the Blockbuster model).
- **Gary Vaynerchuk**: Social media > legacy media advertising, at a time when Fortune 500s spent on TV/radio/print.
- **Cody Sanchez**: Boring businesses (laundromats, plumbing) > tech startups, in peak "Uber of X" culture.
- **Caleb Ralston**: Optimizing for trust, not virality.
