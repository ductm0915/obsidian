# 16 Tánh Người (16 Human Natures/Dispositions)

**Origin:** [[wit-organization]] — [[hong-trieu-bao]]
**Primary source:** [[wit-k07-nhan-thuc-con-nguoi]]

## Concept Summary

16 Tánh Người is WIT's taxonomy of fundamental human character dispositions — the deep-seated drives and tendencies that generate behavior patterns across all people. "Tánh" in Vietnamese means innate nature or disposition; it refers to qualities that arise spontaneously from within, not learned behaviors.

The framework is positioned as one of the three foundational elements of understanding a person:
1. **Tâm** (Chân thật/Chân Tâm) — the authentic core; what is unchanging
2. **Tánh** (16 Tánh Người) — character dispositions; what drives behavior
3. **Tình** (Cảm xúc / 8 muôn 4 ngàn bong bóng ảo giác) — emotions; the 84,000 emotional states

Knowing the 16 tánh "explains why we have the lives we have today" — they are causally connected to life outcomes through the karma/reincarnation framework (see [[quy-luat-luan-hoi]]).

## The Nature of Tánh

Tánh are not flaws to be eliminated — they are structural features of human consciousness that can be directed toward positive or negative expression. The WIT teaching does not aim to suppress human nature but to develop awareness (nhận thức) of which tánh is active in a given moment, so one can consciously redirect it.

A key phrase in Session 01: "Tánh của con người được sinh hóa từ đâu? Và tại sao chúng ta lại có cuộc sống như ngày hôm nay?" — Understanding the origins of one's tánh provides the explanation for one's present circumstances.

## Known/Referenced Tánh

The complete enumeration of all 16 appears to be taught in sessions not reviewed in the current batch (the core modules referenced in Sessions 01 and 20 describe the framework but refer to the specific list as having been or to be covered in dedicated sessions). Based on evidence across reviewed sources:

**Six Base Drives (Lục Dục — Six Desires)** — referenced in [[wit-k07-pham-chat]]:
- **Tánh Tài** — the drive for wealth/material resources
- **Tánh Sắc** — the drive for beauty/aesthetic experience
- **Tánh Thực** — the drive for sensory pleasure (food, comfort)
- **Tánh Danh** — the drive for reputation, recognition, status
- **Tánh Thùy** — the drive for rest/sleep/ease
- *(One additional base drive to complete the six)*

**From classical Buddhist psychology**, which WIT draws on, the full set typically includes drives arising from tham (craving), sân (aversion), si (ignorance), mạn (pride), nghi (doubt), tà kiến (wrong view), and their various compounds — potentially mapping to a system of 16 when subdivided.

The **Phẩm Chất** curriculum ([[wit-k07-pham-chat]]) makes clear that each of the five core virtues (Nhân — Lễ — Nghĩa — Trí — Tín) addresses a corresponding cluster of tánh. For example, Nghĩa (Duty/Responsibility) is explicitly linked to **tánh Danh** (the drive for recognition).

## Application in Understanding People

When observing a person's behavior, WIT teaches that the behavior always traces back to an active tánh:
- Someone who works 80-hour weeks to accumulate wealth → tánh Tài active
- Someone who cannot stop seeking approval → tánh Danh active
- Someone who avoids difficulty and chooses comfort → tánh Thùy dominant

This is not judgment — it is diagnosis. Knowing which tánh drives a person's behavior allows for:
1. Compassion (understanding why they act as they do)
2. Skillful relating (addressing their active drive appropriately)
3. Predicting patterns (knowing what will activate cooperation or resistance)

## The Relationship Between Tánh and Life Outcomes

The [[quy-luat-luan-hoi]] framework creates a causal chain: tánh → tham tưởng (intentions/desires) → hành động (action) → nghiệp (karma/outcome). When a person's tánh drives unchecked, they create patterns of karma that reproduce the same life circumstances across relationships, work, and health.

WIT's intervention: by developing nhận thức (awareness) of which tánh is active, and by cultivating phẩm chất (virtue) as a counter-force, one can gradually redirect the energy of each tánh toward positive creation rather than reactive cycle.

## 84,000 Ảo Giác Emotional States

Distinct from the 16 tánh (structural drives) are the "8 muôn 4 ngàn bong bóng ảo giác" (84,000 emotional illusion-bubbles) — the enormous variety of emotional experiences that arise from the interplay of tánh with circumstances. This number is drawn from Buddhist cosmology (Buddha is said to have taught 84,000 dharma gates to address the same number of human afflictions). WIT uses this figure to convey the incomprehensible variety of human emotional experience, all of which traces back to a much smaller set of root drives (the 16 tánh).

## Relationship to Other WIT Concepts

- **[[quy-luat-luan-hoi]]**: tánh generate karma through intentions and actions
- **[[wit-k07-pham-chat]]**: virtues (Nhân-Lễ-Nghĩa-Trí-Tín) are cultivated in relation to specific tánh
- **[[9-dang-nguoi]]**: each of the 9 relationship types may be understood partly through which tánh predominates in the other person — recognizing tánh patterns helps identify which type someone is being
