# Barbell Strategy — AI Era

## The Framework

[[alex-hormozi]] applies a **barbell portfolio approach** to positioning for the AI transition: allocate to two extremes simultaneously, avoid the fragile middle.

---

## Left Bar: All-In AI Native (High Risk / High Reward)

- Build all new businesses AI-first, AI-native, AI-forward from day one.
- Make hard conversations: automate roles, raise the bar, let non-adapters go.
- Revenue per employee target: millions/year/head.
- Why it's risky: requires radical organizational change; uncomfortable conversations; being wrong about the timeline.
- Why incumbents lose: a startup that never hired doesn't have to fire. Startups without org charts win by default in this scenario.

---

## Right Bar: Timeless Bets (Low Risk / Long-Term Stable)

Inspired by Jeff Bezos's frame: *"What are the few things that won't change?"*

| Category | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| Health / Fitness / Food / Supplements | Humans will still have bodies |
| Housing / Shelter | Humans will still need places to live |
| Entertainment | More automation = more leisure = more demand for entertainment |
| In-person experiences | Social needs are not automatable |

Entertainment thesis: AI massively reduces production cost for content. Prices haven't adjusted yet. One person can now produce a full-motion picture with AI → viral social → $100M+ if it's good. The margin math is transformational.

---

## Why the Middle Is Dangerous

The "sort of AI" companies — big org charts, using AI terminology, not actually automating — are the most vulnerable:
- Too slow to compete with true AI-native startups.
- Too AI-dependent to fall back on human-intensive competitive advantages.
- Sunk cost in existing headcount creates political resistance to the necessary changes.

---

## The Hell-in-a-Handbasket Frame

Hormozi's self-check: "If everything truly collapses into permanent underclass / winner-take-all / anarchy — *none of my planning would matter anyway.*" So the apocalyptic downside neutralizes itself. Plan for sunshine and rain; be an all-weather type of person.
