# Long-Term Thinking

**Origin:** [[sam-ovens]]
**Source:** [[sam-ovens-long-term-thinking]]

## Core Principle

The meta-principle behind all sustainable success: **optimize decisions for long-term outcomes, not short-term comfort.**

> "Time is more valuable than money. Money I can get a lot of. Time I have a finite amount of." — Sam Ovens

## Why It's Hard

Humans are biologically wired for instant gratification:
- Dopamine loops reward immediate pleasure
- Social media, fast food, debt — all exploit short-term wiring
- The marshmallow experiment: children who delayed gratification outperformed peers on every life metric over decades

## The Framework

Every decision has a time dimension that most people ignore:

| Short-term thinking | Long-term thinking |
|---|---|
| "What feels good now?" | "What compounds over years?" |
| Optimizes first-order consequences | Considers 2nd, 3rd, Nth-order consequences |
| Results: debt, obesity, stagnation | Results: wealth, health, mastery |
| Default mode (biological) | Requires conscious override |

## Application

1. **Use the [[effect-horizon]]** to map decisions across time
2. **Consider [[second-order-consequences]]** before acting
3. **"Keep the main thing the main thing"** — wealth, fame, luxury are second-order consequences of doing the first-order thing well. Never chase byproducts.
4. **Remove temptation at the source** — Sam removed all TVs from his house rather than relying on willpower

## The Michael Jordan Rule

Jordan recognized that sponsorships and celebrity were second-order consequences of being the best. He canceled everything and focused on the first-order thing (basketball). His second-order things returned at a higher level.

**Rule:** Never let second-order consequences distract you from the first-order thing that generates them.
