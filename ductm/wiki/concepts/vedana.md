# Vedanā (Sensation / Feeling Tone)

**Vedanā** (Pāli) is the "feeling tone" component of every mental event — the immediate pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral quality that arises when a sense door (eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind) contacts an object.

In Vipassana, vedanā is the primary object of practice. Goenka identifies it as the "missing link" discovered by the Buddha:

**Full chain:** Sense contact → **Vedanā** → Tanha (craving/aversion) → Upadana (clinging) → Becoming → Suffering

Without vedanā, there is no craving. By observing vedanā equanimously (not reacting with craving or aversion), the practitioner breaks the chain at its source.
