# Emotional Regulation

**Emotional regulation** is the capacity to observe, modulate, and respond skillfully to emotional states — rather than being automatically reactive to them.

## Vipassana Framework

Goenka reframes emotional regulation through body sensation: emotions are not purely mental — they manifest as physical sensations (vedanā) which can be observed objectively.

The Vipassana method for emotional regulation:
1. **Notice** the emotion as a body sensation (burning in chest, tightening in throat)
2. **Observe** without reacting (neither suppressing nor acting out)
3. **Maintain equanimity** (upekkhā) — the sensation will pass

> "No one else gives you anger. You create it yourself. The moment you observe it without reacting, it loses power." — Goenka (Day 9 paraphrase)

**Anger duration metric** (Day 9): Emotional regulation progress is measured by how long reactive states last — from hours/days to minutes to seconds.

## Suppression vs. Expression vs. Observation

The Vipassana framework distinguishes between three responses to emotion:
- **Suppression** — Push down, causes underground pressure
- **Expression** — Act out, creates new harm/karma
- **Observation** — Watch the sensation arise and pass — the "middle path"

## WIT Framework

WIT connects emotional regulation to **Tâm Thức** (consciousness): emotion is a signal from the subconscious, not a command. Learning to read and translate rather than obey signals is central to WIT's inner work.
