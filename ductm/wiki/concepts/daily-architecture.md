# Daily Architecture

**Daily architecture** is the intentional design of one's daily schedule to protect and prioritize high-value activities — treating the day as a structured system rather than a reactive sequence.

## Sam Ovens Framework

Sam Ovens' daily architecture centers on:
1. **Morning block** — first 4 hours for deep work (no meetings, no email)
2. **Afternoon** — collaborative/administrative work
3. **Evening** — restoration, learning

See: [[four-hour-workday]], [[defend-the-zone]]

## Vipassana Framework

Goenka (Day 11) prescribes a specific daily architecture for practitioners:
- **Morning**: 1 hour Vipassana immediately upon waking
- **Evening**: 1 hour Vipassana before sleep
- These two anchors structure the entire day energetically

The two Vipassana sittings function as "bookends" — clearing the mind before the day and after — similar in spirit to Ovens' morning block for cognitive protection.
