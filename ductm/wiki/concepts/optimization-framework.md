# Optimization Framework

A 5-step framework (inspired by Elon Musk) for fixing something that already exists. Alex Hormozi applies this to departments, processes, and teams. The critical insight is that **deletion comes before optimization** — most processes, meetings, and features are waste.

## The Five Steps (in order)

1. **Question the Requirements:** Why are we doing everything we're doing? How does each activity relate to output? Challenge assumptions about what's necessary.
2. **Delete:** Cancel everything unnecessary — meetings, reports, processes. The instinct to "add" is strong; the discipline to "remove" is rare. You can always add it back.
3. **Optimize:** Of what remains, prioritize by impact per unit of resource. What gives the most results for the least effort?
4. **Pull Up Timelines:** Ask "how many hours will this take?" not "what day will you finish?" Compress deadlines aggressively. End-of-day instead of end-of-week = 7x speed.
5. **Automate:** Only after deleting and optimizing should you automate. Automating a bad process just makes bad things happen faster.

## Critical Order
Steps must be done in sequence. Most companies automate first (step 5) when they should delete first (step 2). The result: automated waste.

## Real-World Application
When Hormozi took over his media division:
- Eliminated ALL meetings → replaced with daily standup
- Switched to "What-Who-When" task assignment (hours, not days)
- Aligned compensation with output → team more than doubled output
