# Phẩm Chất (Character Virtues)

WIT's framework of core character qualities required for genuine human flourishing. Phẩm chất (character/virtue) is positioned at the center of WIT's comprehensive richness model (7 sự giàu toàn diện) as both cause and effect — it is the hinge between inputs (effort, knowledge) and outputs (results, relationships, prosperity).

## The Five Virtues: Nhân — Lễ — Nghĩa — Trí — Tín

Drawn from the Confucian five constants (五常, ngũ thường), applied in WIT's modern framework:

| Virtue | Chinese | Meaning | Linked tánh (drive) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nhân** | 仁 | Benevolence, Humanity | Tình (feeling/love) |
| **Lễ** | 禮 | Propriety, Ceremony, Respect | — |
| **Nghĩa** | 義 | Righteousness, Duty, Responsibility | Danh (reputation/name) |
| **Trí** | 智 | Wisdom, Intelligence, Discernment | — |
| **Tín** | 信 | Trustworthiness, Integrity | — |

WIT formula: "Nhân — Lễ — Nghĩa — Trí — Tín là năm phẩm chất ưu tú mà con người cần bồi dưỡng suốt đời." (These five are the excellent character qualities all humans must cultivate for life.)

## Virtues vs. Skills (Phẩm Chất vs. Năng Lực)

WIT draws a sharp distinction:

- **Năng lực** (Skills/Competencies): What you can do. Technical abilities, learned behaviors, professional expertise. Can be trained directly.
- **Phẩm chất** (Character Virtues): Who you are. The inner qualities that shape how skills are deployed. Cannot be trained directly — must be cultivated through sustained inner work.

The key insight: "Có tài mà không có đức thì là người vô dụng." (Talent without virtue is useless — attributed to Bác Hồ, cited in sessions.) Skills amplify character — both good and bad. High competence with poor character causes greater harm; high competence with strong character creates greater good.

## Phẩm Chất at the Center

Cô Quế Minh: "Phẩm chất nằm ở ngay trung tâm của ngôi nhà [framework] — nó vừa là nhân, nó vừa là quả." (Character virtues sit at the center — they are both cause and effect.)

In the WIT architecture:
- Virtues are the **input** into the 7 richnesses: you cannot truly become rich in Trí tuệ (wisdom-richness) or Tâm thái (mental-state-richness) without cultivating the underlying virtues
- Virtues are also the **output** of correct practice: when you live by Nhân-Lễ-Nghĩa-Trí-Tín, the richnesses flow naturally
- Virtues are the **bridge** between nhân (cause) and quả (effect) in the nhân duyên quả framework

## Each Virtue in Brief

### Nhân (仁 Benevolence)
The root of all virtues. Care for others as an expression of one's own fullness. WIT teaching: you cannot truly serve others from a place of lack — Nhân with others requires Nhân with self first.

### Lễ (禮 Propriety) — see [[le-礼-respect-ceremony]]
WIT formula: **Lẽ phải + Xin phép** (Rightness + Asking permission). The outer form of inner goodness. Practiced toward the living (người dương) and ancestral (người âm). "Người có Lễ đi tới đâu cũng được bảo vệ hết."

Key aphorism: **"Lễ nghĩa sinh phú quý."** (Propriety and righteousness generate prosperity.)

### Nghĩa (義 Duty/Responsibility) — Primary teaching in Sessions 9–10
WIT definition: Trách nhiệm (Responsibility). Linked to tánh Danh (drive for reputation/recognition).

**Four spheres** (mapping to Tu thân — Tề gia — Trị quốc — Bình thiên hạ):
1. **Bản thân**: Recognizing one's own existence has a purpose or mission; living the 7 richnesses for oneself first
2. **Gia đình**: Taking responsibility for one's family; committing to marital harmony and good parenting
3. **Tổ chức**: Serving the organization one belongs to; doing what is good for the organization without selecting only preferred tasks
4. **Xã hội**: Contributing to the broader social good; ensuring one's family and work create healthy social contribution

Critical teaching: Người trọng Danh (reputation-driven people) often demonstrate spectacular Nghĩa to organizations and society — wanting to be seen as generous — while neglecting Nghĩa to themselves. True Nghĩa must be balanced across all four spheres.

Marriage as Nghĩa test: accepting the responsibility of a family is the clearest demonstration of Nghĩa. WIT teaching holds that those unwilling to commit to this level of responsibility should not be assigned major organizational leadership.

### Trí (智 Wisdom/Discernment)
The ability to perceive clearly, reason soundly, and act wisely. At higher levels: seeing through appearances to underlying patterns, understanding cause-effect relationships at multiple orders.

### Tín (信 Trustworthiness) — Primary teaching in Session 10
WIT definition: **Giữ lời (keeping one's word) + Đúng giờ (being on time)**.

Three forms:
1. **Giữ lời hứa**: Keeping informal verbal commitments to others, even when circumstances change
2. **Giữ lời cam kết**: Keeping formal pledges (e.g., enrollment commitment to complete the Mentor program)
3. **Giữ đúng hẹn**: Being on time as a form of respect

"Lời đã nói thì nhất định phải làm." (What is spoken must be done.) Tín with self (keeping promises to yourself) is the prerequisite for Tín with others. The Confucian sequence: if one cannot keep word with self, credibility with others is hollow.

## The Cultivation Path

The 10 Phẩm Chất sessions in WIT's Mentor curriculum provide *information and concepts* about the five virtues — what they are and why they matter. "Chúng ta mới vừa kịp hiểu Nhân là gì, Lễ là gì... và nó có ích lợi gì cho cuộc đời thôi." (We have only just grasped what each virtue is and why it matters.) The actual cultivation is a lifelong project.

Cô Hoàng Anh: "Nhân — Lễ — Nghĩa — Trí — Tín là năm phẩm chất ưu tú mà con người của chúng ta cần bồi dưỡng suốt đời." She has the organizational responsibility to research and develop the cultivation pathway — the 10 sessions are the beginning.

## Connection to 7 Sự Giàu Toàn Diện

WIT's comprehensive richness model includes seven dimensions:

1. Trí tuệ (Intellectual richness)
2. Tâm thái (Mental-state richness)
3. Nhân cách (Character richness) ← Phẩm chất contributes here
4. Phẩm chất (Virtue richness) ← this concept directly
5. Năng lực (Skill richness)
6. Thể chất (Physical richness)
7. Vật chất (Material richness)

Phẩm chất occupies its own richness slot while also underpinning all others. A person who is Vật chất (materially) rich but lacks Phẩm chất is not truly rich in WIT's framework.
