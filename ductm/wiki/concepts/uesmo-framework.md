# UESMO Framework — The Perfect Business

**UESMO** is Charlie Munger's framework for identifying the ideal business, as interpreted and named by Alex Hormozi. It describes five attributes that together create a durable, high-value enterprise.

## The Five Attributes

| Letter | Attribute | What It Means |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **U** | **Unique** | Competitive moat; differentiation driven by brand or structural advantage |
| **E** | **Expensive** | High margins; the ability to charge a premium relative to cost |
| **S** | **Sticky** | Customers return; recurring or reoccurring purchase behavior |
| **M** | **Managed by** | Owner operates the business with deep involvement |
| **O** | **Owner** | Owner has all or most net worth in the business — maximum skin in the game |

The M and O combine into one principle: **owner-managed with full financial alignment.**

## Why Each Attribute Matters

**Unique (U):** Without differentiation, competition drives margins to zero. Brand is the most durable form of uniqueness — it lives in the customer's mind and cannot be easily copied. See's Candies is chocolate; Apple makes phones. Both command premiums because of what they represent, not what they physically are.

**Expensive (E):** "Buy for a penny, sell for a dollar." High margins create buffer for mistakes, fund reinvestment, and reflect genuine value capture. A business with thin margins is fragile — every operational error becomes existential.

**Sticky (S):** Recurring revenue compounds. A customer who returns creates LTV that dwarfs a one-time buyer. Stickiness reduces CAC burden — the same customer doesn't need to be re-acquired.

**Managed by Owner (MO):** Owner-operators make decisions aligned with decades-long outcomes, not quarterly reporting cycles. Munger observed that many great businesses deteriorated when professional management replaced owner management. Having all net worth in the business creates true accountability.

## Munger's Portfolio as Proof

Every major Berkshire Hathaway holding exhibits UESMO traits:
- **Apple:** Brand (U) + premium pricing (E) + ecosystem lock-in (S)
- **See's Candies:** Brand (U) + high candy margin (E) + seasonal/gifting repeat (S)
- **Geico:** Brand trust (U) + insurance margins (E) + annual renewal (S)
- **Dairy Queen:** Brand (U) + food margin (E) + franchise loyalty (S)

## Connection to Brand

The "U" (unique) in UESMO is almost always driven by brand:
> "Apple's brand allows them to charge above-average prices for average products."

Brand is the moat that *appreciates* with use — every positive customer experience strengthens it — unlike physical assets that depreciate.
