# Creative Leadership

**Creative Leadership** is [[caleb-ralston]]'s philosophy for leading media/creative teams. It stands in opposition to the common top-down, fear-based management style prevalent in the creator economy.

## 5 Core Shifts

1. **Training ≠ Control**: Enable the team to ideate and execute, not just follow directives. A manager who generates all ideas creates a single point of failure and plateau.
2. **Managing ≠ Leading**: Managing = telling exactly what to do. Leading = casting vision, setting expectations, equipping with tools, giving ownership.
3. **IC → Leader Identity Shift**: Stop deriving value from personal output. Your team's output is now your output. Ask "what did my team accomplish?" not "what did I get done?"
4. **Fear Kills Creativity**: Fear, micromanagement, and lack of ownership are "silent, invisible killers." Replace with [[we-love-a-good-flop]] culture.
5. **Creatives Care About Results**: Teams become deeply engaged when shown how their work impacts revenue. Share the business data, not just platform metrics.

## Tactical Implementation
- **Platform Ownership**: One person per platform → they own ideation + strategy + execution.
- **Check On, Not Check In**: "Any roadblocks I can remove?" vs. micromanaging lighting/framing.
- **Chunked Ownership**: Give ownership at varying levels (whole campaign → just audio monitoring). Scale up based on readiness.
- **Delegate to Breed Leaders**: Delegation → more ownership → promotion → leader freed for bigger-picture work.
