# Universal Dhamma

**Universal Dhamma** is S.N. Goenka's central teaching that Dhamma (the law of nature) is not a religion, sect, or belief system — it is a universal truth that operates the same way for all human beings regardless of background.

## Core Argument

> "Dhamma is shared by all because the disease is shared by all. When the mind becomes impure, we suffer. This suffering is not exclusive to any sect, community, country, or gender. The disease is universal. Therefore the cure must also be universal." — Goenka, Day 10

## Key Principles

1. **No sectarian ownership** — Dhamma belongs to no religion
2. **Verifiable by experience** — not belief-based but practice-based
3. **Non-discriminatory** — works equally for Hindu, Muslim, Christian, atheist
4. **Law of nature** — like gravity, works whether you believe in it or not

## Practical Implication

Goenka deliberately strips all ritual, ceremony, and sectarian language from his teaching. The 10-day course has no prayers, no worship, no religious objects. The technique is presented as a "mental science" or "art of living."

## Connection to WIT

WIT's framework resonates with Universal Dhamma in treating inner laws (karmic patterns, consciousness levels) as universal principles, not religious ones.
