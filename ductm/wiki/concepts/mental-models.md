# Mental Models

**Mental models** are cognitive frameworks that represent how things work — simplifications of complex phenomena used to reason, decide, and predict. Popularized by Charlie Munger ("latticework of mental models") and applied across business, science, and philosophy.

## Key Principle

No single mental model is sufficient. Munger's insight: the more models you have, and the more you integrate them, the better your thinking.

## Munger's Core Models (from Hormozi's Synthesis)

These are the models Munger applied most frequently:

- **[[inversion-thinking]]** — "Invert, always invert" — solve problems by asking how to guarantee failure, then flip
- **[[uesmo-framework]]** — Unique, Expensive, Sticky, Managed by Owner — criteria for the ideal business
- **Three L's** — Liquor, Leverage, Ladies — three domains of excess that destroy long-term stability
- **"Too Hard" pile** — Any deal requiring too much analysis goes in the pile; simplicity beats complexity
- **Brand as moat** — Brand appreciates with use; it is the most durable competitive advantage
- **Learning taxonomy** — Dumb/smart/wise = learn from nothing / own mistakes / others' mistakes

## Vipassana Connection

Goenka's framework distinguishes between three types of wisdom — and only the third is "real" in the sense that it transforms behavior:
- **Theoretical mental models** (Shrutmayi / Suta-maya-paññā) — acquired through reading/learning
- **Reasoned mental models** (Chintanmayi / Cintā-maya-paññā) — developed through analysis
- **Experientially verified models** (Bhavanamayi / Bhāvanā-maya-paññā) — only the third actually transforms behavior

See: [[bhavana-maya-panna]], [[goenka-progress-vipassana]]

**Cross-framework insight:** Munger's "latticework" approach is Shrutmayi + Chintanmayi Pragya applied with exceptional breadth. Vipassana adds the third tier — what Munger himself exhibited through decades of consistent practice of his principles.
