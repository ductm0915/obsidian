# Hungry Crowd

## Definition
The principle that **market selection is the strongest variable in how much money you make** — more powerful than your product quality, your pricing, or your persuasion skills. Put your hot dog stand in front of starving people, not in an empty lot.

## The 4-Criteria Filter

A "hungry crowd" must satisfy all four:

| Criterion | Why Required | Example Failure |
|---|---|---|
| **In pain** | Must-have, not nice-to-have | People who are vaguely dissatisfied but not desperate |
| **Can buy** | Purchasing power exists | Unemployed people who desperately need resume help — can't pay |
| **Easy to target** | You can find them efficiently | "Psychedelic Aztecs in Croatia" — real pain, no targeting path |
| **Growing market** | Tailwinds, not headwinds | Newspapers — even with 0 CAC, the market itself is shrinking |

You need all four. Three is insufficient.

## Why Market Dominates Offer
If you're selling toilet paper during COVID:
- Doesn't matter how cheap it is
- Doesn't matter how good it is
- Doesn't matter how fast it delivers
- The crowd is starving → you sell out every time

Supply and demand is "the strongest force in all of economics."

## Practical Selection Process
"What problems have I solved?" → who else has that problem? → can they pay? → can I find them? → is the market growing?

## Interaction with Other Variables
Market is the ceiling. Below that:
- **Offer quality** — superior offer in normal market = next lever
- **Persuasion** — effective closer + mediocre market still sells less than average closer + perfect market

## Connection to Value Equation
The [[value-equation]] applies *within* a market. "Likelihood of achievement" and "dream outcome" are relative to how intensely the market wants the result. A hungry crowd pre-loads both numerator variables before you even speak.
