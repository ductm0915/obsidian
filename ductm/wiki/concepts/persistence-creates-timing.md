# Persistence Creates Timing

## Core Principle

> *"You can time everything perfectly if your intention is to never stop."*

Perfect timing is a myth. Perfect preparation is achievable. The paradox: the only reliable way to be in the right place at the right time is to always be working — then timing takes care of itself.

---

## The Visualization

Imagine your lifeline as a horizontal line. Distributed randomly across it are opportunities (points of leverage, partnership, breakthrough). Most people try to predict when these moments arrive and only work then — but the probability of being present at those three random moments is very low if you're trying to time them.

If you work the whole time: **you will always be ready when opportunity arrives.** Timing becomes guaranteed.

---

## Starting Is the Condition

> *"People think they need perfect conditions to start, when in reality starting is the perfect condition."*

Starting creates the conditions for opportunity to be capitalized on. Not: "I'll start when conditions are right." The act of starting is what *creates* right conditions.

---

## Opportunities Multiply with Skill

> *"The more you do, the more you see you can do."*

Opportunities are not a fixed set waiting to be found — they scale with skill level. The better you become, the larger the space of opportunities visible to you. Elon Musk example: "He has unlimited skills, so the world of opportunities open to him is unlimited." He spent his younger years becoming a polymath across many domains.

This flips the common framing of "waiting for the right opportunity" — the bottleneck isn't opportunity, it's skill and preparation.

---

## Sticking with the Plan

The problem is never the plan. The plans aren't working because you're not working on the plan.

> *"The hard part about the plan is not creating the plan or even following the plan. It's sticking with the plan."*

Boredom is the failure mode. The person who works on the same project for 20 years becomes exceptional — not because of what they worked on, but because of the depth of repetition. "Whatever project the person works on — you do 20 years of reps and you do nothing else, you're going to be good."
