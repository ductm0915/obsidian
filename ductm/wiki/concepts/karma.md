# Karma (Kamma in Pāli)

**Karma** (Sanskrit) or **Kamma** (Pāli) is the law of cause and effect as applied to intentional actions — the principle that every volitional action (mental, verbal, or physical) generates a corresponding result (fruit/vipāka).

## Vipassana / Goenka Definition

In Goenka's teaching, karma is *not* fatalism — it is the active, moment-to-moment process by which mind creates its own experience:

> "Every time you react with craving or aversion, you are sowing a new seed of karma. Every time you observe with equanimity, you stop the cycle."

Karma operates through **sankara** (mental conditionings/volitions):
- A sankara generates an action
- The action leaves a new sankara
- The new sankara generates the next action
- This chain = the karma cycle

## WIT Framework Definition

In WIT (Wisdom Intelligence Training), karma is mapped through the **Three Karma Trees** (Ba Cây Nghiệp):
1. **Thân nghiệp** — karma of body/actions
2. **Khẩu nghiệp** — karma of speech
3. **Ý nghiệp** — karma of thought/intention

The WIT framework also connects karma to the **Law of Reincarnation** ([[quy-luat-luan-hoi]]) — karma persists across lifetimes.

## Practical Application

- **Vipassana path:** Break karma by observing sankara (body sensations) without reacting
- **WIT path:** Identify and transform karmic patterns through consciousness work
