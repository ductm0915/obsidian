# Mental Cognition

**Referenced from:** [[full-stack-mind]]
**Related:** [[tam-thuc-tu-duy-hanh-vi]]

## Overview

Mental cognition refers to the mechanics of **Layer 2** in the [[full-stack-mind]] framework — the processing layer between perception (Layer 1) and output/action (Layer 3).

It encompasses:
- How the mind processes incoming information
- Pattern recognition and mental model application
- Decision-making processes
- Logical reasoning and analytical thinking
- The cognitive biases and heuristics that shape thought

## In the Full Stack Mind

```
Layer 1: Perception (input)
    ↓
Layer 2: Mental Cognition (processing) ← this page
    ↓
Layer 3: Output (action/communication)
```

Layer 2 is where the critical work happens: raw perceptions are filtered, categorized, and interpreted through existing mental models. The quality of this processing determines the quality of decisions and actions.

## Improving Mental Cognition

- **Upgrade mental models** — better models = better processing
- **Reduce cognitive biases** — awareness of biases improves accuracy
- **Practice deliberate thinking** — slow, systematic reasoning vs. reactive responses
- **Cross-domain learning** — more diverse models = more flexible processing

## WIT Parallel

In WIT's framework ([[tam-thuc-tu-duy-hanh-vi]]):
- Tâm thức (consciousness) → Tư duy (thinking) → Hành vi (behavior)
- Mental cognition maps to the **Tư duy** layer
- WIT's unique contribution: the quality of Tư duy depends on the quality of Tâm thức beneath it
