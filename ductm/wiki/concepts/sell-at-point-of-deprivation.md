# Sell at Point of Greatest Deprivation

## Definition
**Timing is part of the offer.** The right offer at the wrong time is still the wrong offer. Sell at the moment of maximum hunger/need (point of greatest deprivation), not at the moment of greatest value delivery.

## The Steak Analogy
- "How was your steak?" → "Amazing." → "Want another one?" → "No thanks, I'm full."
- This is selling at point of greatest value but lowest deprivation. Result: no second sale.
- Correct: "You're starving — someone like you needs two steaks." BEFORE the first bite.
- Sell before satisfaction, not after.

## Why Most Sellers Get It Backwards
Sellers think: "After I delivered massive value, clients are most impressed → best time to upsell."

Reality: *Deprivation* drives motivation to act, not satisfaction with past delivery. Once someone feels they've gotten what they need, the gap closes, and motivation drops to near zero.

## The Gap = The Sales Opportunity
Sales motivation = size of the gap between current state and desired state. To sell effectively:
1. **Identify** where the gap currently is
2. **Extend** the perceived distance between current and desired
3. **Make visible** how far they still have to go
4. **Offer** the bridge at peak gap awareness

This is why sales calls begin with "Where are you now?" and "Where do you want to be?" — establishing the gap before revealing the solution.

## In Pitch/Content Design
Building up the "pain" in a presentation (showing the consequences of inaction, the gap getting bigger) before revealing the solution is the mechanism. This isn't manipulation — it's ensuring the prospect understands their actual situation.

## Connection to Deprivation Motivation
[[deprivation-motivation]]: the psychological reason this works. People are motivated by perceived lack, not by satisfaction. The seller's job is to make the gap maximally vivid without deceiving.
