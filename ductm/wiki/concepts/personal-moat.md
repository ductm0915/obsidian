# Personal Moat

[[matt-gray]]'s term for the unique content identity a founder builds by sharing the things they feel comfortable sharing online that speak to who they are at their core. Not a niche — "you can become the niche."

---

## The Ikigai Method

Matt uses the Japanese "Ikigai" ("Icky Guy") framework to find the personal moat. It sits at the intersection of four circles:

1. **What you're great at**
2. **What the world needs**
3. **What you can be paid for**
4. **What you love to do**

The center of these four circles = your personal moat content.

**Matt's example:** The intersection revealed: building distribution from day one, coaching founders, viewing entrepreneurship as a soulful journey, traveling the world, hosting founder retreats.

### Exercise
List 3-5 bullet points per quadrant → identify the commonalities in the center → that's the content you share.

---

## Key Insight: You Are the Niche

> "You don't need to pigeon-hole yourself as 'the SaaS guy' or 'the agency guy.' Just be you. Talk about the things that interest you."

The personal moat is less about a topic category and more about a perspective, energy, and set of values. Specificity comes from the person, not the subject.

---

## How It Develops

- Most of the moat clarity comes **from doing**, not planning
- 80% of time must be spent executing; 20% on thinking/planning
- Confidence, presence, and unique point of view emerge through consistent sharing

---

## Contrast with Contrarian Belief

[[contrarian-belief]] (Caleb Ralston) is the strategic positioning tool — the specific belief that differentiates you from industry tropes.

Personal Moat is the underlying identity foundation — the authentic whole self from which contrarian beliefs naturally emerge. The moat is broader; the contrarian belief is the sharpest expression of it.

---

## Relationship to Content Strategy

- The personal moat → defines your 4 content categories in [[content-gps]]
- The moat → generates Content IP (proprietary phrases/frameworks linked to you)
- A clear moat makes content creation sustainable (you're sharing things you genuinely care about)
