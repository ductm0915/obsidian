# Five-Line Story Framework

[[matt-gray]]'s simplified hero's journey for business storytelling. Five sequential lines that move an audience from recognition → trust → belief → action. Based on Joseph Campbell's hero's journey (same arc behind Star Wars, The Matrix, Harry Potter).

---

## The Five Lines

### 1. The Mirror — *Where they are right now*

Reflect the audience's current situation so precisely they think: *"This guy is literally reading my mind."*

**Example:**
> "You're stuck in Slack all day. Message after message, it feels impossible to keep up. You keep feeling like you're on this treadmill. The day ends, you look yourself in the mirror. You're brushing your teeth, and you can't help but think: 'How long can I keep going like this?' Every win still requires you."

**Purpose:** Earns trust immediately. If you can describe their situation better than they can, they trust you to solve it.

---

### 2. The Friction — *Why they're stuck*

The **personal cost**, not just the business problem. Goes beyond the logical to the emotional.

**Example:**
> "You keep missing your kids' birthdays. No real vacation in three years. Your partner asks when things will slow down, and you have no good answer."

**Purpose:** Creates emotional investment. People buy to avoid pain, not just to gain outcome.

---

### 3. The Realization — *The epiphany no one tells them*

The insight that reframes the problem entirely.

**Example:**
> "The problem isn't your work ethic — it's that *you are the system*. Replace yourself with systems and everything will accelerate."

**Purpose:** Positions you as the one who sees what others don't. Shifts from commiseration to hope.

---

### 4. The Shift — *Your transformation*

Show the concrete before/after of your own journey.

**Example:**
> "I went from 15-hour days to 4-hour days, burnt out to energized, trapped to free."

**Purpose:** Proof. Without the shift, the realization is just theory.

---

### 5. The Invitation — *The natural next step*

The CTA that feels inevitable, not salesy. See [[coupled-cta]].

**Example:**
> "If this resonates, here's what to do…" → leads to specific resource that closes the loop opened in lines 1–4.

**Purpose:** Convert belief into action. Not a generic "subscribe" — a tightly coupled next step.

---

## Why It Works

- **People don't buy conclusions, they buy clarity about themselves.** The Mirror and Friction earn that clarity early. Most storytellers rush past these to the solution — a fatal mistake.
- Rooted in Campbell's universal structure: the same story pattern across Greek, Norse, African, and Asian myths → baked into human psychology.
- Opens an emotional loop (lines 1–2) that only closes when the audience takes action (line 5).

---

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

- **[[open-loop-technique]]**: Lines 1–2 open the loop; line 5 closes it
- **[[coupled-cta]]**: Line 5 operationalizes the tightly coupled CTA principle
- **[[story-elements-framework]]** ([[alex-hormozi]]): Parallel framework; both simplify the hero's journey for business use. Comparison:
  - Mirror ≈ Setting + Character + Desire
  - Friction ≈ Struggle (external + internal)
  - Realization ≈ Eureka moment
  - Shift ≈ Victory
  - Invitation ≈ Resolution/CTA
- **[[personal-moat]]**: The story itself is the moat; this framework is how to tell it

---

## Application

1. **Diagnose weak line first.** Most stories fail because one of the five lines is broken. Identify which line is killing conversion — usually Mirror (too vague) or Invitation (too generic).
2. **Get outside the room.** Best Mirror and Friction language comes from talking to customers. The best copy exists in the words of your dream customer.
3. **Map before filming/writing.** Use the five lines as a planning template, not just a retrospective review.
