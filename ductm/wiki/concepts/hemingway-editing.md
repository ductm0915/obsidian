# Hemingway Editing

[[alex-hormozi]]'s simplicity-first editing process for writing. Named after the Hemingway App (hemingwayapp.com). Core goal: take 12th grade language → **3rd grade language with pictures**. Maximize how much mental capacity the reader can dedicate to *concepts* rather than *decoding words*.

---

## The Logic

> *"The average American reads at 5th–7th grade level — which means half of Americans read below that."*

Every word of complexity the reader has to decode is mental CPU stolen from understanding the idea. The goal is zero translation friction between your words and their comprehension.

> *"If you can't make something sound simple, it's because you don't get it — not them."*

---

## The Rules

1. **Eliminate if meaning survives.** If you can cut a sentence and the paragraph's meaning is unchanged → cut it.

2. **Shorter sentences.** A long sentence is always worse than two shorter sentences with the same meaning.

3. **Shorter words.** "Use" > "utilize." "Start" > "commence." Shorter = faster to process.

4. **Eliminate adverbs.** When you see an adverb, you've used the wrong verb.
   - *"I walked quickly"* → *"I ran"*
   - *"I really want"* → *"I need"* or restructure

5. **Simple tenses.** *"Wanted"* > *"would have liked to."* Remove modal stacking.

6. **No passive voice.** Active subjects doing active things.

## The Tool

**[hemingwayapp.com](https://hemingwayapp.com)** — free, no account needed.

- Paste any text → highlights complexity in color
- Shows: grade level, adverbs, passive voice, complex sentences, hard-to-read sentences
- Feedback is **immediate** — color changes as you edit → best behavioral training loop for writing

---

## The Editing Process (Hormozi's Full Sequence)

1. Write first draft (go until you hit a logical inconsistency → restart)
2. Self-edit: apply Hemingway rules to achieve target grade level
3. Send to **4–8 ideal readers** — people who respect him enough to be honest (not sycophants)
4. Apply feedback: if everyone flags the same thing → change it; if feedback is scattered → writing is fine (Stephen King rule)
5. Final pass with grammar editor (typos, mechanics)

For $100M Leads: **8 full drafts**, 600 pages of writing → 200 pages final.

---

## Why Ideal Readers (Not Fans)

Feedback quality depends on the reader's willingness to tell the truth. Fans will validate. The right readers are people who value the author but won't let status protect bad writing.
