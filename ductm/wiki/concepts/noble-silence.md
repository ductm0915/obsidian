# Noble Silence (Ārya Mauna)

**Noble Silence** is the rule of complete silence observed by all participants during days 1–10 of S.N. Goenka's 10-day Vipassana retreat.

## What It Entails

- No speaking to fellow students (not even eye contact or gestures)
- No reading, writing, or external communication
- No phone, internet, or contact with the outside world
- Silence of body, speech, and mind

Students may speak only to the teacher (during interview times) and to course management for practical needs.

## Purpose

Noble Silence creates the conditions for genuine introspection. By eliminating social performance (explaining, presenting, processing with others), the meditator is forced inward — to observe what actually arises in the mind.

> "You are not on a holiday or a social retreat. You are performing a surgical operation on your own mind." — Goenka

## The Inner Architecture of Silence

Silence is not merely external. The deeper practice is silence of mental chatter — learning to observe thoughts without following them, which Vipassana trains through body sensation observation.

## Why Noble Silence Works

1. **No impression management** — the mind can't perform for others
2. **No escape valve** — what arises must be observed, not vented
3. **Amplified sensitivity** — the mind sharpens when external noise ceases
4. **Collective field** — a room of 100 people in silence creates a potent meditative environment
