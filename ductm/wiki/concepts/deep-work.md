# Deep Work

**Deep work** (popularized by Cal Newport) refers to cognitively demanding tasks performed in a state of distraction-free concentration — operating at the edge of cognitive capacity to produce high-value output.

Sam Ovens' **four-hour workday** framework operationalizes deep work: protect the first 4 productive hours of each day for cognitively demanding work (elimination-first, no meetings, no email).

## Key Principles

1. **Cognitive depth ≠ time** — 4 hours of deep focus > 8 hours of shallow work
2. **Elimination before optimization** — Remove shallow tasks before optimizing deep ones
3. **Environment design** — Remove distractions structurally, not through willpower
