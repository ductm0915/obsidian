# 7 Bố Thí (Seven Essential Types of Giving)

**Translation:** 7 Important Donations/Givings of Life
**Origin:** [[wit-organization]], taught by [[que-minh]]
**Buddhist root:** Dāna (दान) — the practice of generosity as a path to merit accumulation
**Source:** [[wit-k07-bo-thi]]

---

## Core Idea

WIT's 7 Bố Thí framework is a Buddhist-rooted, practically applied system for accumulating công đức (moral merit) and phước đức (karmic blessing) through seven specific types of giving. The framework transforms "giving" from an occasional charitable act into a continuous daily practice — "như hơi thở" (like breathing).

The fundamental insight: **giving is not altruism, it is investment**. Every act of genuine giving creates phước đức, which flows back to the giver as tài (wealth), sắc (health/beauty), danh (reputation), thực (food/sustenance), or thùy (rest/sleep) according to the giver's dominant desires (tham).

However, the deepest level of practice transcends this transactional framing: **thi ân bất cầu báo** — giving without any expectation of return. At this level, bố thí becomes effortless and the accumulation of merit becomes equally natural.

See source: [[wit-k07-bo-thi]]

---

## Why 7 Specific Types?

Each of the 7 bố thí addresses a specific channel through which human beings can give — ranging from the most subtle (a facial expression) to the most material (physical service and resources). Together they cover the full range of daily human interactions, making it impossible to encounter another person without an opportunity to give.

Cô Quế Minh frames the 7 as a complete system: "Con người chúng ta nếu làm được bảy bố thí thì như hơi thở thì nó cũng thuộc về thi ân bất cầu báo rồi đó cả nhà." (If you can do the 7 givings like breathing, you have naturally become someone who gives without expectation.)

## The 7 Types

### 1. Nhan Thí — Giving of the Smile

Offering a genuine, heartfelt smile to another. The most accessible bố thí: no resources, no relationship, no preparation needed. Available with every human encounter.

**Three levels:**
- Surface smile (nghi thức/ritual)
- Smile from genuine inner joy (an vui tự tâm)
- Smile of deep recognition and gratitude for the other person

**Karmic return:** People who nhan thí consistently attract warmth, welcome, and ease in all interactions. "Nhiều khi lại keo kiệt với chính mình và chính những người thân yêu trong gia đình" — even the most natural smile can become guarded; relearning it is a profound practice.

### 2. Nhãn Thí — Giving of the Gaze

Offering an eye contact that truly contains (chứa đựng) the other person. The distinction: "nhìn" (to look with eyes) vs. "thấy" (to see from the heart). Eyes look; the heart sees.

**Three levels:**
- Level 1: Nhìn mà thấy — actually perceiving that a person exists (not invisible to you)
- Level 2: Thấy điều tốt đẹp — perceiving only the good in others; using trí tuệ tầng bậc 1 (ability to discern good from bad) to deliberately choose to hold only the good
- Level 3 (peak): Chứa đựng sự chuyển hóa của con người — holding within your gaze the vision of who this person can fully become; seeing their transformation potential as already real

The peak level is attributed to [[hong-trieu-bao]]: he maintains within his gaze the fully realized version of every person who enters WIT, which is why the community creates such profound transformation.

**For practitioners:** Nhãn thí is the second most essential after nhan thí — together they are the minimum of human acknowledgment. "Chưa cần phải làm gì hết, chúng ta chỉ cần vừa gặp và tiếp xúc" — even before saying a word, nhan thí + nhãn thí can connect two people.

### 3. Ngôn Thí — Giving of Words

Offering speech that benefits others:
- Words that awaken wisdom (creates công đức)
- Words that bring joy, encouragement, or comfort (creates phước đức)
- Words that reveal truth in a form the listener can receive

The inverse is also true: words that diminish, confuse, or manipulate create ác đức (negative karmic burden). Ngôn thí is one of the most powerful and most frequently misused channels.

**For Mentors:** Ngôn thí is the primary professional instrument. The entire practice of inner-life coaching is a form of continuous ngôn thí.

### 4. Tâm Thí — Giving of the Heart (Gratitude)

Bố thí sự trân trọng biết ơn — offering genuine appreciation and reverence in all circumstances:
- When good things happen → express gratitude
- When unwanted things happen → find the lesson embedded in the experience, then express gratitude for the lesson
- When someone serves you → express genuine recognition of their contribution

The scope is total: "Cho nên có con người đến với chúng ta là kiểu gì chúng ta cũng có thể trân trọng biết ơn được." (However a person appears in our life, we can always practice tâm thí toward them.)

Tâm thí is WIT's most-practiced daily bố thí — the phrase "trân trọng biết ơn" (appreciation and gratitude) is a constant refrain in all WIT sessions, functioning as a lived practice rather than a phrase.

### 5. Phòng Thí (Bao Dung Thí) — Giving of Tolerance

Bố thí lòng bao dung — extending genuine forgiveness, tolerance, and acceptance. The "phòng" (room/space) in the name suggests making room for others' imperfections.

Also encompasses pháp thí (dharma giving): sharing wisdom or teachings that help others develop their own inner lives and worldview.

**The practical application:** Bao dung is not passive tolerance — it is active recognition that the person who wronged you was operating from their own karma and limited worldview. Understanding this dissolves the need for resentment.

One of the three core inner dispositions taught throughout WIT: an vui (peace), bao dung (tolerance), trân trọng biết ơn (gratitude). Together these are described as the foundation of tâm thái đúng (correct inner disposition).

### 6. Thân Thí — Giving of the Body (Service/Action)

Bố thí hành động nhân ái — offering acts of kindness and service. The most externally visible bố thí; the form most commonly practiced even by those who have never studied the concept.

**Three levels (from lowest to highest impact):**

| Level | Vietnamese | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Lower (hạ sách) | Cho vật chất | Give material goods/money; addresses immediate need but person returns to same state when resources run out |
| Middle (trung sách) | Cho năng lực chuyên môn | Give professional skills/capability; teach them to fish; more sustainable |
| Highest (thượng sách) | Cho quan niệm sống | Give a worldview/philosophy of life; changes how they see themselves and their circumstances; creates permanent transformation |

Cô Quế Minh's own story with [[hong-trieu-bao]] illustrates all three levels: he gave her shelter (material), work as an MC (capability), and a completely new worldview (quan niệm sống). Without all three, no lasting transformation occurs.

**The principle:** "Cho quan niệm sống là thượng sách" (Giving a worldview is the highest strategy). For WIT Mentors, this defines their professional practice.

**Balance consideration:** Do not only give the highest level and neglect the immediate. "Có những trường hợp là cần phải bố thí cả ba" — some situations require all three levels simultaneously. Wisdom (trí tuệ) determines which to apply when.

**Thi ân bất cầu báo:** The ideal state of thân thí — giving regardless of whether the recipient acknowledges, reciprocates, or even wants the gift. Priority order for giving: family first, then those who are grateful, then all others.

### 7. Tọa Thí — Giving of the Seat (Space/Position)

Bố thí chỗ ngồi / không gian — the most subtle and relational bố thí. "Tọa" means "seat" or "sitting position." The act of making space — literal and figurative — for another person:

- Giving someone a seat at the table
- Elevating someone's position or status
- Creating a platform for someone to be heard and seen
- Making room in your attention and consideration for another's presence

Tọa thí is especially powerful for Mentors and community leaders: every time you genuinely give someone a platform to speak, be seen, and be celebrated, you are practicing tọa thí.

In WIT's learning environments, tọa thí manifests as the facilitator's role: creating the conditions where participants can sit comfortably, share freely, and feel genuinely received.

## Phước Đức and Công Đức — The Returns

**Công đức (moral merit):** Created when actions genuinely awaken wisdom or activate virtue in others. Primary channel: ngôn thí and thân thí at the highest levels. Công đức is more active and deliberate.

**Phước đức (blessing/karmic merit):** Created by any act of genuine benefit to others, conscious or unconscious, witnessed or unwitnessed. Phước đức is more passive and accumulative.

Both can be enjoyed in this lifetime (phước đức âm) or carried to the next (phước đức dương). The practitioner's dominant desires (tham: tài, sắc, danh, thực, thùy) determine in which domain the accumulated merit manifests.

**Key liberation:** "Tích được phước nhưng mà do cái tánh của mình á, mình thích cái gì thì phước của mình nó sẽ đổ về đó." (Merit flows toward what you most desire — no need to calculate which bố thí gives more of which type of return.)

## Relationship to Inner Development

The 7 bố thí are not supplemental to WIT's inner-life curriculum — they are its practical expression. Every day of genuinely living the 7 bố thí is a day of:

- Nhan thí → practicing an vui (peace/joy) as a continuous state
- Nhãn thí → practicing trí tuệ tầng bậc 1 (discernment) and chứa đựng (containing/receiving)
- Ngôn thí → practicing lời nói kiến tạo giá trị (value-creating speech)
- Tâm thí → practicing trân trọng biết ơn (gratitude) in all circumstances
- Phòng thí → practicing bao dung (tolerance) as a natural state
- Thân thí → practicing nhân cách kiện toàn (complete character) through action
- Tọa thí → practicing khiêm tốn (humility) and yêu thương (love) through space-making

Practicing all 7 naturally cultivates the character traits described in WIT's 9 attributes of complete character.

## Connection to [[buong-tai]]

The practice of 7 bố thí is intimately connected to the concept of buông tay (releasing/letting go). Each act of giving involves releasing attachment:
- Nhan thí releases the contracted face, the defensive mask
- Nhãn thí releases the habit of not-seeing, of ignoring others
- Ngôn thí releases the words we withhold out of fear or indifference
- Tâm thí releases the expectation that circumstances should be different
- Phòng thí releases the grievance, the held wound
- Thân thí releases the calculation of return-on-giving
- Tọa thí releases the need for one's own prominence

Giving is releasing; releasing is freedom; freedom is the beginning of genuine abundance.
