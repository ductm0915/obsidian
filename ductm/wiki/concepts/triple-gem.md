# Triple Gem (Ti-Sarana — Three Refuges)

The **Triple Gem** (also: Three Jewels, Ti-Sarana) is the foundational refuge in Buddhist teaching, consisting of:

1. **Buddha** — the awakened one; the exemplar of what is possible
2. **Dhamma** — the law of nature; the path and practice
3. **Sangha** — the community of practitioners; collective support

## Goenka's Non-Sectarian Interpretation

In Goenka's teaching, the Triple Gem is freed from religious ritual:

- **Buddha** = not a deity to pray to, but a role model who proved liberation is possible for a human being
- **Dhamma** = the universal law (not a sectarian religion) — truth works the same for everyone regardless of belief
- **Sangha** = not a religious institution, but the community of all who walk the path sincerely

Taking refuge in the Triple Gem is taking refuge in the possibility of your own liberation.

## "Atta dīpa" — The Fourth Refuge

Goenka adds a crucial fourth element from the Buddha's own words:

> "Be a lamp unto yourself. Be your own refuge. Seek no external refuge."

The deepest refuge is the Dhamma you have personally developed within yourself — not the Buddha as deity, not the institution, but your own verified truth.
