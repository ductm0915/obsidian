# Quy Luật Luân Hồi (Law of Karma/Reincarnation)

**Origin:** [[wit-organization]] — [[hong-trieu-bao]]
**Primary sources:** [[wit-k07-nhan-thuc-con-nguoi]] (Sessions 05 and 12)
**Also referenced:** [[wit-k07-hon-nhan]] (Session 2)

## Overview

"Quy luật luân hồi" is WIT's working application of the Buddhist concept of karma and reincarnation — adapted from its traditional religious context and redeployed as a practical framework for understanding human life, behavior, and relationship dynamics. [[hong-trieu-bao]] acknowledges that this concept was "dominated by religious language" and reclaims it for secular, practical use.

The framework operates at three levels:
1. **Vũ trụ quan** (Cosmological): the cycles of birth, death, rebirth across lives — addressed briefly, not the focus
2. **Thế giới quan** (World view): all things return to their source — earth returns to earth, water to water, fire to fire — the cycle of material existence
3. **Nhân sinh quan** (Human life view): the continuous cycle of identity-taking and identity-releasing within a single life — **this is the primary application**

## Core Definition

**Luân** = going toward, becoming, taking on, manifesting  
**Hồi** = returning, releasing, going back to "not being anyone"

The cycle: empty (hồi) → take on identity/role (luân) → fulfill → release (hồi) → take on next identity → repeat.

Alternative vocabulary used interchangeably:
- **Buông – Định** (Release — Commit/Define)
- **Không – Có** (Zero/Nothing — Something/Yes)
- **0 – 1** (binary language analogy)

## The Nhân Sinh (Human Life) Application

Every day, every person cycles through multiple rounds of luân hồi without realizing it. The teaching uses a day-in-the-life illustration:

- Wake up: not yet anyone (hồi — the base state)
- Interact with your child: you become "parent" (luân)
- Leave the child, walk to your spouse: release "parent," become "spouse" (hồi → luân)
- Arrive at work: release "spouse," become "CEO" or "employee" (hồi → luân)
- Leave work: release role, return to base (hồi)
- Meet parents: become "child" again (luân)

A person who lives this cycle consciously — fully inhabiting each role when in it, fully releasing when leaving — has a life that is fluid, appropriate, and rich. They create maximum value in each context because they are fully present in each role.

## Two Pathologies

### Only Luân (Never Hồi) — Attachment to Identity
The person who takes on a role (CEO, expert, caregiver, parent) and never releases it. They identify with the role; the role becomes their self-definition. This creates:
- Rigidity in other contexts (showing up as "CEO" at the dinner table)
- Suffering when the role is threatened, criticized, or removed
- Inability to create value outside their defined role

WIT term: **Sĩ thân** — being attached to one's title/status/identity.

### Only Hồi (Never Luân) — Attachment to Emptiness
The person who releases everything but never commits to anything — always in the state of "I am nobody, I am free," but never taking on any identity or role long enough to create value. This creates:
- No value creation
- Depletion of accumulated merit (phước báu) over time — because merit accumulates through value-creation, which requires luân

## Why This Matters for Human Understanding

The framework explains why different people have different capacities and life circumstances without attributing it to luck or unfairness. A person who has cultivated excellent luân-hồi fluency across many lifetimes (under the vũ trụ quan reading) or across many years in this life arrives with different "starting resources" of:
- Trí tuệ (wisdom/intelligence)
- Tâm thái (inner state quality)
- Phước báu (accumulated merit)

This is not fatalistic — one can actively improve all three within a lifetime through the right practice — but it explains current differences without judgment.

## Application: "Not Being Anyone"

A key teaching phrase: "Chúng ta không là ai. Do không là ai nên chúng ta có thể trở thành bất kỳ ai." (We are not anyone. Because we are not anyone, we can become anyone.)

The practice of tánh không (emptiness of inner self) — "I am not anyone" — is the foundation state. From here, one can intentionally define (định) "I am becoming a person with wisdom," "I am becoming a skilled parent," "I am becoming a good friend." One then directs attention and effort from that defined identity, creates value, and releases when the context changes.

The three-step practice:
1. **Hồi**: invoke the recognition "I am not anyone, I have no fixed nature" (tánh không of self)
2. **Luân**: consciously commit to "I am becoming [identity]" — make this concrete through clear concept (rõ khái niệm) and clear image (rõ hình) of that identity
3. **Create and release**: create value from that identity, then release when the context closes

## Application to Understanding Others

The luân hồi framework changes how you see other people's behaviors. When someone acts badly in one context, you recognize: they are stuck in a role (their luân is fixed, hồi is absent). When someone acts well in one area but fails in another, you recognize: their luân-hồi fluency varies by domain.

This generates **compassion without condoning**: the other person's behavior is a structural consequence of their awareness level, not a personal attack. The appropriate response is not to take it personally but to meet them where they are.

## Application to Marriage

[[hong-trieu-bao]] (Session 2, marriage module) warns against "quy hồi tiền kiếp" (past-life regression services) that reveal what a person "was" to a spouse in a prior life. If you discover your spouse was an enemy in a past life, you cannot un-know that, and it interferes with present-moment relationship building. The principle: what matters is the duyên (conditions) of this present life, and this present life's choices determine the quality of the relationship going forward.

## Relationship to Other WIT Concepts

- **[[9-dang-nguoi]]**: each of the 9 relationship types is a luân the other person is currently occupying — recognizing it allows appropriate đối đãi (relating)
- **[[16-tanh-nguoi]]**: tánh are the tendencies that pull one into particular luân and resist appropriate hồi — cultivating awareness of tánh supports luân-hồi fluency
- **[[nang-cap-moi-quan-he]]**: upgrading a relationship requires recognizing which role (luân) the other person is currently in and relating from that frame
- **[[buong-tai]]**: "releasing" talent, roles, outcomes — is the practical application of hồi in the domain of work and material goods
