# Arahant Path (Path of the Fully Liberated)

An **Arahant** (Pāli: arahant; Sanskrit: arhat) is a being who has fully traversed the path of liberation and eradicated all defilements — the final stage before nibbāna (nirvana).

## Four Stages of Liberation (Sotāpatti to Arahant)

| Stage | Fetters Broken | Description |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Sotāpatti** | 3 (identity view, doubt, rites/rituals) | Stream-entrant — will not fall below human rebirth |
| **Sakadāgāmi** | + weakening of sensual desire and ill will | Once-returner |
| **Anāgāmi** | + sensual desire and ill will | Non-returner |
| **Arahant** | All 10 fetters | Fully liberated |

## Goenka's Teaching

Goenka presents the arahant path as the natural culmination of Vipassana practice — not a supernatural achievement but the result of sustained, correct practice over lifetimes (or an intense concentrated lifetime).

The key: eradicating all sankara (mental conditionings) accumulated over lifetimes. Each session of Vipassana reduces the sankara load.
