# Four Ways to Advertise

## Definition
The foundational matrix outlining the only four possible methods a business can use to let people know their product exists. Outlined by [[alex-hormozi]], it removes the complexity from marketing by breaking it down into a 2x2 grid based on **audience size** (1-to-1 vs. 1-to-many) and **audience awareness** (stranger vs. known).

## The Four Methods
1. **Warm Outreach (1-to-1 / Known):** Reaching out individually to friends, family, past customers, or people who already know who you are.
2. **Cold Outreach (1-to-1 / Stranger):** Reaching out to completely new individuals via cold DMs, cold emails, or cold calling.
3. **Content (1-to-many / Known):** Broadcasting messages (like organic social posts, podcasts, or email newsletters) to an audience that has opted in or subscribed to you.
4. **Paid Ads (1-to-many / Stranger):** Broadcasting messages by paying platforms (Facebook, YouTube, direct mail, radio) to push your message into the feeds of strangers.

## Application
According to Hormozi, if a business owner is trying to scale from $0 to $1M-$3M/year, they should be spending the first four hours of every single day executing on at least one of these four quadrant activities. If you are not doing one of these four, you are not aggressively advertising, which means you are capping growth.
