# Delegation Math

[[matt-gray]]'s ROI framework for deciding when and whom to hire. Calculate the value of the founder's time saved vs. the cost of the hire to determine whether a hire generates leverage.

---

## The Formula

```
ROI = (Founder Hourly Rate × Hours Saved per Month) / Monthly Hire Cost
```

### Example

- Founder hourly rate: $6,000/hour
- Social media manager cost: $8,000/month
- Hours saved: 80 hours/month (20 hours/week)
- Calculation: ($6,000 × 80) / $8,000 = **60x ROI**

The saved time must be **reallocated** to high-leverage revenue-generating activities (not free time) for the math to work.

---

## Critical Assumption

The ROI is only real if the freed hours are used on higher-value work. The founder's hourly rate is not what they currently earn — it's what they *could* generate with that time if applied to their highest-leverage work (deals, strategy, flagship content, key hires).

---

## Relationship to AED System

Delegation Math is part of the [[aed-system]] process:
1. Audit tasks
2. Eliminate unnecessary ones
3. Automate what can be automated
4. **Delegate** the rest — using this formula to justify and prioritize hires

---

## Why Founders Resist Delegation

Matt describes being the "chief everything officer" early on — doing everything himself with a 23-person team. The root failure: not trusting the math, and not building documentation/systems to hand things off cleanly.

The fix: film a Loom video for every delegated task; make the hire the **systems designer** going forward (they own, improve, and iterate on the process).
