# Conviction Framework (Hidden Dialogue)

## Definition
[[alex-hormozi]]'s framework for how belief drives tonality — and how tonality closes deals independently of scripted words. The insight: two simultaneous conversations happen on every sales call, and scripts only address one of them.

## The Two Dialogues

```
What you say  →  Speaks to the LOGICAL brain
How you say it →  Speaks to the EMOTIONAL brain (the one making the decision)
```

> "How you say what you say is what you say."

The emotional brain doesn't process words — it processes tone, pace, pauses, and energy. If these signals contradict the logical content, the prospect feels it and resists even without articulating why.

## Conviction Corrects Tone

Training tonality directly (drilling pitch inflections, vocal exercises) works eventually but is slow. The shortcut:

> **"Conviction will correct your tone."**

If you genuinely believe the product helps, your tone will naturally be:
- Confident (not apologetic when naming price)
- Curious (not defensive when meeting objections)
- Present (not distracted by fear of losing the sale)

This explains the hot streak / cold streak cycle that all salespeople experience. They don't forget how to sell — they temporarily stop believing. Same skills, different result.

## Practical Implication for Training
- Filling conviction is often **more impactful than script training**.
- Have reps read testimonials, talk to happy clients, revisit successful calls before shifts.
- Role play and recording analysis: watch your hot streak recordings when you go cold to recalibrate.

## Tone Patterns to Master (advanced)
- **Raise voice at end of sentence** → makes a statement into a question
- **Lower voice** → signals gravity, importance
- "Ghetto tone" (humor/lightness) → defuses hard conversations without losing the frame
- Childlike curiosity tone → "Huh, that's so interesting, tell me more" → keeps you non-adversarial
