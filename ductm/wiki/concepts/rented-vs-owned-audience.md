# Rented vs. Owned Audience

A critical distinction in audience building: social media followers are **rented** (the platform owns them); email subscribers are **owned** (you control the relationship directly).

---

## Rented Audience

- Lives on: YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, TikTok
- The platform owns the audience, not you
- Organic reach: ~10% of followers see any given post (algorithm-gated)
- Platform can change algorithms, ban accounts, or shut down
- Best use: awareness and discovery; top-of-funnel

## Owned Audience

- Lives in: email lists, newsletters, SMS
- You control delivery — no algorithm gatekeeping
- Email open rate: ~70% (vs. ~10% social reach)
- Cannot be taken away by a platform
- "Your email list is probably the most valuable asset you can build in your business." — [[matt-gray]]
- Best use: relationship depth, conversion, monetization

---

## Migration Strategy

The goal is to use the rented audience to build the owned audience:

```
Rented (Social) → CTAs → Owned (Email List) → Conversion → Monetized
```

Every platform should have CTAs directing followers to join the newsletter. Matt places CTAs in 20 locations across all platforms.

---

## Metrics Comparison

| | Rented (Social) | Owned (Email) |
|---|---|---|
| Reach per post | ~10% of followers | ~70% open rate |
| Platform risk | High | None |
| Relationship depth | Shallow | Deep (permission-based) |
| Conversion rate | Low | 120% better than social |
| Value | Awareness | Revenue |

---

## Newsletter as Owned Asset

Newsletter audiences convert **120% better** than social media followers because of **permission** — they chose to receive your content in their inbox. This converts to revenue at a fundamentally different rate.
