# Decision Making

**Decision making** is the process of selecting between alternatives — the quality of which determines lifetime outcomes more than any other skill.

## Sam Ovens Framework

Three key Ovens frameworks for decision quality:
1. **Inversion** — ask "what would make this fail?" before "what would make this succeed?"
2. **Second-order consequences** — trace every decision 2-3 levels downstream
3. **Full-stack thinking** — understand all components of a system before deciding

See: [[inversion-method]], [[second-order-consequences]], [[full-stack-mind]]

## Vipassana / WIT Framework

Both Vipassana and WIT argue that most "decision making failures" are not cognitive — they are **state failures**: decisions made from reactive mind (craving/aversion sankara) rather than equanimous mind.

The Vipassana precondition for good decisions: observe the sensation of urgency/fear/desire that's driving the decision before acting.
