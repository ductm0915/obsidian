# Ideal Future Now

## Definition

The principle that you should **start practicing your ideal future habits today at a small scale**, rather than waiting for the "right" conditions to begin. If your ideal life involves writing 2 hours every morning, start with 5 minutes today. The ramp-up happens gradually, but the practice must begin immediately.

> "It doesn't make sense to wait and put off what you're already going to be doing."
> — [[dan-koe]]

## Core Logic

The argument is elegantly simple:
1. You've identified what habits your ideal future requires (writing, exercise, creation, learning)
2. You will need to do these things eventually anyway
3. Delaying means you'll *still* have to start small and ramp up — just later
4. Therefore, starting now (at small scale) gives you more compounding time and eliminates the ramp-up problem

Put differently: **the future version of you is not a different person who will find it easier to start.** They will face the same startup friction. Starting now transfers that friction to the period when you have the most time and fewest obligations.

## The Tapering Method

Koe's practical approach:
- Want to write 2 hours/day → start with **5 minutes/day**
- Week 2: 15 minutes/day
- Month 2: 30 minutes/day
- Month 6: 1 hour/day
- Year 2: 2 hours/day

The goal isn't duration early on — it's **building the identity anchor** that "I am someone who writes every day." Duration follows identity.

## Why This Is Often Missed

Most people think: "I'll start when I have more time / more money / when I finish this project." But those conditions are either permanent or irrelevant. You will never have more time than now (obligations only compound with age). The idea of a future self with free time and perfect conditions is a cognitive mirage.

> "If you don't have the time right now to write for 15 minutes every day, you're not going to have the time in the future if writing is going to be what you're doing for work."
> — [[dan-koe]]

## Connection to Identity

This concept implicitly ties to the principle that **doing = being** (from [[hormozi-how-to-win]]): you become the writer by writing, not by planning to write. The small daily practice is not a warm-up for real action; it *is* the real action.

## Hard Reset Application

When life gets off track, the [[dan-koe-get-ahead-99]] hard reset process can be used to identify which "ideal future" habits have fallen away — and restart them at their smallest viable form.
