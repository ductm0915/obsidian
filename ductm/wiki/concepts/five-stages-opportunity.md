# Five Stages of Opportunity

[[alex-hormozi]]'s framework describing the predictable emotional arc every entrepreneur goes through when pursuing a new opportunity. Most people never reach stage 4 or 5 — they cycle endlessly through stages 1–3 via **opportunity hopping**.

---

## The Five Stages

| Stage | Name | State of Mind |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Uninformed Optimism** | "This looks amazing. Everyone's making money. I can do this." |
| 2 | **Informed Pessimism** | "This is harder than I thought. There's a lot they didn't tell me." |
| 3 | **Valley of Despair** | "Is this really for me? Maybe I'm not passionate about this." |
| 4 | **Informed Optimism** | "I understand the process now. I can see the path to get there." |
| 5 | **Achievement** | You win. You accomplish what you set out to do. |

---

## The Trap: Opportunity Hopping

Most people quit at **Stage 3** and jump back to Stage 1 with a new opportunity — forever repeating the cycle:

> Uninformed Optimism → Informed Pessimism → Valley of Despair → *new opportunity* → Uninformed Optimism → ...

The irony: when they jump to the next shiny thing, they don't realize *that* opportunity also has a Valley of Despair waiting for them. "The grass is green on the other side because there's manure on both sides — you just didn't know until you got there."

---

## What Stage 4 Actually Feels Like

Stage 4 (Informed Optimism) is not blind hope — it's earned confidence:
- You understand the process of getting from where you are to where you want to go
- You've broken it into concrete, repeatable actions
- You have a feedback loop to measure progress
- You know how to win — you just have to execute

---

## Why This Matters

Wealth is made during the Valley of Despair — because that's when most people leave. The people who stay through Stage 3 inherit the market share of everyone who quit:

> *"A smooth sea never made a skilled sailor."*

Hard times are not obstacles to success; they are the proof of the skill. Clients remember who stayed open during the recession. The market rewards those who didn't hop.

Once you've completed the full cycle once, you stop fearing Stage 3 on the next opportunity — you recognize it as temporary and predictable.
