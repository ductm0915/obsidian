# My Fault Philosophy

## Definition
The foundational principle that **everything is your fault** — including circumstances outside your control. Not a description of what caused your situation, but a declaration of who holds agency over it.

Two words: *It's my fault.*

## The Core Logic
Whatever you blame is where you also cast power. If you attribute your failure to X (a person, system, event, race, economic condition), then X controls your future. Keeping blame external is keeping power external.

- "I can't succeed because of my mother's behavior" → your mother controls your success.
- "I can't succeed because of this political party's policies" → the political party controls your success.
- "It's my fault I haven't learned sales yet" → it's within your power to change it.

## The Victim Frame Taken to Extreme
Hormozi's technique: take the victim narrative to its logical conclusion. "You're right — you'll never make this work. Now what?" The moment someone hears their victim frame affirmed completely, they instinctively reject it ("No, I can too!"). That's the moment agency returns.

## What It Doesn't Mean
- Not self-blame in a psychological harm sense.
- Not pretending circumstances don't exist.
- Not "it's your fault you were born poor/abused/disadvantaged."
- It means: *given where I am now, it's my job to change it.* Not because you caused the past, but because you're the only one who can change the future.

## The Foundation Metaphor
Success is a building. The base layer (who is in power, who is source?) must be *you*. Everything else (learning sales, marketing, scaling) is built on top of this. If the foundation is blame, everything else crumbles. "You cannot build on top of blaming other people."

## Practical Implication
The first step out of poverty, failure, or stagnation is not a tactic — it's this declaration. After that:
1. Use what you have (time, energy, anger, proximity to problems)
2. Accept the cost (brute force when leverage is absent)
3. Build skills with brutal repetition

## What Offends You Controls You

Extended formulation from [[hormozi-brutally-honest-truths]]: **whatever offends you, controls you.** This is the same logic applied to emotionally charged situations.

Example: "I can't be in a committed relationship because my mother hurt me as a child." By saying this repeatedly: my mother controls how my love life goes. By using the excuse: I give my mother power over my future.

The pattern: attributing current inability to a past wound = handing ongoing control to whoever inflicted it.

> *"The day that you choose to control yourself is the day that you choose to divorce your results from the very viable reasons you have to not win. You are right. And so what?"*

The psychological trap with parents specifically: you may believe that becoming successful "validates" how you were parented. So you stay unsuccessful to punish them — and stay there as long as you want them to control you.

## Play the Cards You're Dealt

Hormozi's father's response to his reluctance to use inherited advantages:

> *"Do you think Shaq, when he was 7 feet tall, was like 'It wouldn't be fair for me to play basketball. Other people aren't as tall as me'? You play the cards you're dealt. These are the cards you have. Play them."*

Others will disqualify your success regardless of circumstances — "he had connections," "she was born in America," "he's a man." Since they'll always find a justification for discounting your achievement, the rational choice is to use every advantage you have and ignore the verdict.
