# Saves Over Shares

**Source:** [[skool-games-q4-2025]] (Alex Hormozi)

TikTok Shop's meta-analysis finding: **saves** are the #1 metric correlated with purchase intent — not shares, not likes, not views.

## The Core Insight

- **Share:** Someone forwards a meme to friends → viral reach, but does not build authority or purchase intent in the sharer
- **Save:** Someone saves "11 steps to fix X" → they don't know the steps yet (non-obvious content) → they trust the creator → high future purchase likelihood

> "The likelihood that you're like, 'That guy — or gal — really knows what she's talking about.' […] You only save it if you don't know the 11 steps."

## Why Saves Signal Purchase Intent

When someone saves content, they:
1. Recognize they need this information (felt the gap)
2. Trust the source enough to come back
3. Self-identify as a prospect (they have the problem the content solves)

A shared meme creates zero authority transfer: the sharer is just the messenger. The saver treats the creator as the trusted source.

## Application: Content → Ads

Hormozi's workflow:
1. Sort your content by **saves, not views**
2. The top-saved pieces are your highest purchase-intent content
3. Run those as ads with a simple overlay CTA banner
4. The platform distributes what organic already validated

> "Look at the content that has the most saves. Try running those as your ads."

## What Content Gets Saved

- Non-obvious insight (if it were obvious, nobody needs to bookmark it)
- Actionable steps to a specific problem
- "Layers below the surface" thinking — not the ChatGPT first-level answer
- Content that removes friction from people's lives (friction-removal heuristic)

## Contrast: Saves vs. Virality

| | Saves | Shares/Virality |
|---|---|---|
| **Drives** | Purchase intent | Reach |
| **Content type** | Non-obvious, actionable | Entertaining, relatable |
| **Trust built** | High | Low |
| **Uses as ad** | Excellent | Poor |
| **Audience quality** | Qualified buyers | Mass exposure |
