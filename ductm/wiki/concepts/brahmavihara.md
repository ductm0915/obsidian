# Brahmavihāra — Four Sublime States

The four sublime states (Pali: brahmavihāra) are mettā (loving-kindness), karuṇā (compassion), muditā (sympathetic joy), and upekkhā (equanimity). In Goenka's teaching, these are FRUITS of sīla-samādhi-paññā, NOT standalone practices. One cannot cultivate genuine brahmavihāra through willpower alone; they arise naturally when the mind is purified through Vipassana. Ego (ahaṅkāra) is the root obstacle. Mettā Bhāvanā is taught at the end of each Vipassana sitting as an overflow of purified awareness.
