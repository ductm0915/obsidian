# Saddhā — Enlightened Faith

**Saddhā** (Pāli) is the first of the **Pañca Bala** (Five Strengths) and one of the most misunderstood concepts in Goenka's teaching. It is often translated as "faith" but Goenka insists it is **enlightened faith** — not blind belief.

## The Distinction

| Blind Faith | Enlightened Faith (Saddhā) |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Believing without verification | Trusting based on personal verification |
| Stopping inquiry | Opening inquiry |
| Dependent on authority | Develops personal authority |
| Static | Grows through practice |

> "Saddhā is not sectarian faith. It is verified confidence. You come with open mind, you try, you verify. The confidence that grows from your own experience — that is saddhā." — Goenka, Day 7

## Its Dark Side

Without paññā (wisdom/vision), saddhā degenerates into blind devotion, superstition, or fanaticism. Goenka uses the metaphor of **mắt và chân** (eyes and feet):
- **Paññā** = eyes (see where you're going)
- **Saddhā** = feet (ability to walk)

Without eyes, even strong legs will walk off a cliff. Without feet, even clear vision takes you nowhere.

## The Five Strengths (Pañca Bala)

1. **Saddhā** — Enlightened faith
2. **Viriya** — Energy/effort
3. **Sati** — Mindfulness/awareness
4. **Samādhi** — Concentration
5. **Paññā** — Wisdom
