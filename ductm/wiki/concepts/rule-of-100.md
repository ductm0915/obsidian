# Rule of 100

## Definition
**100 primary actions, sustained for 100 days, guarantees your first result in any new channel.**

"Primary actions" = the core revenue-generating behavior for your chosen channel:
- Content: 100 pieces/day (or 100 minutes of content/day)
- Cold outreach: 100 DMs/emails/calls per day
- Paid ads: $100/day in spend
- Warm outreach: 100 personal contacts per day

## Why It Works
When you take 10,000 actions (100 × 100) in one specific direction, results are mathematically inevitable. The issue is that most people quit before hitting a statistically significant sample.

*"If you do the rule of 100 for 100 straight days, your business will grow."* Most people who fully commit get their first customer by week 3 — but you must commit to 100 days in your heart before you start.

## Feast/Famine Diagnosis
Volatile, unpredictable revenue = a symptom of insufficient volume, not bad luck or inconsistent lead flow. If you're getting 3 customers/month and you're doing sporadic advertising:
- That means 1 customer per every 10 days of random advertising
- If you **deliberately** did in 1 day what you randomly did in 10 days, you'd get a customer daily

Companies doing 30× more revenue are typically doing 30× more advertising.

## The Input vs. Output Distinction
Make inputs (actions) the goal, not outputs (results). At the end of the day: "Did I do my 100 actions? Yes or no." Not "Did I get a sale?" because results lag behind inputs.

This is the key to psychological sustainability: you can control inputs, not outputs. Measuring inputs = measurable progress every day regardless of revenue.

## Application Levels
| Stage | Application |
|---|---|
| Launch (first acquisition channel) | 100 actions × 100 days = first result |
| Expansion (second channel) | Start Rule of 100 again for new channel |
| Scale (existing channel) | Measure how many "hundreds" per day — Hormozi's team does ~450 pieces of content/week |

## Connection to Frustration Tolerance
Rule of 100 is [[frustration-tolerance]] made structural. By committing to 100 days upfront, you take the "how long do I do this?" question off the table. You don't negotiate with yourself each day.
