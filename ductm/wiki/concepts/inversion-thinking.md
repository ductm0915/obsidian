# Inversion Thinking

**Inversion thinking** is a problem-solving method attributed to Charlie Munger (who credited it to Einstein and algebraist Carl Jacobi): instead of asking "how do I achieve X?", ask "how would I guarantee the failure/destruction of X?" Then invert the answers.

> "Invert, always invert." — Charlie Munger

## Why It Works

Humans are neurologically wired to detect threats far more effectively than opportunities. Threat detection is a stronger cognitive engine than positive generation.

Inversion *exploits this asymmetry*:
- "Tell me 10 things you're grateful for" → hard
- "Tell me 10 problems you have" → easy — rattle off 100

By asking the "destroy" question first, you get **completely different and more vivid answers** than asking the "build" question directly. The inversion then converts those answers into an action plan.

## The Method

1. State the goal clearly (e.g., "grow my business")
2. Ask: "If I wanted to destroy this in as few moves as possible, what would I do?"
3. List specific sabotage actions (make products terrible, hide from market, price stupidly, etc.)
4. Invert each item → your positive action plan

The inverted list often reveals threats and blind spots that direct positive thinking misses entirely.

## Applications

**Business:**
- "How would I destroy my marketing?" → inverted: what great marketing looks like
- "How would I guarantee customer churn?" → inverted: retention best practices

**Personal life:**
- "How would I ruin my health?" → inverted: health maintenance
- "How would I destroy a relationship?" → inverted: relationship care

**Risk management:**
- Pre-mortem: "Assume the project failed. What killed it?" → then fix those things before starting

## Historical Lineage

- **Carl Jacobi** (algebraist): "Invert, always invert" — original source Munger cited
- **Albert Einstein:** Applied inversion to physics problems
- **Charlie Munger:** Applied to investing, business, personal decisions; popularized via Berkshire letters and speeches
- **Alex Hormozi:** Applied throughout 8 Laws video as the structural frame for presenting all 8 principles
