# 1 Avatar + 1 Product + 1 Channel

## Definition
The simplest formula for reaching $1M–$2M+ in annual revenue: serve **one specific person** with **one product** through **one advertising channel**, and go as deep as possible before adding complexity.

## The Three Constraints

### 1 Avatar
One very specific person you are going to help. The more specific the better. Not "small business owners" — "fitness coaches who want to fill their first 10 clients." Clarity on the avatar determines everything downstream (offer, messaging, channel).

### 1 Product
Don't add products early. Under $1M, you don't have enough customers to justify the complexity. Instead: *"How can I sell 3× as many of the same product to the same person?"*

Adding a second product before the first is maxed out = adding complexity without foundations. You end up with two underdeveloped products instead of one dominant one.

### 1 Channel
Pick one acquisition channel and max it out before adding a second:
- **Content:** Keep posting on the same platform; improve quality and quantity.
- **Paid ads:** Just spend more if profitable; no need for a second channel.
- **Outreach:** Just do more outreach to more of the same type of person.

Switching or adding channels before you've hit the ceiling of your current one = premature complexity.

## When This Breaks Down
At $1M+, adding a second channel makes sense as insurance and growth. But if you can't get to $1M profitably on one channel, you have a business or pricing problem — not a channel problem.

The myth: "I need 7 income streams." Reality: the wealthy built those streams sequentially (one → max → overflow → next), not simultaneously.

## Connection to the Millionaire Roadmap
This formula represents the "nail it" before "scale it" phase. Once these three are locked:
- Product-market fit established
- Unit economics proven
- Repeatable acquisition process documented

Then and only then add: second channel, second product, second avatar.
