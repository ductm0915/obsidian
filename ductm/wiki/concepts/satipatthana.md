# Satipaṭṭhāna — Four Foundations of Mindfulness

Satipaṭṭhāna (Pali: sati = mindfulness, paṭṭhāna = foundation/establishment) refers to the four foundations of mindfulness taught by the Buddha in the Satipaṭṭhāna Sutta: (1) kāyānupassanā — observation of the body, (2) vedanānupassanā — observation of sensations/feeling tones, (3) cittānupassanā — observation of mind states, (4) dhammānupassanā — observation of mental objects. In Goenka's teaching, vedanānupassanā (sensation observation) is the central or "gateway" foundation — it is the junction point in Dependent Origination where liberation becomes possible.
