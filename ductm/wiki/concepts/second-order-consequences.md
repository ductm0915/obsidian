# Second-Order Consequences

The chain of effects that unfolds after any decision, beyond the immediately visible first-order consequence. Almost all business and life failures are caused by people who only considered first-order effects.

---

## The Framework

Every decision triggers a chain:
- **First-order consequence** — immediately visible, easy to see
- **Second-order consequence** — what first-order creates
- **Third-order consequence** — what second-order creates
- **Nth-order consequence** — the chain continues to infinity

Most people stop at first-order. Billionaires think to 3rd, 4th, and beyond.

---

## The Valeant Case Study (Sam Ovens)

The defining example from Sam's corpus:

| Order | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| 1st | Cut all R&D → save 40% on expenses → profits explode |
| 2nd | No new drugs in pipeline |
| 3rd | Competitors with R&D release better drugs |
| 4th | Valeant loses customers to superior competitors |
| 5th | Revenue collapses → bankruptcy |

The CEO saw only the 1st order. Investors cheered. Within years, the whole edifice collapsed.

---

## The Personal Application

Buying a 42-inch TV on debt (Sam's recurring example):

| Order | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| 1st | Better entertainment today |
| 2nd | Watching TV → wasting time |
| 3rd | Training brain to prefer laziness when faced with hard choices |
| 4th | Pattern of lazy behavior becomes default mode |
| 5th | All future high-stakes decisions route through this lazy filter |

---

## Michael Jordan Counterexample

Jordan recognized that fame, sponsorships, and celebrity appearances were second-order consequences of being the best player. He canceled all of them to focus on the first-order thing (basketball). His second-order things returned at a higher level.

**Rule:** Never let second-order consequences distract you from the first-order thing that generates them.

---

## The Effect Horizon (Sam's visual tool)

Map any decision on a 2-axis chart:
- X-axis: Time (now → years out)
- Y-axis: Upside/Downside

Two options are almost always mirror images:
- **Option A (wheelchair path):** Immediate upside, long-term downside
- **Option B (the hill):** Short-term downside, long-term upside

The feedback loops at each node amplify the divergence exponentially.
