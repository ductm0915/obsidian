# Sammā Samādhi (Right Concentration)

**Sammā Samādhi** is the eighth factor of the [[noble-eightfold-path]] — "Right Concentration." In the Vipassana tradition, it is specifically defined as concentration developed in the service of liberation (paññā), not as an end in itself.

## The Critical Distinction

Goenka emphasizes that not all samādhi is sammā samādhi:

| Wrong Samādhi | Sammā Samādhi |
|---------------|---------------|
| Concentration for pleasure/bliss | Concentration for insight |
| Withdrawal from reality | Penetrating reality |
| Creates attachment to states | Develops equanimity |
| Used in most concentration traditions | Used in Vipassana |

> "If you develop samādhi to enjoy pleasant sensations, you are violating sammā samādhi. The purpose is to sharpen the mind for seeing the truth of impermanence, suffering, and no-self." — Goenka (Day 2 paraphrase)

## How It's Developed in Vipassana

1. **Anapana** (Days 1–3) — Develops initial concentration through breath observation
2. **Vipassana** (Days 4–10) — Uses that concentration to observe body sensations with equanimity
3. The combination creates sammā samādhi: mind fixed, clear, and equanimous — ready for insight

## Connection to the Three Pillars

In the Sīla → Samādhi → Paññā structure:
- Sīla (morality) → creates the conditions for
- Samādhi (right concentration) → which is the tool for
- Paññā (wisdom/insight) → liberation
