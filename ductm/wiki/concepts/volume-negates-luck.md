# Volume Negates Luck

## Definition
The principle that engaging in an extreme, overwhelming amount of repetition eliminates the variability of "luck" or initial "talent" in achieving an outcome. 

## Application
Coined frequently in business and famously emphasized by [[alex-hormozi]] in go-to-market operations. 
In sales, it implies that forcing yourself or your team into a massive funnel of leads and reps guarantees an eventual statistical success. The more you do, the better you get (skill acceleration); the better you get, the more efficiently you close.

Rather than looking to score the perfect lead, an practitioner of this concept welcomes all leads ("be a trashman") simply to acquire the repetitions necessary for mastery.

## Consistency Beats Talent

From [[hormozi-brutally-honest-truths]]: you can become competent at nearly any skill in **20 hours**. Most people wait 10 years to log the first 20 hours.

> *"What makes things hard isn't complexity, it's consistency."*

The constraint isn't the learning curve — it's the willingness to keep doing it before seeing results.

> *"The world belongs to those who can keep doing without seeing the result of their doing."*

The 20-hour competence threshold applies across domains (guitar basics, public speaking, outreach). What varies is whether you compress those hours into days/weeks or spread them across years.

### Habituation as the Confidence Mechanism

Self-doubt is not overcome through affirmations — it's overcome through **habituation**: repeat exposure until the nervous system adapts and the fear response diminishes.

> *"Outwork your self-doubt through repetition, not affirmations."*

Hormozi example: years of leading gym fitness sessions (speaking into a microphone in front of people, multiple times per day) meant that when he first got on stage, he wasn't nervous — he'd already habituated to the stimulus. Domain-specific reps, not generic confidence-building.

This connects to [[learning-intelligence-confidence]]: confidence comes from past proof in the specific domain. You can't borrow confidence from adjacent activities.

### Reps Can Be Compressed

The number of required reps is roughly fixed. What varies is the *time* it takes to accumulate them. High-frequency practice compresses the timeline dramatically: "Let's say it takes this many reps. You can have this many reps take you a year or you can have it take you a month depending on how dedicated you are."
