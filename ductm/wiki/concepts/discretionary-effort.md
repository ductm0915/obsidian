# Discretionary Effort

Everything above the minimum required to not get fired. In most companies, there is a massive unlockable gap between "doing enough to keep my job" and peak output. People can triple or quadruple their output — but **only if incentivized**.

## The Unlocking Mechanism
Discretionary effort is released when:
1. **Incentives are aligned with output** — pay for performance, not politics
2. **Technical tracks exist** alongside management tracks — so experts don't have to become managers to advance
3. **The culture is self-policing** — high performers naturally push out underperformers

## Perverse Incentives That Kill Discretionary Effort
When the only way to advance is by growing headcount underneath you:
1. People keep secrets (teaching others = creating competitors)
2. Everyone lobbies to hire more (more people = higher rank)
3. Promotion gatekeepers gain disproportionate political power
4. People **show** work rather than **do** work

## Pay for Performance — Implementation
1. **Transition:** Existing employees keep salaries + add performance bonuses on top
2. **New hires:** Build performance into comp from day one
3. **Validation:** Back-test the new comp model against last month's data
4. **Simplicity:** Even flat bonuses by tier can unlock significant effort

## Key Insight
> Fire when things are good, not when things are bad. Good times = most bloat. Pruning during growth feels disciplined; pruning during decline feels desperate.
