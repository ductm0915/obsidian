# Offer Stack Model

[[matt-gray]]'s framework for designing a tiered product line that serves customers at every stage — from beginner to 8-figure scaling — while maximizing LTV through upsell pathways.

Core principle: **"You always want to be in the money."** No matter where a customer is in their journey, you have something valuable to sell them.

---

## The 3 Tiers

| Tier | Price Range | Primary Role |
|---|---|---|
| Entry Level | $29–$300 | Taste; lead generation & nurture pipeline |
| Core | $7.8K–$36K | Main community + guided implementation |
| High-Ticket | $100K–$250K | Intimate proximity; done-with-you transformation |

### Entry Level

Low-price products launched ~every 3 months. Not primarily for revenue — they serve as **lead generation**: buyers enter the ecosystem and get nurtured toward higher-ticket offers over the following year.

### Core

The primary revenue engine. Examples at Founder OS: "Program" ($7.8K, 30 founders/month) and "Velocity" ($36K, 15 founders/month). Access to Matt's team + systems + community.

### High-Ticket

Maximum intimacy and proximity. At Founder OS: "Edge" ($100K–$250K, 3 founders/month). Weekly sessions with Matt + C-suite, text access, in-person availability. Only for founders doing $1M+/year.

---

## The 4 Levers

Every tier is calibrated using 4 design variables. The key difference between tiers is *how much* of each lever is available:

1. **Education** — Systems, modules, frameworks (e.g., 180+ systems at Founder OS)
2. **Support** — Human team support; dedicated founder success managers; live sessions (50+/month)
3. **Proximity** — Direct access to the founder: WhatsApp, weekly 1:1s, in-person sessions
4. **Community** — Peer network; platform (Slack, Circle, Mighty Networks); in-person retreats at higher tiers

### Lever Distribution by Tier

| Lever | Entry | Core | High-Ticket |
|---|---|---|---|
| Education | Modules only | Full systems library | Full + custom work |
| Support | None / self-serve | Success managers + live sessions | C-suite + founder direct |
| Proximity | None | 1 session/month with founder | Weekly + text access |
| Community | None | Online community | Online + in-person retreats |

---

## The Upsell Mechanic

Solving one problem surfaces the next. Customers naturally graduate up the stack as their business grows. The founder becomes the guide for the entire entrepreneurial journey — from $0 to $100M+.

> "As you solve one problem, a new problem presents itself. Because the journey of building a business is just this endless journey of solving things."

This means the offer stack isn't just revenue architecture — it's a **retention and ascension system**.

---

## Lifetime Value Math (Illustrative)

A customer enters at $29 entry-level → after experiencing results, joins Program at $7.8K → achieves milestones, upgrades to Velocity at $36K → hits $1M revenue, joins Edge at $100K+.

Full journey LTV: $143,829 from one acquisition.

---

## Connection to Application Router

The [[application-router]] ensures prospects are routed to the right tier at first contact — not oversold into an offer too advanced for their stage, and not undersold into an offer below their capacity.

---

## Contrast with Other Offer Frameworks

- vs. [[value-equation]] (Hormozi): Gray's stack explicitly uses *community* and *proximity* as levers, not just dream outcome / effort / time — he's designing access and relationships as products
- vs. [[grand-slam-offer]]: A Grand Slam Offer is a single irresistible offer; the Offer Stack Model is the architecture of *multiple* GSOs serving different customer segments
