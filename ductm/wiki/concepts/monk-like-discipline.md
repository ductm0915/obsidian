# Monk-Like Discipline

Sam Ovens' term for the level of self-discipline required to achieve meaningful success. Not motivational discipline — systematic, routinized, environment-enforced control over impulses and attention.

The word "monk" is intentional: monks don't rely on motivation. They have rituals, structures, rules, and environments built over years that make the right behavior automatic.

---

## Motivation vs. Discipline

| Motivation | Discipline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Temporary | Permanent |
| Reactive (emotion-based) | Proactive (system-based) |
| Depletes | Compounds |
| Losers wait for it | Winners don't need it |

"True masters rely on discipline, not motivation. They're not motivated all the time — but they don't stop."

---

## The 11 Practices

1. **Resist impulses** — don't react immediately to cravings (snacks, social media, email)
2. **Long-term thinking in every decision** — always map the effect horizon
3. **Strict routine, fixed daily** — same wake/sleep times, same morning/evening rituals
4. **Clear standards** — define what good behavior looks like and stick to it regardless of mood
5. **True sacrifice** — say no to things you genuinely want that conflict with the goal
6. **Environment engineering** — remove temptations before you need willpower to resist them
7. **Visualization and planning** — keep long-term goals vivid through daily review
8. **Health tracking** — Oura Ring for sleep, metrics for nutrition and exercise
9. **One habit at a time** — don't stack new habits; build one to mastery first
10. **Vision-to-action review** — every night: vision → annual → quarterly → initiatives → actions
11. **Accountability structure** — trainer at door, chef prepared, systems that enforce behavior

---

## The Environment Principle

Willpower is finite. Environment is not. Design the environment so the default path = the right path. Sam's examples:
- Remove all TVs from house (ratchet and socket set on day 2 in LA)
- Put phone in drawer on airplane mode during work
- Build gym in garage ($5K) to eliminate 30-min daily commute to external gym
- Hire chef so food decisions are eliminated entirely

"Like snakes and ladders — just get rid of the damn snakes."

---

## Instant Gratification as Addiction

Structurally identical to chemical addiction:
- Social media likes → dopamine spike → need more → compulsive return
- Sugar, alcohol, Netflix → same loop
- Training yourself on instant gratification = becoming unable to do hard things later

The inverse: train on delayed gratification. The marshmallow experiment showed that children who waited for 2 marshmallows dramatically outperformed their peers across every life metric tracked over decades.
