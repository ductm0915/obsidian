# Signal vs. Noise (Content)

The distinction between content that **converts dream customers** (signal) and content that **generates vanity metrics** (noise). Optimizing for the wrong metric — likes, comments, engagement — leads to a content strategy that attracts the wrong people or no one who will buy.

Coined in this context by [[matt-gray]] in his Beautiful Business framework.

---

## The Problem

When you're too focused on platform-surfaced metrics — likes, engagement, comments — you can quickly end up playing a game of making "viral noise" instead of creating content that actually attracts your dream customer.

High-engagement content ≠ high-converting content.

---

## The Signal Definition

Signal content = content that **reads the minds of your dream customers**. The goal is to make prospects say:

> "Oh my god, I've been waiting for someone to say this."

This content may not get the most views or engagement. But it will drive the most people to your actual product or service.

---

## How to Generate Signal

The technique is **direct customer interviews**. Talk to your most valuable existing customers — the kind of people you want to attract more of. Ask:

1. **"What's your biggest goal right now?"** — Reveals their *heaven* (desired outcome)
2. **"What's currently keeping you up until 2am?"** — Reveals their *hell* (avoided pain)
3. **"If I could wave a magic wand, what's the one thing you'd most want my help with?"** — Reveals the **exact gap** where you have leverage

The answers give you a map of your audience's heaven and hell. Build content strategy around that map.

## Contrast

| Signal | Noise |
|---|---|
| Solves a specific, felt pain | Generic, broadly appealing |
| Attracts dream customers | Attracts everyone (and no one) |
| Low views possible, high conversion | High views possible, low conversion |
| Based on customer interviews | Based on platform trends |
| "I've been waiting for someone to say this" | "I saw this everywhere this week" |
