# Learning, Intelligence, and Confidence — Hormozi Framework

## Three Definitions

### Learning = Same Condition, New Behavior
Learning has occurred when, presented with the same situation, you respond differently than before.
- Phone rings → no script → phone rings → no script → **phone rings → reads script** ✓ = learned
- If you attend training and return to the same environment doing the same thing: you did not learn.

### Intelligence = Rate of Learning
How quickly you change your behavior when the same condition repeats.
- Faster behavioral change under the same conditions = higher intelligence.
- "You may not have ever thought you were smart, but if you change your behavior, you can beat even the smartest person."
- This reframes intelligence as a *choice* to adapt, not a fixed trait.

### Confidence = Past Proof, Domain-Specific
Statistical confidence: the percentage likelihood that something will happen based on evidence.
- Confidence is built by doing the thing enough times, *in the exact domain*, until you can complete it on autopilot.
- Practicing in a mirror ≠ stage confidence. Domain specificity is everything.
- *"Confidence comes from the past, not the present."*
- Implication: if your plan requires *current* confidence you don't have, it's a bad plan. Build confidence before you need it (dig the well before you're thirsty).

## How They Connect
1. **Operationalize** the skill (see [[operationalize-traits]])
2. **Practice** in the exact domain until the condition triggers automatic correct response = building learning
3. Repeat until bored and automatic = building confidence
4. Change behavior faster each time = building intelligence

## Practical Implications
- "Count how many times you've done the thing in the specific situation" → that number predicts your confidence level.
- To improve confidence: do the thing more times in the exact conditions you'll need it.
- To improve intelligence: actively try new approaches the moment the same bad result appears.
- Traits described as "born with" are really just high rep counts accumulated early in life.

## Key Quotes
- *"Same condition, new behavior = learning."*
- *"Intelligence = rate of learning = how quickly you change your behavior under the same conditions."*
- *"Confidence comes from the past, not the present. If your plan requires luck, it's a bad plan."*
