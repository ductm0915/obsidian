# Entropy Principle

## Definition

The application of thermodynamic entropy to personal and business life: **without active order-creation, systems naturally trend toward disorder and chaos.** Used by [[dan-koe]] as a foundational principle explaining why discipline, vision, and routine are not optional — they are the only thing standing between you and a declining life.

> "That's what entropy does. That is the supreme law of the universe. If you do not create order for your life, you will decline into chaos."
> — [[dan-koe]]

## The Core Idea

In thermodynamics, entropy measures disorder in a closed system — systems tend toward maximum entropy (disorder) unless energy is added. Dan Koe applies this metaphorically to human life:

- **Default state = decline.** Without deliberate action, your health, relationships, finances, and focus deteriorate.
- **Order must be created, not maintained.** You don't "keep" a good life; you continuously generate it.
- **Mindless action = accelerated entropy.** Passive living (not making decisions, following social defaults) is not neutral — it actively accelerates decline into the [[anti-vision]].

## Practical Implications

1. **Vision + strategy as anti-entropy tools.** A clear life vision with a daily system is the mechanism that counteracts entropy. Without both, even good intentions decay.
2. **"Disappear" as entropy management.** Cutting distracting people, apps, and habits is removing entropy sources from your personal system.
3. **Boring fundamentals are load-bearing.** Repetitive daily actions (writing, training, outreach) aren't just habit — they are the continuous energy input required to maintain order.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

- **[[anti-vision]]** — Making entropy's implied outcome *personal and vivid* to generate the drive to resist it.
- **[[frustration-tolerance]]** — The capacity to sustain the energy input required to fight entropy long-term.
- **[[my-fault-philosophy]]** — Radical accountability for entropy in one's own life; no external forces are to blame for disorder.
- **[[six-horsemen-stagnation]]** — The specific business-level entropy patterns that cause plateaus.

## Note on Usage

Koe uses "entropy" philosophically and motivationally, not scientifically. The point is not thermodynamics — it's that passivity has a cost, and that cost compounds over time. This is structurally identical to the concept of **[[problems-vs-missed-opportunities]]**: doing nothing isn't neutral.
