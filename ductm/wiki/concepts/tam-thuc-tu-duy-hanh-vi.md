# Tâm Thức → Tư Duy → Hành Vi (Consciousness → Thinking → Behavior)

**Origin:** [[wit-organization]], taught by [[hong-trieu-bao]]
**Core function:** The primary causality model explaining why change must start from consciousness, not behavior

---

## Summary

The "Tâm Thức → Tư Duy → Hành Vi" chain is WIT's foundational model of human causality. It states that a person's inner consciousness (tâm thức) determines their thinking (tư duy), which in turn determines their behavior (hành vi), which produces outcomes (nhân quả — cause and result).

This reverses the typical self-improvement approach, which targets behavior directly. WIT's position: behavioral change without consciousness change is unstable, temporary, and exhausting.

Introduced on Day 1 of [[wit-k07-tu-duy]] and woven through every subsequent module.

---

## The Three Components

### Tâm Thức (Inner Consciousness)

Tâm thức is the awareness of one's inner world. It is distinct from:
- **Tiềm thức** (subconscious): already-imprinted images and concepts, without an active state component
- **Nhận thức** (awareness in general): applies to external world perception

Tâm thức has three components:
1. **Hình ảnh** (mental images) — the pictures in mind associated with people, topics, situations
2. **Khái niệm nguồn** (source concepts) — the deep mental frameworks/definitions one holds
3. **Trạng thái nội tâm** (internal state) — the electromagnetic quality of the inner field (dương/positive or âm/negative)

Tâm thức is the only component one can directly manage. One cannot directly manage the subconscious or change behavior through will alone, but one can work with inner state, images, and concepts.

### Tư Duy (Thinking)

Tư duy flows from tâm thức. It is not purely rational or cognitive; it is the totality of how the mind processes and responds. In WIT's definition, thinking is: "a process of imprinting (huân tập) through hearing, seeing, speaking, and knowing, passing through the layers of human character (tánh) and emotion (tình)."

When tâm thức is negative, thinking will be negative — even if the person "tries" to think positively. This is why the dictum "giận quá mất khôn" (extreme anger loses wisdom) is true: anger (negative tâm thức) shuts down access to good knowledge.

When tâm thức is positive and clear, thinking naturally orients correctly without effort.

### Hành Vi (Behavior)

Behavior is the expression of tư duy in action. When the preceding two layers are correct, behavior corrects itself. The teacher's metaphor: "tư duy mà không tư duy" — thinking without thinking — the behavior just emerges correctly because the source is right.

## The Ship Analogy (Hình Tượng Con Thuyền)

Introduced in [[wit-k07-tu-duy]] Session 1, this is WIT's signature teaching metaphor:

- The ship's hull = tâm thức (the containing structure, the foundation)
- The rudder/direction = tư duy (how you steer / think)
- The movement through water = hành vi (what you actually do)

A ship with a damaged hull cannot be steered properly regardless of rudder skill. A ship pointed in the wrong direction will go the wrong way regardless of engine power. Only when hull (tâm thức) is sound and direction (tư duy) is correct does movement (hành vi) produce arrival at the destination.

## Practical Implications

### Why behavior change fails

Most people try to change hành vi directly (set rules, use willpower, form habits through repetition). This fails because:
- The tâm thức has not changed, so it continues generating the old tư duy
- Old tư duy produces old hành vi
- Change requires force and attention to maintain — exhausting
- When attention lapses, old behavior returns

### The WIT approach

1. Identify the topic or domain where results are undesirable
2. Diagnose the tâm thức: what is the internal state, image, and source concept held about this topic? (Apply the [[nhan-thuc-du-day]] diagnostic)
3. Work directly with tâm thức: change the internal state (through tâm thái — joyfulness, magnanimity, gratitude), update the mental images, replace incorrect source concepts with correct ones
4. Tư duy corrects automatically
5. Hành vi corrects automatically
6. Results (nhân quả) change

This is summarized: *"Muốn quản trị nhân quả trong cuộc sống thì cần quản trị tâm thức, tư duy và hành vi. Chốt lại là làm sao để quản trị được tâm thức là quan trọng nhất."* (To manage cause-and-effect in life, manage consciousness, thinking, and behavior. Bottom line: managing consciousness is most important.)

## Managing Tâm Thức Through Nguyên Lý (Principles)

From [[wit-k07-tu-duy]] Session 10: The method for managing tâm thức is not through direct effort ("I will feel positive") but through consistently following the nguyên lý (principles). The seven principles taught in the Tư Duy module automatically orient tâm thức correctly when followed. Laws and principles are like the river current — flow with them and tâm thức naturally settles into a correct state.

## The Three Mental States That Drive the System

From [[wit-k07-tu-duy]] Session 11, three tâm thái (mental states) are the "play buttons" that activate different aspects of tâm thức:

- **An vui** (joyfulness/equanimity) — activates tổng nghiệp thức (accumulated wisdom). Enables clarity, insight, and the "natural knowing" (tự nhiên biết) intelligence.
- **Bao dung** (magnanimity/tolerance) — activates tổng nghiệp duyên (accumulated relational conditions). Enables good relationships to emerge and be maintained.
- **Trân trọng - Biết ơn** (appreciation-gratitude) — activates tổng nghiệp quả (accumulated outcomes). Draws desired results into manifestation.

The critical rule: when internal state is negative (điện từ âm), stop all output. Do not think, speak, decide, or act. *"Không suy nghĩ, không nói, không làm, không ra quyết định gì hết."* Everything produced from a negative state will be negative. Wait until state returns to positive before resuming.
