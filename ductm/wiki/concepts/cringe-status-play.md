# Cringe as a Status Play

**Cringe** (in the context of secondhand embarrassment) is frequently used defensively by peers as a status play, rather than a genuine expression of empathetic embarrassment.

When someone labels early attempts at content creation, business, or passion projects as "cringe," they are reacting to a perceived shift in status. The person trying something new is attempting to change their status relative to their peers. Calling it "cringe" is a societal mechanism to pull them back to the baseline.

## Why Caring is Cringe

By definition, taking anything extremely seriously or putting in unguarded effort can be framed as "cringe" by observers. Because "cringe" is weaponized to maintain the status quo, the fear of looking cringe is the greatest barrier to starting.
