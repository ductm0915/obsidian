# CLOSER Framework

## Definition
A step-by-step sales script acronym by [[alex-hormozi]] for structuring any sales call, B2C or B2B, from $500 to $100,000+ tickets. The framework reflects a diagnostic rather than transactional sales approach.

## The Acronym

| Letter | Step | Purpose |
|--------|------|---------|
| **C** — Clarify | Why are they on the call? What's the problem? | Surface the real goal, not the stated one |
| **L** — Label | Restate their problem back to them | They must own/acknowledge the problem |
| **O** — Overview | What have they tried before? | Exhausts past solutions; generates ammo for close |
| **S** — Sell the vacation | Pitch using 3 anecdotal stories (not features) | Paint the destination, not the flight |
| **E** — Explain away concerns | Handle objections; loop until resolved | Use [[aaa-method]] and key stories |
| **R** — Reinforce | Celebrate the decision post-close | Reduce buyer's remorse; cement commitment |

## How It Works

**C — Clarify**: "What made you reach out today? What's the goal? Why is that important?"  
Probe past "I just wanted more info" — find the real pain.

**L — Label**: "So if I'm hearing you right, you're struggling with X — does that sound about right?"  
They must say yes to their own problem. You can't cure cancer until the patient admits they have it.

**O — Overview the past pain**: "What have you tried so far? How'd that work for you? What else?"  
Run the "pain cycle" until all prior attempts are exhausted. Three outcomes: (a) they failed because they lacked your product's element; (b) they succeeded elsewhere — disqualify or adjust; (c) generates ammo to reframe in the close.

**S — Sell the vacation**: Use three anecdotal stories (one per pillar of your solution) to explain **why** each element matters — not *what* it is. See [[sell-the-vacation]].  
- Only three things come out of your mouth on a call: questions, restatements, anecdotal stories.
- The pitch should be ≤3 minutes. "They're buying because of how understood they feel."

**E — Explain away concerns**: When they say no, resolve the concern and ask again (looping). Handle in order: Price → Decision maker → Avoidance. See [[aaa-method]] and [[blame-distortions]].

**R — Reinforce**: Personalized video/message from leadership. Customer decides in first 48 hours whether they made the right choice. The sale continues through onboarding.

## Key Insight
> "On a sales call, only three things should ever come out of your mouth: questions, restatements, anecdotal stories."

The prospect's logical brain gets words; their emotional brain (the one making the decision) gets tone and feeling of being understood.
