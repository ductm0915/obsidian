# Body Scan Technique (Vipassana)

The **body scan** is the core physical technique of Vipassana practice, introduced on Day 4. The meditator systematically moves attention from head to feet (and back), observing sensations at each point — without reacting with craving or aversion.

## Technical Procedure

1. Begin at the top of the head (crown)
2. Move attention slowly downward — face, neck, shoulders, arms, chest, abdomen, back, legs, feet
3. At each point: simply observe whatever sensation is present (or absent)
4. If no sensation: rest attention there for ~1 minute, then continue
5. Do not create, intensify, or suppress sensations — only observe

## Two Modes

- **Part by part** (for beginners / gross sensations): scan area by area, dwelling where sensations are strong
- **Free flow** (for experienced / subtle vibrations): sensations experienced simultaneously across entire body as a field of vibrations

Progression from gross → subtle sensations marks deepening practice.
