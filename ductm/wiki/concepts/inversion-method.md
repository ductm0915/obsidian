# Inversion Method

## Definition
A problem-solving technique: instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how do I *guarantee* failure?" The brain is naturally better at finding problems and threats than at generating solutions. Inversion exploits this bias productively.

**Process:**
1. Imagine the least successful version of yourself.
2. What does that person do (or not do) to guarantee failure?
3. Make the list as specific and concrete as possible.
4. Flip the list into a winning action set.
5. [[operationalize-traits|Operationalize]] each item into specific observable behaviors.

## Origins
- Attributed to Albert Einstein; popularized in Hormozi's context by Charlie Munger: *"Invert, always invert."*
- Used whenever facing a complex growth or performance problem.

## Why It Works
- The brain has stronger threat-detection than opportunity-detection (evolutionary survival mechanism).
- Anxiety-driven problem-finding can be redirected into productive output.
- People already *know* what failure looks like from personal experience — this knowledge is more reliable than vague ideas of success.

## Example Application (Sales/Real Estate)
Failure list: "Don't follow up, don't advertise, don't ask for referrals, show up late, be unprepared, be transactional."
Win list (inverted): "Always follow up, advertise consistently, always ask for referrals, always on time, always over-prepared, always relational."

## Connection to Hormozi's Sales System
The smallest list of failure-guarantors is most powerful. If there's one thing that would guarantee you fail, that single item becomes the north star of how to win.
