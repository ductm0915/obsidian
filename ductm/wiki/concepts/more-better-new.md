# More, Better, New

## Definition
The sequential framework for scaling practically any business operation—especially advertising and marketing—popularized by [[alex-hormozi]]. 

## The Framework Steps
1. **More (Volume):** When starting out, the answer is almost always to simply do *more* of what generates output. [[volume-negates-luck]]. Most businesses fail to scale because they dramatically underestimate the sheer volume of output (content, ads, cold calls) required. Until you are maxing out your distribution channels, focus on "More."
2. **Better (Optimization):** You shift to "Better" when the return on effort for optimizing a process exceeds the return of adding one incremental unit of "More". For example, if you have 10 sales reps, increasing their conversion rate by 20% mathematically beats hiring an 11th rep. 
3. **New (Expansion):** Try something new *only* when you cannot do any "More" and you've hit diminishing returns on getting "Better." Establishing a new channel (e.g., trying YouTube ads when Facebook is saturated) involves guaranteed setup costs but unproven returns. 

## Application
Entrepreneurs often reverse this order. They try 80% new things because they crave novelty, skipping the boring work of doing 10x more of what is nominally working. Master the boring work first.
