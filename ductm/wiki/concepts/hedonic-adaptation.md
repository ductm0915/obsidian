# Hedonic Adaptation

**Hedonic adaptation** (also: hedonic treadmill) is the psychological phenomenon where humans quickly return to a baseline level of happiness after positive or negative life changes.

In Vipassana terms, this is the direct result of **tanha** (craving): we pursue an object, obtain it, experience a brief pleasure, then adapt — and must pursue the next object. The craving is never permanently satisfied.

Goenka's teaching: the solution is not to get better objects but to observe the sensation of craving arise and pass — experiencing its truly impermanent nature (anicca).
