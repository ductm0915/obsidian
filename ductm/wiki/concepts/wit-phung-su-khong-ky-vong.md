# Phụng Sự Không Kỳ Vọng (Service Without Expectation)

**Phụng Sự Không Kỳ Vọng** is one of WIT's core leadership virtues: the practice of serving others (community, family, organization) without any expectation of return, recognition, or reward.

## Core Principle

This is not naïve selflessness — it is a sophisticated understanding that:
1. Expectation creates attachment → attachment creates suffering when unmet
2. Pure service creates clean karma — action without the contaminating energy of "what's in it for me"
3. The one who serves freely accumulates the highest form of "social capital" — genuine trust

## Connection to Vipassana

Goenka uses an identical concept as one of the **two yardsticks of Dhamma progress** (Day 10):

> "If you feel a natural desire to serve others without expectation of reward — that is a sign Dhamma is working."

## WIT Application

In WIT leadership contexts, this manifests as:
- Giving advice/mentorship without tracking who owes you
- Contributing to community without scorekeeping
- Making decisions based on "what serves" not "what returns"
