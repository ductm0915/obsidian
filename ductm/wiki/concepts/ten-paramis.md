# Ten Pāramīs (Ten Perfections)

The **Ten Pāramīs** (Pāli: pāramī = perfection, fulfillment) are ten virtues/qualities that, when cultivated to completion, culminate in full liberation (nibbāna). They represent the "outer" path that complements the "inner" path of meditation.

## The Ten Perfections

1. **Dāna** — Generosity, giving
2. **Sīla** — Morality, ethical conduct
3. **Nekkhamma** — Renunciation, letting go of craving
4. **Paññā** — Wisdom, discernment
5. **Viriya** — Energy, effort, persistence
6. **Khantī** — Patience, tolerance, forbearance
7. **Sacca** — Truthfulness, authenticity
8. **Adhiṭṭhāna** — Determination, strong resolve
9. **Mettā** — Loving-kindness, goodwill
10. **Upekkhā** — Equanimity, composure

## Goenka's Usage (Day 10)

On Day 10, Goenka presents the ten pāramīs as the "full-spectrum" of what a practitioner develops through consistent Vipassana practice. They are not morality rules but natural qualities that emerge as the mind purifies.

The two yardsticks of progress Goenka offers:
1. Desire to serve others without expectation (Dāna + Mettā)
2. Feeling genuine gratitude (Sacca + Upekkhā)

## Connection to Vipassana

Each sitting develops multiple pāramīs simultaneously:
- Sitting through pain = Khantī + Viriya
- Observing without reacting = Nekkhamma + Upekkhā
- Continuing daily practice = Adhiṭṭhāna
