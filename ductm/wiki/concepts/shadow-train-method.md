# Shadow Train Method — 3-Phase Delegation

## What It Is

A structured 3-phase process for transferring ownership of a task from founder/manager to employee. Designed to ensure the handoff is complete — not just delegated in name but genuinely owned.

## The 3 Phases

### Phase 1: Shadow Train
The employee *watches you* do the task. They observe the full context, judgment calls, and workflow before attempting it themselves.

### Phase 2: Supervised Execution
You *watch the employee* do it. You're present to catch errors, correct in real time, and provide context they couldn't absorb just by watching.

### Phase 3: Supported Independence
You're **available but not involved.** They own the work. In the early days: more ad hoc calls and consults. Over time, if the employee is competent, those taper off as they internalize the decisions.

**Full handoff is complete** when they own it without needing you — decisions are automated down to them.

## Key Metrics for Knowing It Worked

- Accept 80% as good — sometimes 60% — as long as there's a clear improvement trajectory
- Measure by **rate of improvement**, not starting level. Someone starting at 60% improving fast will pass someone starting at 80% who plateaus. See [[learning-intelligence-confidence]].

## The Belief to Shift

*"No one's going to be able to do it as well as you with one-tenth of the reps. But every human is replaceable... if they learned all the things you learned the right way without learning all the bad mistakes, they might get to 50% as good as you right off the bat."*

Specialization compounds. 80% of your output with 100% of their time beats 100% of your output with a fraction of yours.
