# Kaleidoscope Process

## Definition
A framework for extracting maximum value from a winning advertisement or piece of content by relentlessly iterating and "remixing" it, rather than moving on to create net-new ideas. Concept detailed by [[alex-hormozi]].

## Application
When an ad heavily outperforms the baseline, most marketers say, "That was nice, what's next?" The Kaleidoscope approach dictates that you halt new ideation and aggressively iterate the winner. 

Methods to squeeze a winner include:
1. Changing the background color or physical setting
2. Introducing physical props
3. Re-enacting the exact same ad with different clothes
4. Re-ordering the sequence of the clips
5. Applying visual filters (black & white, high contrast)
6. Adding visual effects, annotations, or lower thirds
7. Changing fonts and caption styling
8. Altering the pacing (Speeding up 1.2x or slowing down)
9. Modifying the background music
10. Using the winning hook with a newly recorded body

The core principle: Assume your audience does not perceive the "remix" as the same ad. A single winning hook/concept can generate 10x-100x more revenue if iterated completely.
