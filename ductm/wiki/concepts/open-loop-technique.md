# Open Loop Technique

A storytelling and content retention mechanism: deliberately opening unresolved questions in the audience's mind and delaying the closure. The brain's discomfort with unfinished business (the Garnic Effect) compels attention until the loop is closed.

---

## The Garnic Effect

The psychological foundation: **the brain genuinely hates unfinished business.** Once a question or unresolved tension is introduced, the nervous system craves closure. The audience literally cannot stop thinking about it until it's resolved.

This is why:
- Cliffhangers work in TV series
- Mystery headlines drive clicks
- Stories with unresolved tension keep people watching

---

## Intrigue vs. Tension

Two distinct mechanisms, applied at different stages:

| Stage | Mechanism | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-content (thumbnail, headline) | **Intrigue** | Drive the click |
| Inside content | **Tension** | Drive retention to the end |

Intrigue asks: *"What is this?"* (curiosity about existence)  
Tension asks: *"What happens next?"* (investment in outcome)

---

## How to Use It

1. **Open a loop early.** State something surprising, incomplete, or paradoxical at the start.
   - Example (Matt Gray): *"If you thought of someone making $730,000/month, how long would you guess they're working? Most would guess 12–14 hours a day. I'm doing the complete opposite."* — answer not given until several minutes in.

2. **Layer multiple loops.** Stack open loops throughout the content, not just at the start. Shitty stories hook at the beginning but fall flat because there are no more curiosity gaps later.

3. **Delay the closure.** Don't answer immediately — hold the tension for minutes, sections, or even episodes.

4. **Close at the right moment.** When you close a loop, the audience feels satisfaction. This is also when you insert the next loop.

---

## Curiosity Gap Phrases

Phrases that open loops mid-content:
- *"You wouldn't believe what happened next."*
- *"This nearly killed my business."*
- *"This was crazy, but nothing yet compared to what happened next."*
- *"There's one thing Kim Kardashian understands that most founders ignore..."*
