# Anusaya Kilesa — Latent Defilements

Anusaya kilesas (Pali: anusaya = latent, kilesa = defilement) are the deepest-level dormant impurities sleeping in the unconscious mind, like seeds or volcanoes. They are activated by contact → perception → sensation → reaction. Surface defilements (at the level of speech/action) can be controlled by sīla; defilements at the level of thought can be managed by samādhi; but only Vipassana's paññā (wisdom at the experiential level) can eradicate anusaya kilesas at their root. This makes Vipassana uniquely liberating, not just suppressive. Related: [[vedana]], [[sankara-mental-volition]], [[raga-dosa-moha]], [[sila-samadhi-panna]]
