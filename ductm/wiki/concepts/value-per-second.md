# Value Per Second

## Definition

**Value per second** = the density of value delivered per unit of time consumed, as opposed to the total *seconds of value* (volume/quantity of content or features).

> "People who are using your services are not full-time using your stuff. Think about value per second, not seconds of value." — [[alex-hormozi]]

## Two Contexts

### 1. Retention / Product Design
Adding more features, more content, more calls rarely improves retention. Users feel they're getting less value because they're using a smaller percentage of what's available.

**Fix:** Find the one thing people stay for → do that one thing extremely well → remove everything else.

### 2. Content / Advertising
Audiences have infinite information available. More content is not valuable. What wins is:
- Distillation: 20-hour book summarized in 10-minute read
- Curation: filtering signal from noise
- Crystallization: compressing maximum insight into minimum time

> "If I have an hour, I would spend 55 minutes on the first 5 seconds." — [[alex-hormozi]]

## Relation to the Attention Economy

In the attention economy, time is the scarcest resource. Value per second directly maps to [[value-equation]]: reducing Time Delay (fast delivery) and Effort (easy consumption) increases perceived value even without changing the Dream Outcome.

## Implications

- Shorter, denser content outperforms longer content at the same quality
- Onboarding should frontload the single highest-value experience
- Product decisions should delete features that dilute value-per-second
- Retention tactic: weekly call + weekly best-of post = maximum value density at minimum time cost
