# Info Business vs. Software Business

A systematic comparison of the two dominant online business models. Per [[sam-ovens]], who ran a $35M info business (consulting.com) before founding [[skool-platform]]: *"The two couldn't be more different — basically the opposite in almost every dynamic."*

---

## The Comparison

| Dimension | Info Business (courses, masterminds) | Software Business (SaaS, platforms) |
|---|---|---|
| **Time to start** | Days to weeks | Months to years |
| **Capital required** | Low — build yourself | High — team + infrastructure |
| **Engineers needed** | None | Core requirement |
| **Revenue timeline** | High-ticket from day 1 | Low revenue for long time |
| **Profitability** | Very fast | Slow |
| **Team size** | Can be solo | Requires whole team |
| **Scale ceiling** | You are the product | Can scale to billions of users |
| **Short-term advantage** | Massive | Minimal |
| **Long-term potential** | Limited | Massive |
| **Lifestyle fit** | Excellent | Demanding |

---

## The Core Insight: Leverage vs. Ceiling

Info business = **selling yourself through leverage** (content, courses, consulting). The leverage is high, but the product *is you*. Revenue ceiling emerges because:
- Your time and energy are finite
- Your personal brand is the product
- Even with employees, growth is constrained by your involvement

Software = **building a scalable system separate from yourself**. Hard at the beginning because:
- Validation takes long
- Everything is expensive
- Nothing works until it does

But once it works, it compounds independently.

> *"Info is way easier at the beginning and real profitable. But it doesn't have that potential. And software is basically the opposite."* — Sam Ovens

---

## Decision Framework: Which to Build?

- **If you want $1M/year profit with minimal employees and good lifestyle** → Info business is the right answer
- **If you want to build something that could be worth billions** → Software; accept the pain upfront
- **Hybrid path** (Sam's actual path): Use info business cash flows to fund software development in parallel; lean down info as software scales

---

## Transition Dynamics

Moving from info → software is not a switch — it's a phased shift:
1. Start the software business early (3–5 years of lead time)
2. Operate info business for cash flow
3. Gradually reduce info complexity (fewer products, leaner team)
4. Exit info fully when software is self-sustaining

The transition costs money (refunds, opportunity cost) but the opportunity cost of staying in info grows as software scales.
