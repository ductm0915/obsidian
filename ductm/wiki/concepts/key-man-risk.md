# Key Man Risk

## Definition

Key man risk is the degree to which a business's value, revenue, or operations depend on a specific individual — usually the founder. When that person leaves, gets sick, or scales back, the business degrades.

## Why It Destroys Wealth

1. **Unsellable:** A business that requires its owner cannot be transferred. No acquirer will pay a multiple for a business that breaks when the owner exits.
2. **Unscalable:** You become the bottleneck. Your capacity limits the business ceiling.
3. **Ego trap:** Founder dependency *feels* good — you feel irreplaceable, needed. But: "You need to become less important to make an important business."

The [[owner-independence-valuation]] framework quantifies the gap: the same $2M profit business is worth $0 (unsellable) vs. $12M depending on whether the owner is required.

## Where Key Man Risk Hides

- **Sales / marketing:** Founder's face drives acquisition. Revenue drops when face is absent.
- **Operations:** Founder makes day-to-day decisions; team can't act without approval.
- **Relationships:** Clients stay because of the founder personally.
- **Judgment calls:** No documented decision trees; everything requires founder input.

## How to Remove It

1. **[[self-inventory-method]]** — map every founder activity, triage with red/yellow/green
2. **[[shadow-train-method]]** — systematically hand tasks off
3. **Decision infrastructure** — if-then trees + spending thresholds + KPI scorecards
4. **Remove from marketing** — install 7 systems that generate marketing without the founder face (testimonials, lifecycle ads, review mining). See [[lifecycle-ads]].
5. **[[lma-test]]** — take 90 days off; fix what breaks; repeat until nothing breaks

## Key Quote

*"If your face gets the customers, you are the key man. And so in order to make an important business, you need to become less important."* — [[alex-hormozi]]
