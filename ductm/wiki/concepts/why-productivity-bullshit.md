# Why Productivity Is Bullshit

**Origin:** [[sam-ovens]]
**Companion piece to:** [[sam-ovens-death-by-1000-cuts]]

## Core Argument

The productivity industry has it backwards. The solution to getting more done is **not** to optimize how you do things — it's to **eliminate most of what you're doing** in the first place.

> Do less, not more.

## The Trap

Most people's approach to being "more productive":
1. Feel overwhelmed by too much work
2. Seek productivity tools, hacks, systems
3. Implement complex systems to manage the complexity
4. Systems themselves become overhead
5. Now overwhelmed by even more complexity
6. Repeat

## The Alternative

Instead of optimizing the 100 things you're doing, ask: **"Which 3 things actually matter?"**

Then eliminate the other 97.

The productivity you "gain" from tools and hacks is marginal compared to the productivity you gain from simply not doing 97% of what you currently do.

## Connection to Sam's Philosophy

This is the same core logic as [[sam-ovens-death-by-1000-cuts]]:
- Death by 1000 cuts = the accumulated weight of tiny unnecessary commitments
- The cure is not band-aids on each cut — it's stopping the cutting

And [[long-term-thinking]]:
- Productivity hacks feel good now (short-term optimization)
- Elimination is hard now but massive long-term leverage
