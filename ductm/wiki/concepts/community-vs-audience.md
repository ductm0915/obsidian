# Community vs. Audience

The most important distinction in building online groups: a **community** is defined by the strength of member-to-member relationships; an **audience** is defined by the creator-to-follower relationship. These require entirely different design approaches.

---

## The Social Network → Social Media Shift

Social platforms were originally called "social *networks*" — interactive, peer-to-peer. They became "social *media*" — one creator broadcasting to many passive followers. The algorithm then amplified this asymmetry.

- **Audience model**: 1 creator → many followers. Followers don't interact with each other. Creator posts, followers consume. Platform = distribution channel.
- **Community model**: many members ↔ many members. The creator facilitates; members interact with each other. Platform = gathering place.

> *"A community's strength is measured by the strength of the relationships between the members — not between the members and the owner. That's called fame."* — Sam Ovens

---

## The Engagement Paradox

The standard playbook ("add value by posting daily") produces the *opposite* of community:
- It leaves no room for member participation
- It positions the creator as lecturer, not facilitator
- It optimizes for content consumption, not relationship formation
- High-posting groups → low engagement

**Root cause**: Audience habits transplanted into community contexts.

> *"Audience builders provide answers. Community builders ask questions."*

---

## What Measures Community Health

| Signal | Audience | Community |
|---|---|---|
| Primary relationship | Creator → follower | Member ↔ member |
| Success metric | Views, likes, subs | Monthly active members, member-initiated posts |
| Growth mechanism | Algorithm reach | Word of mouth, referrals |
| Value source | Creator's content | Peer relationships + shared identity |
| Ceiling | Creator's time/energy | Network effects — scales independently |

---

## Implications for Community Designers

1. **Ask, don't tell** — prompts that get members to share > posts that broadcast information
2. **Design for member-to-member interaction** — accountability partners, practice groups, peer reviews
3. **Step back** — the best community facilitation is often invisible; the creator coordinates and then gets out of the way
4. **Measure differently** — don't use audience metrics (views, likes) to evaluate a community
