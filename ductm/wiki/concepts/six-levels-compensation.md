# Six Levels of Compensation

## Definition
A hierarchy of trade structures ranked by risk borne and compensation potential. The central thesis: **you are compensated in proportion to the risk you take on — specifically the perceived risk**. The best earners exploit asymmetry: high *perceived* risk for the other party, low *actual* risk for them.

## The Six Levels

| Level | Structure | Example | Risk Who Bears It |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I work → you pay | Employee (W2) | Employer; employee trades risk for reliability |
| 2 | Pay as we go | Independent contractor | Shared; 5× annual turnover vs. employees |
| 3 | You pay → I work | Surgeon, lawyer retainer | Client; you demand upfront payment |
| 4 | When X happens → you pay | Revenue share, equity, outcome fees | Client bears delivery risk; you bear only skill |
| 5 | When nothing happens → you're paid | Insurance model, warranties, guarantees | Systemic risk redistributed to you; profit during normalcy |
| 6 | No matter what → you're paid | Taxes, payment processors, franchisors | Societal/monopoly risk; control the money flow |

## How to Move Up the Ladder

- **Royalties/licensing:** "Paid off the top" — revenue share before expenses, not profit share. Revenue is harder to manipulate than profit.
- **Guarantees and warranties:** Any service business can inject Level 5 risk-bearing by offering guarantees — services businesses, home services, and coaching especially.
- **Control the money flow:** Payment processors (Level 6) — take your cut before remitting the rest. Franchise models sometimes work this way.
- **Equity:** Level 4 mechanism — as the business does well, you do well, divorced from time worked.

## Key Insight: Mispriced Risk
The market overcompensates for *perceived* risk. If you understand why something isn't as risky as it appears, you can bid against conventional wisdom and capture outsized returns. Howard Marks buying distressed debt; Elon Musk building rockets. The question in every deal: *who bears the risk* — and can I shift that risk in my favor?

## Practical Applications
- As an employee: offer a lower base + uncapped upside. Takes you from Level 1 toward Level 4.
- As a service provider: start with layaway (Level 3 variant), guarantee outcomes (Level 5 variant).
- As a business builder: structure rev-share deals, affiliate commissions, performance bonuses — all Level 4.
