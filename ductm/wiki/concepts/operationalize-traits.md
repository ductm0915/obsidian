# Operationalize Traits

## Definition
The process of breaking abstract identity traits ("charismatic," "patient," "disciplined") into specific, observable, teachable behaviors — things you can see with your eyes, measure, and train others to do.

**Core insight:** All "born with it" traits are really just bundles of smaller skills grouped under shorthand labels. These labels communicate efficiently but create confusion when used as instructions.

## The Problem with Abstract Instructions
- "Be charismatic" → meaningless as an instruction.
- "Smile when people walk in, address everyone by name, change tonality when speaking, nod while listening, maintain eye contact, address everyone when entering/exiting" → teachable, measurable.
- The more skilled the recipient, the more general the instruction can be. The less skilled: the more atomic it must become.

## The Rule: Describe Only What You Can Observe
Instructions must be actions or behaviors visible to an outside observer. No feelings, thoughts, intentions, or psychological states. "Seems confident" → "speaks at a pace of X, uses pauses here, smiles before answering, maintains eye contact."

## Application to Common Traits

| Abstract Trait | Operationalized Behaviors |
|---|---|
| Patient | "Figure out what to do in the meantime" — identify alternative action while waiting |
| Disciplined | Choose and complete N outreaches/actions regardless of emotional state |
| Charismatic | Smile on entry; use names 3× per conversation; change pitch on emphasis words; exit with individual acknowledgments |
| Relational (vs. transactional) | Ask one personal question per client call; follow up on prior conversation details |

## Why This Matters for Teams
If a chain of instructions has one unresolved step that the subordinate doesn't understand, they stop — and they don't always say why. This is why "go advertise" yields wildly different results from person to person. The fix: break instructions down until everyone can say "yes, I know exactly how to do step 1."

## Implications for Learning
If something can be learned, it is a skill. If it's a skill, it can be taught. Therefore: no one is born charismatic, patient, or disciplined — they learned it, either formally or informally through reinforcement. Any trait can be acquired by decomposing it and practicing components.
