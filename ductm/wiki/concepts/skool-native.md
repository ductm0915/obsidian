# Skool Native

**Source:** [[skool-games-q4-2025]] (Sam Ovens)

A creator whose **primary platform is Skool itself** — not Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, or any external social network.

> "I call them Skool natives. It's like their native platform, their YouTube or whatever, is Skool."

## The Phenomenon

Most Skool advice centers on bringing traffic from external platforms (Instagram → ManyChat → about page → community). But a new class of creator has emerged who builds entirely *within* the Skool ecosystem:

- No paid ads
- No off-platform social media presence
- Wins the Skool Games via platform-native engagement

**"Goose"** (Skool CEO) is the canonical example: won Skool Games multiple times with no off-platform audience.

## Why It Works

Skool has its own engagement mechanics and discovery engine:
- ~30% of memberships come from Skool's internal discovery features
- The platform rewards genuine member-to-member activity (see [[community-vs-audience]])
- Skool natives develop "psychic connection" with the platform's culture

## The Tradeoff

**Advantages:**
- Platform is already aligned with your preferences (you use it, you understand it)
- Discovery engine works in the background
- Community engagement depth > audience breadth

**Risks:**
- Single-platform dependency
- Off-platform warm traffic often converts better than cold traffic from discovery
- Scaling beyond platform discovery requires external traffic at some point

## Sam's Advice to Skool Natives

"If what you're doing on Skool is working — you won the Skool Games — double down on that rather than trying to build an off-platform audience from scratch."

Consider: a public Skool group as a "blog" (SEO via Skool's own search) as a gentle extension without full platform switch.

## Retention for Off-Platform Migration

When bringing people from off-platform onto Skool:
- Pin post: "Welcome here" until ~500-1,000 activities/day flywheel is established
- Classroom walkthrough: how to post → how to comment → here's a freebie
- Get members to Level 3 — retention skyrockets at that point
