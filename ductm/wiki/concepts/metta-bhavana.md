# Mettā Bhāvanā (Loving-Kindness Meditation)

**Mettā Bhāvanā** (Pāli: loving-kindness meditation) is the final practice taught in S.N. Goenka's 10-day Vipassana course, practiced on Day 11 as the "graduation" session.

## Core Mechanism

After 10 days of purifying the mind through Vipassana (observation of sensations with equanimity), the practitioner shares the resulting peace outward:

> "May all beings be happy, may all beings be peaceful."

The purified mind radiates mettā outward — not as a wishful prayer but as a natural overflow of inner cleanliness.

## Key Distinction from Other Practices

- **Vipassana** = purification inward (removing defilements)
- **Mettā** = radiation outward (sharing the resulting purity)

Without prior purification, mettā becomes hollow words. With purification (via Vipassana), mettā becomes a genuine energetic transmission.

## Goenka's Teaching

Goenka describes mettā as the "daily fee" — the gift practitioners give to the world each day in exchange for their Vipassana practice. It is practiced at the end of each sitting session.

## Connection to Vipassana Course

- Taught on **Day 11** (graduation)
- Prerequisite: sufficient inner purification from 10 days of Vipassana
- Duration: brief (5–10 minutes) at end of each sitting
