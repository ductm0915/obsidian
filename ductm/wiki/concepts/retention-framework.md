# Retention Framework

## Core Insight

Reducing monthly churn from 20% → 10% does not improve LTV by 10–12% — it **doubles** LTV. Small churn improvements have outsized compounding effects.

## Churn Cohort Model

Churn is not uniform — it follows predictable cohort curves:

| Milestone | Churn Rate | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1–3 | 20%+ | Highest churn; focus on activation/onboarding |
| Day 90 | ~10% | First major cliff; get people a win before here |
| Month 6+ | ~2% | Near-permanent; focus on community connection |

**Tactical reframe:** Don't ask *"how do I lower monthly churn?"* Ask *"how do I get people past day 90?"* and *"how do I get people to month 6?"*

## The Overwhelm Trap

The #1 churn driver is overwhelm — not price. Adding features increases churn because:
- Users feel they're using only 20% of value when 5 features exist but they use 1
- The same feature delivered as the *only* feature = 100% value perception

> **Planet Fitness case:** Stripped to 10–15% of a typical gym → beat full-service gyms on retention.

**Diagnostic:** *"If I had to delete everything, what one thing would you fight for?"*

## Value Per Second

Related: [[value-per-second]] — users have finite attention. The metric is not *seconds of value* (more content, more features) but *value per second* (highest density of value per unit of time consumed).

## Retention Levers by Milestone

- **First 30 days:** Activation, onboarding, expectation setting, fast wins
- **Day 90:** Deliver a concrete outcome or behavioral change
- **Month 6:** Foster connections with other members (1 real friend = 5x tenure)

## 10 True Regulars Model

Identify 10 model customers → DM them → spotlight publicly → matchmake with new members. These regulars anchor engagement and prevent "out-group" feelings for newcomers.

## Annual Plan

Annual billing reduces churn because decision is made once vs. 12 times. Even removing the "can't cancel" effect, annual buyers are more committed and more qualified. Standard discount: ~16–17%.
