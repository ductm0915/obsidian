# Owner Independence Valuation — Frustrated Fred vs. Wealthy William

## The Core Insight

Two businesses, identical financials ($10M revenue, $2M profit). One owner is required to run it; one is optional. They are not equally wealthy — the difference is roughly **20×** in wealth generation.

## The Math

| | Frustrated Fred | Wealthy William |
|---|---|---|
| Profit | $2M | $2M |
| After 50% tax | $1M | $1M |
| Personal savings | ~$500K/year | (optional — owns equity) |
| Business value (6× multiple) | $0 (unsellable) | **$12M asset** |
| Extra $500K profit impact | +$250K savings | **+$3M net worth** (6×) |

- Fred accumulates wealth linearly through income (taxed once, ~$500K/year).
- William holds a compounding asset that multiplies every dollar of profit by 6× (or more).
- The **multiplier gap** is why building a sellable business beats being a high-paid operator.

## Why the Multiple Exists

A business that requires its owner has **zero transfer value** — a buyer would need to also buy the owner. An owner-independent business is essentially a growing annuity. Investors pay a premium for annuities with upward trajectories.

The moment something goes from "valuable to you" → "valuable to anyone," it becomes:
- Sellable
- Fundable
- Worth multiples on profit

- The 6× multiple is conservative — businesses growing 20%+/year command 10–15×.

*"It's very inefficient to become wealthy off of regular income because it's taxed to oblivion. You don't get 10 years of work. You get one year divided by two after taxes. So the difference is like a 20× difference between a valuable company that can sell for 10× versus one that can't."* — [[alex-hormozi]]
