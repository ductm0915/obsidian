# Ovens Frustration Tolerance

**Frustration tolerance** is Sam Ovens' reframe of the traditional concept of "discipline": instead of suppressing discomfort, it is about developing the capacity to remain stable and productive **while** experiencing frustration, confusion, or failure.

See: [[frustration-tolerance]] for the primary concept page.

## Key Insight (Ovens)

> "Discipline is not the ability to force yourself to do things you don't want to do. It is the ability to feel like giving up — and not give up." — Ovens paraphrase

High frustration tolerance = the capacity to **smile when things go dead wrong** (Goenka parallel from Day of Happiness).

## Vipassana Parallel

Goenka's equanimity (upekkhā) is the meditative development of the same quality: maintaining inner stability when external or internal events create pressure. See: [[equanimity-with-sensation]]
