# WiSBY Principle — Why Should I Believe You?

**Source:** [[skool-games-q4-2025]] (Alex Hormozi)

The foundational authority question every creator and salesperson must answer before their instructions carry weight. Hormozi keeps the acronym "WiSBY" — *Why Should I Believe You?* — posted in his creative room.

> "We should always be thinking — everyone comes in with a huge amount of skepticism. Like everything's fake, everyone's lying. Why should I believe you?"

## The Mechanism

- Start with the most extreme proof of what's possible: "Here's me doing a 1200" → *then* say "here's how you do an ollie"
- The extreme example sets the anchor; the instruction becomes credible
- Same principle: Red Bull space jump → "if that guy fell from space, it'll probably wake me up"
- Vibe-coded a $10M app → "he can probably help me automate my email"

## WiSBY vs. Authority

WiSBY is not just "show your credentials." It's about **demonstrating visible control over the things your audience wants to control**. The authority must be:
1. Visible and observable (not just claimed)
2. In the domain they care about
3. Extreme enough to "regress down" to their situation
4. Paired with personality (see [[talent-personality-formula]])

## Applications

**Content:** Lead with your most unbelievable result → instructions become credible  
**Sales:** Past proof (outcomes for others) + conviction (see [[conviction-framework]])  
**Brand:** Be the world #1 in your niche's hardest dimension → everyone beneath respects you  

## The Pyramid Model

The higher your real-world achievement, the larger the cross-section of people who view you as authority. Beyond a point (top 1%), even others at elite level don't see you as a threat — they "tip their hat." That's when the brand solidifies fully.
