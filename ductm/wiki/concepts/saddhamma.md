# Saddhamma — True Dhamma

Saddhamma (Pali: sat = true/good + dhamma) is the adjective the Buddha consistently used for his own teaching, in contrast to sectarian religious labels. Goenka uses this concept to distinguish the universal, experiential, non-sectarian nature of Vipassana from any religious identity. Key point: "Dharma" (Sanskrit) or "Dhamma" (Pali) in the Buddha's usage means the universal law of nature (dhāretī ti dhamma = "that which supports/upholds"), not a religious sect. Saddhamma is accessible to anyone who practices; its marker is direct experiential verification, not belief. Contrasted with: sampradāya (sectarianism).
