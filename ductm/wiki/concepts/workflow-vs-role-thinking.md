# Workflow-Based vs. Role-Based Thinking

## The Shift

**Old paradigm (role-based):** "I need to hire an editor."

**New paradigm (workflow-based):** "What are the 5 tasks an editor actually does? Each of those should be a workflow."

This is the fundamental mental model shift for operating an AI-native business. Org charts organize *communication between humans* and establish hierarchies for decision-making. Workflows organize *inputs and outputs* — the actual value creation.

---

## The Framework

For every hire you're considering:

1. Write down the 4–10 things that person does with their hands, eyes, and mouth.
2. For each task, ask: **can this live inside a workflow instead of headcount?**
3. Automate task by task — not role by role.

The goal is not "automate away the editor" — it's "automate task 1, then task 2, then task 3." The role dissolves naturally as the tasks get systematized.

---

## Manufacturing Analogy

Every business at its core: **raw inputs → special sauce → more valuable output**.

A manufacturing business already thinks in input/output flows. A service business pretends it's organized around humans when really it's organized around tasks. Treat your service operations like a factory floor.

---

## Practical Action

Hormozi's tactical prescription:
1. Write every task you perform daily at the most granular level possible.
2. Take the first task and put it into AI: *"Help me automate this. What steps would you take?"*
3. Execute the first step on the list. Screenshot when stuck. Keep moving.

---

## Why Most Organizations Fail the Transition

- Role-based thinking is emotionally comfortable (people have names, faces, relationships).
- Incumbents with large org charts face a structural disadvantage: sunk headcount + emotional resistance + reluctance to have hard conversations about automation.
- AI-native startups don't have those conversations to have — they automate from day one.
