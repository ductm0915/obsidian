# Mind as Wild Elephant

The **wild elephant** metaphor is Goenka's standard teaching device for the untrained mind in Days 1–2 of the Vipassana course.

## The Metaphor

An untrained elephant is dangerous — powerful, unpredictable, destructive. A trained elephant becomes one of humanity's most useful tools. The difference is entirely in training.

Similarly:
- **Untrained mind** = wild elephant: scattered, reactive, capable of great harm (to self and others)
- **Trained mind (Vipassana)** = working elephant: powerful, directed, stable

## The Training Process

- **Rope** = Anapana practice (breath observation as the rope that ties the elephant to a post)
- **Post** = the small area under the nose (the fixed observation point)
- **Day 1–3** = initial training; the elephant keeps running away
- **Day 4+** = elephant begins to follow; Vipassana can begin
