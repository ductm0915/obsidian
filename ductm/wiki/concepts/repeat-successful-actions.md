# Repeat Successful Actions

**Source:** [[skool-games-q4-2025]] (Alex Hormozi)

When something works, keep doing it — until it *actually* stops working. Not until you fear it will stop working, not until it gets boring, not until someone suggests a new approach.

> "You will do something and as soon as it begins to work, do not stop doing that thing until it stops working. Do not stop doing that thing because you fear it will stop working."

## The Rule

**If it worked → do it again.**  
"Did it work? Yes. Do it again. But we did it yesterday. Do it again."

## Why People Stop (And Why That's Wrong)

- It feels repetitive or boring (to you — your audience is likely seeing it fresh)
- You fear it'll stop working before it actually does
- Someone from a different niche suggests a new model
- AI announcements, platform changes, FOMO create anxiety

None of these are data. They're fear masquerading as strategy.

## The Book Launch Proof

Hormozi's $100M Offers book launch:
- Day 2: exact same presentation, identical slides
- Result: $25M in sales — same amount as Day 1
- Audience: tons of the same people who already bought — they re-bought
- "We watch reruns of shows we already know happened"

## The Input-Output Frame

Once something works, you have a **proven input-output equation**:
- Input: the content format, the offer, the traffic source
- Output: revenue, subscribers, members

The only question then becomes: *how do I 10x or 100x the inputs?*
- More volume (work ethic)
- More leverage (one piece of content → 10 platforms, see [[content-waterfall-system]])

## Scope

This applies to:
- Content formats that convert
- Sales scripts and presentations
- Traffic sources that drive members
- Onboarding sequences
- Pricing structures

## Important Caveat

"Repeat" doesn't mean "freeze." It means:
- Keep doing what works as the foundation (70% in 70/20/10)
- Experiment on the margins (20% adjacent, 10% moonshot)
- Only stop the core when you have **first-party data** that it's actually declining
