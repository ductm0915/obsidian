# Adhiṭṭhāna — Strong Determination

Adhiṭṭhāna (Pali) means strong determination or firm resolution, especially one made with sacca-kiriyā (truth-declaration). In Vipassana courses, adhiṭṭhāna refers to the instruction to sit completely still without moving during sittings of strong determination (typically one-hour sittings on Days 4–10). More broadly, it is one of the Ten Pāramīs (perfections). Goenka's personal story: the night before the first Vipassana course in India (July 3, 1969), Goenka made an adhiṭṭhāna that if it was the right time to start the work, the first student would arrive by morning — and 14 students arrived. U Ba Khin's "Dhamma wish" (that Vipassana would return to India) was framed as an adhiṭṭhāna that was fulfilled exactly 23 years later.
