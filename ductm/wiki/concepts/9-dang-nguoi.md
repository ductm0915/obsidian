# 9 Dạng Người (9 Types of People)

**Origin:** [[wit-organization]] — [[hong-trieu-bao]]
**Primary source:** [[wit-k07-nhan-thuc-con-nguoi]] (Session 12, 15)
**Also taught in:** [[wit-k07-nhan-thuc-nhat-anh]]

## Concept Summary

The 9 Dạng Người framework is WIT's relational taxonomy: nine functional roles that any person in your life can occupy, classified by the kind of value they contribute to your journey. The key insight that generates this taxonomy: **"Thành tựu do người"** — all meaningful achievements come through other people, not through individual effort alone.

The framework dissolves conventional "nhân xưng" (named labels: husband, colleague, friend, parent) and replaces them with function-based recognition. The same individual can occupy multiple roles simultaneously, or shift roles over time. A spouse can be a quý nhân one day, a cao nhân the next, and a nhân tài when carrying household responsibilities.

## The 9 Types

### 1. Thiện Tri Thức (Benevolent Wisdom-Opener)
**Function:** Khai mở trí tuệ — opens your wisdom, illuminates what you couldn't see before

The person whose presence or words triggers a fundamental shift in how you understand yourself or the world. This is not a teacher who instructs you in a topic; this is the person who changes your entire epistemic frame.

Key characteristic: Thiện tri thức does not require payment, recognition, or gratitude. The gift is given freely; the appropriate response is to receive it, embody it, and pass it forward. Naming or crediting them is not required and may even interfere with the natural flow of merit.

The role of WIT itself is often described as thiện tri thức: an environment that opens wisdom.

### 2. Thầy Cô (Teachers)
**Function:** Cho đạo lý — gives ethics, principles, moral foundations

Formal and informal teachers who transmit principles of living. The content is not technical skill (that belongs to chuyên gia) but ethical-moral formation: how to treat people, what obligations one has, what constitutes right conduct.

Parents are the first thầy cô. School teachers. Mentors whose guidance shapes character. The relationship is more formal and ongoing than a thiện tri thức encounter.

### 3. Chuyên Gia (Expert)
**Function:** Cho tư duy — gives thinking models, mental frameworks, domain expertise

The person who upgrades how you reason in a specific domain — finance, health, business, communication. Not moral formation (that's thầy cô) but cognitive upgrade. A good accountant who changes how you think about money. A doctor who teaches you a health framework you carry for life.

### 4. Cao Nhân (Enlightened Guide / Pointer)
**Function:** Chỉ điểm — points the way; gives a perspective, story, or principle that helps you transcend a current obstacle

Often anonymous, often brief. The person who, at the right moment, says exactly the right thing that dissolves a stuck pattern. They don't need to be wise in general — they need only to have been the conduit for this one insight that shifted you.

Cao nhân can be anyone: a spouse, a child, a stranger on the street. The Vietnamese phrase "cao nhân ở trên núi" (the enlightened sage lives on the mountain) is reframed: in WIT's teaching, cao nhân are everywhere, often hidden in plain sight, often preferring anonymity.

Appropriate response: **Tri phúc** (recognize your fortune), **Trọng phúc** (honor it), **Tạo phúc** (create merit — spread the value received). You don't repay the cao nhân directly; you spread what they gave you.

### 5. Quý Nhân (Benefactor)
**Function:** Giúp đỡ — helps you directly, in practical ways, when you need it

The "helper" in the traditional sense. But WIT (especially [[nhat-anh]]) adds a critical inversion: **the person who receives your help is actually your quý nhân**, because by accepting, they give you the opportunity to create merit (công đức phước đức). You cannot create merit without someone to receive value from you.

Therefore: when you "give" to someone, inwardly recognize that they are the quý nhân — they are the ones giving you something irreplaceable. This prevents the trap of feeling that helpers are owed gratitude or that giving creates entitlement.

The correct posture when you are the helper: "Người nhận mới là người giúp mình" (The receiver is the one helping me).

### 6. Thần Tài (Fortune / Energy Amplifier)
**Function:** Tương trợ — supports with their energy, presence, and the favorable conditions their existence creates

Not about money or material gifts — about the energetic effect of their presence. Some people, when they show up, cause good things to happen. Their presence amplifies the merit and energy of the environment.

The teacher [[hong-trieu-bao]] describes himself as "thần tài tâm thái" — a fortune-bringer of inner state. People who enter the WIT Zoom environment often find their mood lifting, their inner state stabilizing.

Appropriate response: **Trân trọng biết ơn sự hiện diện** (appreciate and be grateful for their presence). Don't demand more from them — their presence is already the gift.

### 7. Nhân Mạch (Network Connector)
**Function:** Kết nối — connects you to other people, organizations, resources

The human through whom you reach everyone else. The person who makes an introduction that changes your life. They don't do the work — they bridge gaps between people who otherwise would not meet.

Appropriate response: Acknowledge the connection, and return it: become a nhân mạch for others. "Mình lan tỏa lại" — pass the value forward through your own connections.

### 8. Nhân Tài (Capable Carrier)
**Function:** Gánh vác — takes on and carries a domain of responsibility that enables you to focus elsewhere

Not simply "a talented person" — nhân tài in WIT's usage refers specifically to someone who gánh vác (bears the weight of) a function in your life or organization. They carry something so you don't have to.

Appropriate response: Bồi dưỡng — nurture them across all 7 human dimensions (trí tuệ, tâm thái, nhân cách, phẩm chất, năng lực, thể chất, vật chất). Help them realize their ước mơ (dreams). Material support alone is insufficient — they need to be developed holistically.

### 9. Minh Sư (Enlightened Master)
**Function:** Dẫn dắt — guides, illuminates the path; shapes the whole arc of becoming

The highest relational type. The minh sư doesn't just share knowledge or solve problems — they see who you're becoming and guide the entire journey. The relationship is typically long-term, deeply personal.

Critical distinction: **Minh sư dẫn dắt** (genuine guidance that strengthens your own agency) vs. **thao túng** (manipulation that creates dependency). A true minh sư empowers; a false one builds attachment to themselves.

The first minh sư is always cha mẹ (parents). As one matures, the relationship deepens: "Minh sư không ở đâu xa — Minh sư là mẹ là cha" (The enlightened master is not far — it is mother and father).

## The Framework In Use

### Recognizing Types
The practice is not to lock anyone permanently into a category but to notice, in each interaction, which function they are currently serving. This attention creates appreciation: "Bên cạnh mình luôn có đủ 9 dạng người" (all 9 types are always present around you, if you have awareness).

### Attracting / Becoming Each Type
The 9-type framework inverts from recognition to aspiration: through learning and practice, you can become any of the 9 types for others. Session 15 of the main course suggests that completing the full WIT curriculum positions a person to serve as all 9 types within their sphere — thiện tri thức who opens wisdom, thầy cô who gives principles, etc.

### The Complete Circle
A life with all 9 types flourishing:
> "Bên cạnh chúng ta luôn có Cao nhân chỉ điểm, Quý nhân giúp đỡ, Nhân mạch kết nối, Thần tài tương trợ, Nhân tài gánh vác, Minh sư dẫn dắt, Thầy cô cho đạo lý, Chuyên gia cho tư duy, Thiện tri thức khai mở trí tuệ."

## Relationship to Other WIT Concepts

- All 9 types are understood through the [[quy-luat-luan-hoi]] lens: they arrive through accumulated nhân (causes), and their presence is duyên lành (favorable conditions)
- Identifying someone's type is the prerequisite for [[nang-cap-moi-quan-he]] (upgrading the relationship)
- Properly relating to each type is how one accumulates công đức and phước đức (merit and blessings)
- The [[buong-tai]] concept is particularly relevant to properly releasing the "quý nhân identity" when giving
