# Sam Ovens — Long-Term Thinking, 2nd Order Consequences & Effect Horizons

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]]  
**Type:** YouTube video (recorded from LA apartment; stops to let his cat out mid-video)  
**Core theme:** Most business failure is caused by short-term thinking. The time variable is the most neglected variable in all of business.

---

## Summary

Sam argues that time is the most important variable in business, yet almost nobody accounts for it properly. Humans are biologically wired for instant gratification (dopamine loops, social media, fast food), which systematically leads to bad long-term decisions.

**Three core frameworks:**

### 1. Short-term vs. long-term thinking
The marshmallow experiment: children who delayed gratification (2 marshmallows later vs. 1 now) dramatically outperformed peers on every life metric tracked over decades. Sam applies this universally: debt is a better-today-at-the-cost-of-tomorrow decision. So is junk food, TV, and opportunity-hopping.

### 2. Second-order (and third, fourth, Nth-order) consequences
Most people see only first-order consequences. Sam uses Valeant Pharmaceuticals as the definitive case study:
- CEO's strategy: buy pharma companies, cut all R&D departments, pocket the savings, show spectacular profits
- First-order: profits explode → stock soars → CEO becomes celebrity
- Second-order: no new drugs in pipeline
- Third-order: competitors with R&D release better drugs
- Fourth-order: Valeant loses customers to competitors
- Fifth-order: collapse, bankruptcy, investors lose everything

Michael Jordan counterexample: Jordan noticed that sponsorships and celebrity were second-order consequences of being the best. He canceled everything and focused on the first-order thing (basketball). Result: climbed back to GOAT.

### 3. Effect horizons
A decision-making tool: mentally map any decision on a chart of Time (x-axis) vs Upside/Downside (y-axis). Two options are usually mirror images:
- Option A: immediate upside, long-term downside (wheelchair path)
- Option B: short-term downside, long-term upside (the hill)

Rule: always take the hill. The feedback loops make compounding exponential, not linear.

---

## Key Claims

1. **All bad decisions are short-term decisions.** Debt, fast food, TV, social media addiction — structurally identical.

2. **Second-order consequences are invisible to most people.** They can only see first-order effects. Business leaders who cut R&D, buy private jets early, neglect the product — all failed to see second-order consequences.

3. **"The main thing is to keep the main thing the main thing."** All wealth, fame, and luxury are second-order consequences of doing the first-order thing exceptionally well. Spend time chasing the second-order things = first-order thing degrades → second-order things evaporate.

4. **Compounding is exponential, not linear.** A small decision (read a book vs. buy a TV) diverges exponentially over years because feedback loops amplify at each node.

5. **Sam literally removed all TVs from his LA house with a ratchet set on day two.** Unplugged landlines. Eliminated the temptation at the source rather than relying on willpower.
