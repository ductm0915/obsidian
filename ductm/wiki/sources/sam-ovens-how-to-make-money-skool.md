# Sam Ovens — How To Make Money On Skool (Full Course)

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]] (with co-presenter)  
**Type:** Full course video (recorded training)  
**Core theme:** A practical framework for building a monetized community on Skool, from zero to full-time income.

---

## Summary

Sam and a co-presenter walk through the complete framework for building a successful Skool community. Presented as practical training based on data from 50,000+ communities and 15M+ users.

**The Big Picture Model:**
Traffic → Community → Members → Income

Traffic sources:
- YouTube (most common example: CubeCraft DevOps community — $33K/month)
- Instagram (Pickleball School — $30K/month, $39/month subscription)
- Facebook Ads (Grow with Evelyn — $111K/month; Skool about page outperformed ClickFunnels)
- TikTok (Barber Accelerator — going live + link in bio = $15K/month)
- Email list + website (Calligraphy School — $9/month, $7K/month)
- Skool discovery itself (22% of memberships platform-generated; no audience needed to start)

**Case study: Hazel (Crochet Creations):**
- Zero audience, zero content, zero ads
- Told no one she knows about the community
- Got 126 members from Skool discovery alone
- Listed as #2 for "crochet" searches on Skool

**Stages of Community Evolution:**
1. **Noob:** Find your thing (passion/topic that you can obsess about)
2. **First 3 members:** Real people who aren't you, aren't fake
3. **10 True Regulars:** The core that drives engagement — 10 engaged people matter more than 1,000 lurkers
4. **First $1:** Solve one community problem → business is born
5. **Full-time income:** Replace the job
6. **Multiple revenue streams:** Subscription + services + products + adjacent communities

**What makes communities work:**
- Shared passion/purpose
- Starting conversations, asking questions
- Recognizing and celebrating members
- The "regulars" effect: a bar with 10 regulars who love it creates the culture for everyone else

**On monetization:**
- Free community with product sales: Brotherhood of Scent (fragrances → physical products)
- Paid subscription: Pickleball School, CubeCraft
- Service-based: coaching, consulting sold to community members
- Physical products: Midnight Monster (stickers/merch)

**Skool's mission:** Help 1 billion people find community. "When you find your thing, you find your people."

---

## Key Claims

1. **22% of Skool memberships come from the platform's own discovery engine.** You don't need a large existing audience to start.

2. **Quality of engagement beats quantity of members.** 10 true regulars who love the community > 1,000 passive lurkers.

3. **The Skool about page outperforms custom funnels (ClickFunnels, etc.)** for paid subscriptions. Evelyn's test proved this directly.

4. **Every community has problems — solve one of them and you have a business.**

5. **A loneliness epidemic creates tailwinds.** Community is one of the most undersupplied goods in the modern world.
