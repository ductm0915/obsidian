# Freedom from Addiction — Dhamma Giri Seminar, 1989

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Closing address at the Seminar on Vipassana for Relief from Addictions & Better Health, Dhamma Giri, 1989
- **Audience:** Seminar participants, many with addiction issues
- **Key theme:** All addiction is addiction to sensation; Vipassana breaks the cycle by working at the biochemical-mental interface

---

## Summary

This is arguably the most technically detailed of Goenka's discourses on addiction. He lays out a complete theory: addiction is not primarily to the substance or behavior, but to the *sensation* (vedanā) produced by that substance or behavior. The biochemical flow (āsava) creates a sensation; the mind reacts with craving; craving stimulates more of the same biochemical flow; a self-reinforcing cycle begins.

Goenka distinguishes the surface mind (paritta citta — small, conscious, intellectual) from the large unconscious mind that is always in contact with bodily sensations and constantly reacting. There is a barrier between these two layers. Intellectual understanding of the harm of addiction does not cross this barrier.

Vipassana breaks this barrier. Once the student can feel sensations throughout the body (typically by day 3-4), they can observe the sensation that accompanies an urge — and by observing it with equanimity (understanding anicca), the urge loses its grip without suppression or expression. Ten to fifteen minutes of calm observation typically dissolves an urge cycle.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Addiction is addiction to sensation, not substance.** "When you say that you are addicted, you are actually addicted to the sensation." This applies to alcohol, drugs, anger, passion, fear, egotism — all are addictions to particular sensations.
2. **Āsava — the biochemical mechanism.** A defilement (passion, anger, fear) generates a specific biochemical secretion (āsava) that in turn stimulates more of the same defilement. This is why intellectual resolve fails — the biochemistry continues.
3. **The unconscious is not unconscious.** The "unconscious" mind is always in contact with the body and its sensations, reacting day and night. Only the surface conscious mind is unaware of this.
4. **The barrier between layers.** Intellectual acceptance of Dhamma is insufficient because understanding stays at the surface. Only Vipassana practice breaks the barrier and allows change at the root level.
5. **Practical technique for urges.** When an urge arises, don't immediately act. Wait 10-15 minutes and observe the sensation associated with the urge. The sensation — observed as anicca — will pass, and the urge with it.
6. **Progress is gradual.** If successful even 1 time out of 10, "a very good beginning is made because the root has started changing."
7. **Āvijjā (ignorance) is the strongest āsava.** Alcohol and drug intoxication multiply ignorance — inability to observe what is actually happening inside — making sensation-observation more difficult.

## The Biochemical Loop

```
Defilement arises (e.g. passion)
→ Biochemical secretion (kāmāsava) flows through body
→ Sensation created (āsava)
→ Mind reacts with more craving (kāma-taṇhā)
→ More secretion → stronger sensation → stronger craving
→ Vicious cycle multiplies for minutes or hours
```

**The intervention point:** Observe the sensation with equanimity (not suppressing, not reacting). Break the feedback loop. One moment of equanimity → a few seconds → minutes. Eventually the cycle breaks entirely.

## Notable Quotes

> "All these are addictions. All these are addictions to your impurities. And at the intellectual level you understand very well, 'Anger is not good for me.' Yet you are addicted to anger, you keep generating anger."

> "When you say that you are addicted, you are actually addicted to the sensation. You are addicted to this flow, the bio-chemical flow."

> "The unconscious mind is actually not unconscious, as at all times it remains in contact with this body."

> "Life then is full of action instead of reaction. Action is always positive. It is only when they react that they generate negativity and become miserable."

> "Not merely addiction to drugs or alcohol: the addiction to the mental impurities is stronger than these."

> "We are not doing it to oblige anybody; not to please any God Almighty, not to please your teacher. You are doing it to oblige yourself."
