# Role of Dhamma in Improving the Society — Igatpuri Seminar, 1995

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Excerpts from closing talk at the seminar "Dhamma — its True Nature," Igatpuri, 1995
- **Audience:** Monks, scholars, Dhamma practitioners
- **Key theme:** How Vipassana can reach the masses; historical precedent of Emperor Ashoka; priority targets (elite, youth, scholars)

---

## Summary

This closing address follows a two-day seminar on Dhamma and its applications to society. Goenka grounds his optimism in a historical precedent: Emperor Ashoka's reign, during which Vipassana was taught on a mass scale throughout India through specially appointed "dhammamatyas" (Dhamma teachers/officials). The rock edicts testify that the program worked — they have survived 2,200 years precisely because people's conduct actually improved.

Goenka's strategy for modern spread focuses on three priority groups: (1) the elite/leadership class, since "all good or bad things trickle down from the top"; (2) the coming generation, since young people in schools and colleges are more receptive to rational Dhamma than their parents who cling to sectarian form; and (3) scholars, who need to experience Vipassana to understand the experiential meaning of the texts they study.

A notable feature of this discourse: multiple testimonies from practitioners of other traditions who report that Vipassana illuminated their own scriptures in ways mere reading could not. A Christian mother superior said "You are teaching Christianity in the name of the Buddha." A learned mullah reported that Vipassana clarified two Quranic ayats about observing respiration to observe Allah. Sikh students found they understood the words of their Gurus. Goenka himself found the Gita transformed in meaning after Vipassana.

The central message: sermons, seminars, scripture study — all useful as preparation — but none of them produces actual change. Only experiential practice transforms behavior. The nation that lost Dhamma 2,500 years ago can regain it.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Ashoka's Vipassana experiment on mass scale is historical proof.** It worked for 500 years after the Buddha; rock edicts testify to improved conduct. The precedent exists.
2. **Dhammamatyas = Dhamma officials, not religious proselytizers.** They taught Dhamma without conversion; for 500 years nobody used the term "Buddhist Dhamma."
3. **Elite first, then trickle-down.** "All good or bad things trickle down from the top and then spread through the whole society."
4. **Youth are more receptive than elders.** Young people see that their parents perform rites without personal transformation. They respond to rational, results-oriented Dhamma.
5. **Practical benefits for students.** Anapana practice before opening an exam paper calms the mind, improves recall, and reduces exam anxiety. Concrete, verifiable results.
6. **Experiential understanding unlocks scripture.** Texts that are opaque when merely read become transparent after meditation practice. This is why scholars need to practice, not just study.
7. **Vipassana is Gita applied.** Goenka: "Oh, this is applied Gita, not merely theories. This is how one can become vita rāga, vita dvesha, vita bhaya."
8. **Tihar Prison course (1,000 people) was a turning point.** Sayagyi U Ba Khin's prophecy ("one day he will teach a course for 1,000 students") was fulfilled.

## Notable Quotes

> "To improve society we cannot forget the individual; Man matters most. And to improve the individual, we cannot forget the mind; mind matters most."

> "I found that those studying at schools and colleges are accepting it and getting good results. Their memory becomes very strong, their comprehension is much improved and their nervousness goes away."

> "The first mother superior who joined a course, Mother Mary, said, 'You are teaching Christianity in the name of the Buddha!' Yes, I am teaching Christianity in the name of the Buddha."

> "A very learned mullah... told me, 'Oh, now we understand.' [The two Quranic ayats about observing respiration to observe Allah]"

> "He laughed and said: 'One day he will teach a course for one thousand students!'" (Sayagyi U Ba Khin's prophecy)

> "Even with one lamp lit, we are happy to have a small circle of light. Soon this one lamp will become two and then three lamps, and in this way the Dhamma will spread."
