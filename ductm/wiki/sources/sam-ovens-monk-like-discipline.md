# Sam Ovens — Monk Like Discipline: What It Really Takes To Win

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]]  
**Type:** Training module (likely part of a course)  
**Core theme:** Success is the sum of your human will. Discipline, not motivation, is the master's tool.

---

## Summary

Part of a larger mindset training. Sam distinguishes discipline from motivation and presents 11 concrete practices for building steel-like self-discipline.

**Motivation vs. Discipline:**
- Motivation is temporary. When it runs out, most people stop and wait for more (loser behavior).
- Discipline is permanent. Pro athletes perform when completely demotivated. Discipline keeps them going.
- True masters are addicted to long-term gratification, not instant gratification.

**What is willpower?** The ability to control yourself, your impulses, and your actions. Strong determination that allows you to do something difficult without giving up or getting distracted. It is the superpower of successful people.

**How to build willpower:**
- Discipline itself is the most powerful energizer. Not motivational videos.
- Wean off instant gratification triggers: social media, sugar, alcohol, Netflix, partying
- Get hooked on delayed gratification: reading, exercise, building skills

**11 practices for self-discipline:**
1. Resist impulses (don't react immediately to cravings)
2. Practice long-term thinking in every decision
3. Maintain strict routine (same wake/sleep times daily)
4. Create standards and stick to them regardless of mood
5. Sacrifice the things you truly want that aren't aligned with the goal
6. Remove temptations from the environment entirely
7. Use visualization and planning to keep long-term goals vivid
8. Track your progress and health metrics (Oura Ring)
9. Build one habit at a time before adding the next
10. Review vision, annual goals, quarterly goals daily before starting work
11. Have accountability structures (trainer at door, chef prepared, etc.)

**Vision-to-Action hierarchy:**
Vision → Annual Goals → Quarterly Goals → Initiatives → Actions → Tasks. Plan tomorrow today every night at 9pm. In the morning, execute without rethinking.

---

## Key Claims

1. **"Success is the sum of your human will."** The height of a person's suffering and pain tolerance equals the height of their ultimate success.

2. **Discipline is the tool of the Masters.** Anyone can set a vision. The hard part is executing it every day for years without stopping.

3. **Sacrifice means saying no to things you genuinely want.** Not sacrifice when you discard what you don't care about. Real sacrifice is the most valuable practice.

4. **Instant gratification is an addiction structurally identical to drug addiction.** Social media likes, sugar, fast food — same dopamine loop, same compounding harm.

5. **The environment must be designed to make the good behavior the default.** Willpower depletes; environment doesn't. Like snakes and ladders: remove the snakes, place ladders on the path you want to travel.
