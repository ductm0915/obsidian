# Sam Ovens — Success Has Ingredients: Garbage In Garbage Out

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]]  
**Type:** YouTube video  
**Core theme:** Genius and talent are functions of inputs (mentors, books, environments) not innate ability. To improve your outputs, improve your inputs.

---

## Summary

Sam ran 436 job interviews over ~6 months and discovered he was asking the wrong questions. He was asking about outputs (skills, experience, past work) — which are easy to fake and hard to verify — instead of inputs (mentors, books, influences) — which are hard to fake and instantly revealing.

**The Chef's Table insight:** The world's best chefs are not the best at cooking technique — they are the best at sourcing ingredients. Many started their own farms and obsessed over soil quality, animal diet, and climate. The secret is upstream.

**The Genius Social Network (Dean Simonton research):** Simonton analyzed journals and letters of Newton, Galileo, Einstein, Copernicus, etc. Finding: geniuses are produced by the inputs of other geniuses. Newton stood on 14 "idols" (Bacon, Copernicus, Galileo, Kepler, ~10 others). Einstein's greatest influence was Newton. Genius compounds through generations of input.

Newton's own quote: "If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants."

**Interview methodology after the shift:**
- Old question: "Do you have skill X?" → Easy to lie about
- New question: "Who is your idol in field X?" → Can't fake; those who know answer instantly and in detail; those who don't, can't name anyone
- New question: "What books have taught you most about X?" → Same discriminating power

Sam found 100% correlation: people who could not name influences or books were universally poor performers. People who named them clearly, with specific learnings, were strong performers.

**Applied to you:** Take an audit of your inputs:
- Who are the 5 people you spend the most time with?
- What books have shaped your thinking?
- What courses/mentors have influenced you?
- What information do you consume daily?

If the answers are: social media, news, Netflix, drinking friends → your brain is rotting.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Talent is an output; inputs determine it.** The genius is not born — they are assembled from high-quality inputs accumulated over time.

2. **Asking about outputs in interviews is nearly useless.** Outputs can be fabricated. Inputs reveal genuine engagement and depth.

3. **The best way to hire is to ask about influences and books.** People who genuinely care about their craft can instantly recall names, titles, and specific learnings.

4. **The 5 people you spend the most time with determine your trajectory.** Raise the intelligence/ambition average of your close network and you will rise with it.

5. **Bad inputs actively degrade good ones.** "If you spend most of your time on Facebook, Instagram, and watching CNN — your brain is rotting."

## Notable Quotes

> "My output — my results, my skill, my ability — is a function of the quality of inputs I've been in contact with, not of being born with any skill."
