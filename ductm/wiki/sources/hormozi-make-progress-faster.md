# How to Make Progress Faster Than Everyone

Alex Hormozi reflects on his early, "cringe" content and outlines why judging your own beginning against someone else's middle is a flawed metric. He posits that the willingness to look foolish is a prerequisite for greatness, and that documenting the struggle is a powerful mechanism for future brand-building.

## Key Claims & Arguments

- **The Cringe Barrier**: Caring about anything deeply will always look "cringe" to those who care about nothing.
- **Status Play**: When someone calls you "cringe," it's often a defensive status play responding to the fact that you are changing your status relative to them.
- **Documenting the Journey**: Failing to document the early struggles out of shame is a missed opportunity. Winners document early because they have conviction they will eventually succeed.
- **The Tolerance for Suck**: The world is rational and rewards the deserving; to get good, you must be willing to be bad for a very long time.

## Quotes

> "Shame is breaking someone else's rules. Guilt is breaking your own rules. Cringe is supposed secondhand embarrassment... but in reality, it's a defensive status play."

> "The only way to get good is to be willing to be bad for a very long time."
