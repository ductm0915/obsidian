# Interview on BBC Radio 2 Jimmy Young Programme — 2002

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka (interviewed by Jimmy Young, BBC Radio 2)
- **Context:** Live radio interview on BBC Radio 2, part of Goenka's Europe and North America tour in 2002. Audience: 5.5 million listeners in Britain and abroad.
- **Format:** Q&A interview + extensive listener response section
- **Key theme:** Vipassana's effectiveness in prisons and for business leaders; the mechanism of habit-pattern change; word-of-mouth as the only spread mechanism

---

## Summary

This is an unusual document: a mainstream media interview for a mass audience, followed by a representative sample of 20+ listener responses. The combination reveals both Goenka's most accessible explanation of Vipassana and the public's spontaneous reaction to it.

Goenka describes his own entry into Vipassana through personal suffering — severe migraine headaches for which doctors were about to prescribe morphine. The practice eliminated not just the migraines but also his short-tempered nature and ego-centrism. This autobiographical opening is atypical of his other discourses and particularly effective for a general audience.

The prison application section is concrete: the challenge came from Vinoba Bhave (a great Indian saint), who said "If Vipassana has any efficacy, give a trial with these hardened criminals." The results convinced the Indian government to introduce Vipassana in jails. The mechanism is straightforward: prisoners observe that when they generate hateful thoughts (of revenge), their body heats up, perspires, pulses with tension. They realize, "I'm a miserable person. What am I doing? I'm harming myself." This self-recognition — not moralistic instruction — initiates genuine change.

Goenka's point about business executives is concise: their capacity increases because the mind stays "tranquil and transparent" — enabling quick, accurate decisions under pressure.

The listener responses overwhelmingly express fascination, openness, and recognition — several from people who have already taken courses, many asking how to find out more.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Vipassana healed Goenka's migraine and temper.** Personal testimony for a general audience — prior to any philosophical framework.
2. **Vipassana = observation of truth within oneself.** "Pertaining to the interaction of mind and matter." The simplest definition.
3. **Prison results.** Prisoners recognize through direct body-observation that their angry thoughts cause their own suffering. This is more persuasive than moral instruction.
4. **Challenge from Vinoba Bhave** was the trigger for prison courses.
5. **Business executives benefit too.** Senior Indian officials sent by the Bombay government. Capacity to work increases; decisions become faster and more accurate.
6. **Word of mouth only.** "We never have any kind of publicity or propaganda." People share because they get benefit.
7. **100,000 students per year** (at time of interview), from every country and religion.
8. **Lancaster Prison and US courses.** Vipassana has already reached British and American prison systems.

## Notable Quotes

> "I was a very ego-centred person, although very rich, but very ego-centred, very short-tempered, and I used to have very strong migraine headaches."

> "Those who get benefit from it, they can't resist, they start telling their friends and family, 'It is so good, it has done me so much good, you also go and give a trial.' This is how it has spread."

> "They recognize, 'I'm a miserable person. What am I doing? I'm harming myself.' When they start realizing this, the habit pattern begins changing." (On prisoners)

> "The mind remains so tranquil and transparent; when any problem comes they give a quick decision, a right decision, and so their capacity increases." (On business executives)
