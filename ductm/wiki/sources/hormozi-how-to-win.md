# How to Win (At ANYTHING)

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** Conference keynote transcript  
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-10

## Summary
A keynote speech structured around 5 questions: **What** to do, **How** to do it, **Why** you aren't doing it, **Who** you need to become, and **When** to start. The through-line: winning is achievable by anyone through inversion, skill decomposition, environmental design, and understanding motivation as deprivation.

## The 5 Framework Components

### 1. What — Inversion
Use the [[inversion-method]]: imagine the least successful version of you. What does that person do to guarantee failure? Then flip the list. Your brain is better at finding problems than solutions — exploit that.

### 2. How — Operationalize Everything
Abstract traits (charismatic, patient, disciplined) are really buckets of smaller, observable behaviors. Break them down to their most atomic, behaviorally measurable form. "Be charismatic" → smile when people walk in, use names, change tonality, nod while listening, maintain eye contact, address everyone when entering/exiting.
- See [[operationalize-traits]]

**Patience redefined:** "Figuring out what to do in the meantime" — not a personality trait, a skill.

### 3. Why — Two Blockers
**External:** [[five-stages-opportunity]] — Uninformed Optimism → Informed Pessimism → Valley of Despair → Informed Optimism → Achievement. Most people quit at the Valley and restart the cycle ("opportunity hopping").

**Internal:** [[deprivation-motivation]] — Motivation is proportional to the perceived gap between where you are and where you want to be. Rich people feel *more* deprived because they compare to richer peers. To get motivated: change who you compare yourself to; change your environment.

### 4. Who — Being is Doing
"Be, Do, Have" is backwards. Identity is a description of what someone repeatedly does. **Just do.** *"We are what we repeatedly do."* — Aristotle. When you do the things future-you does, you are that person already. The evidence just takes time to catch up.

### 5. When — Start Now
Three reasons people delay:
1. Season (economy/life stage) — Start when busy: if you only practice during easy times, you'll stop when things get hard.
2. No time — You're spending time on the wrong things. Elimination precedes addition.
3. When/Then fallacy — "I'll save money when I have more money." You do the things first to get the result; you don't get in order to do.

## Key Concepts Introduced
- [[inversion-method]] — Invert to identify failure modes, then flip to winning behaviors
- [[operationalize-traits]] — Break abstract traits into observable, teachable behaviors
- [[deprivation-motivation]] — Motivation = perceived gap; environment sets the standard
- [[learning-intelligence-confidence]] — Learning = same condition, new behavior; Intelligence = rate of learning; Confidence = past proof, domain-specific
