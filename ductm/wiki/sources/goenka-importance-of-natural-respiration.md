# The Importance of Natural Respiration — S.N. Goenka (Zee TV Discourse #5)

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Fifth in a series of 44 Hindi discourses broadcast on Zee TV (translated and adapted)
- **Audience:** General public, Hindi-speaking audience
- **Series:** Discourses on Samādhi (Concentration) — Anapana Meditation

## Core Argument

A meditator must understand the ultimate goal clearly: not merely to concentrate the mind, but to reach the depth of the mind where defilements arise, multiply, and accumulate. Natural breath is the only tool that can serve as a bridge between the "known field" (voluntary bodily functions) and the "unknown field" (autonomous bodily functions) — and thus reach those depths. Any adulteration of the breath observation (adding words, images, regulated breathing) destroys this bridge function.

---

## Key Claims and Arguments

### The Two Fields of the Body

The body has two territories:
1. **Known field** — organs under voluntary control (limbs, eyes, etc.); the meditator knows these
2. **Unknown field** — organs that work automatically (heart, lungs, liver, etc.); no experiential knowledge of these

The entire practice of Vipassana is about crossing from the known to the unknown field.

### Breath as the Bridge

Breath is unique: it is the only bodily process that is both voluntary and involuntary. This dual nature makes it the bridge between the known and unknown fields.

**Analogy:** A person living on one bank of a river cannot reach the beautiful other bank by praying for it to come to him. He must cross the river — and breath is that bridge.

> "_Saans dekhte dekhte, satya prakaṭatā jāya, Satya dekhte dekhte, parama satya dikh jāya._"  
> — "Observing respiration, truth manifests itself. Observing truth, the supreme truth manifests itself."

### Why Nothing Should Be Added to the Breath

Adding any word, form, image, or regulation to the breath immediately ruins its function as a bridge:
- The mind becomes absorbed in the added element, not in the breath itself
- Awareness of the breath's intimate connection to the mind and defilements is lost
- The journey from gross to subtle truth is blocked

### The Experiential Progression

As one observes natural breath, the truth of the body reveals itself progressively:
1. Gross breath → awareness of breath's rhythm
2. Link between defilements and breath rhythm becomes clear
3. Understanding of body-mind interaction at the experiential level
4. The gross body starts to "disintegrate" in perception
5. Eventually: the entire body felt as subatomic particles arising and passing — a direct experience of impermanence

### Defilements Arise Within, Not Outside

> "If a defilement such as anger arises, one always tries to find the external cause... But the cause of your anger, the cause of your misery, is not outside."

The external event is only a link; the defilement arises internally. Once this link is observed directly, understanding grows and the meditator learns to remove the internal cause rather than fighting with external situations.

## Notable Quotes

> "One must observe pure breath, natural breath, as it comes in, as it goes out. Just continue to observe and everything regarding the body and the mind will become clear at the experiential level."

> "The object of meditation should be natural respiration only... By the observation of pure respiration, one can reach the unknown field where things work on their own."

> "What does one gain by [reading about subatomic particles]? But if one experiences this truth..."

---

## Key Analogy

**The Two Banks of the River**: The known field (this bank) and unknown field (other bank) cannot be joined by prayer — only by crossing via the bridge of natural breath observation. No one can reach the other bank simply by wishing for it.
