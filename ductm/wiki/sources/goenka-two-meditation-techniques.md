# The Buddha's Two Main Meditation Techniques

## Source Metadata

- **Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Translated and adapted from *Kyā Buddha Dukkhavādī The?* (first published in Nepal, May 2000)
- **Significance:** Comprehensive treatment of both Vipassana and Mettā Bhāvanā as the Buddha's two principal meditation methods; includes moving case studies of women liberated through Vipassana; full Pali text of mettā radiation

---

## The Two Principal Techniques

To live a happy and harmonious life and to eradicate all misery, the Buddha taught many meditation techniques. The two principal ones:
1. **Vipassana** — eradicates defilements of craving and aversion through mastery of mind on the basis of morality and experiential wisdom
2. **Mettā Bhāvanā** — loving-kindness meditation; radiating compassionate goodwill to all beings

---

## Vipassana: Two Case Studies

### Kisā Gotamī
For years she suffered from childlessness. When her only son was born, she was overjoyed. The son died of snakebite after a few years — she was plunged into extreme grief. When she met the Buddha and learned Vipassana, she was liberated not only from grief over her son's death but from all suffering of repeated births and deaths. She spent the rest of her life helping other suffering women.

### Paṭācārā
Daughter of a rich Sāvatthī family. Ran away with a servant in passion. On her journey back to her parents, she lost her husband, both sons, and discovered her mother, father, and only brother had all died in an earthquake — the same night. Completely insane with grief, she met the Buddha. Through Vipassana she was liberated from all the suffering of the cycle of birth and death. She began serving others and helped many lead happy lives.

## Vipassana in Contemporary Context

- Prison courses: thousands of inmates burning with revenge. At the end of 10-day courses, "they proclaim their acceptance of their faults with tears in their eyes; instead of smoldering in thoughts of vengeance, they start generating loving-kindness for everyone."
- Terminal illness: serious meditators face cancer without strong painkillers, choosing full awareness over unconsciousness, dying with peaceful alert minds.

## Mettā Bhāvanā: The Practice

Mettā is practiced *after* Vipassana has begun eradicating defilements. The meditator first observes for himself:

> "Whenever he generates hatred or animosity against anyone, at that very moment, he becomes agitated. He loses his peace of mind."

This direct observation makes mettā not mere wishful thinking but a natural flowering:

> *"Ahaṃ avero homi, abyāpajjo homi, anīgho homi, sukhī attānaṃ pariharāmi."*
> "May I be free from animosity, free from hatred, free from anger. May I experience peace within myself."

---

## Scope of Mettā Radiation

The article includes the full Pali text of mettā radiation covering:
- All directions: East, west, north, south, above, below, and all intermediate directions
- All categories: all sentient beings, animals, humans, non-humans, those born and those in the womb
- The wish: *"Averā hontu, abyāpajjā hontu, anīghā hontu, sukhī attānaṃ pariharantu"* — Be free from animosity, hatred, anger. May they all experience peace.

---

## Seven Benefits of Practicing Mettā

1. One sleeps contentedly
2. One gets up happily
3. One does not have bad dreams
4. One's face acquires a glow
5. One is liked by many
6. One dies with an alert mind full of peace and happiness
7. One gets liberated from the cycle of becoming
