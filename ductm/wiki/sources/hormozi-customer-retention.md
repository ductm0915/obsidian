# How to Get Your Customers to Stay FOREVER

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Platform:** YouTube / Skool platform learnings  
**Type:** Tactical guide — 13 retention tactics for recurring revenue businesses  

## Overview

Hormozi shares 13 retention tactics derived from observing 22M+ users on Skool. Core thesis: reducing churn from 20% → 10% doubles LTV per customer. Growth = new members minus churned members.

## Platform Benchmarks

- **Platform average:** 80% monthly retention (20% churn)
- **Best groups:** <10% churn (90%+ retention)
- **Poor groups:** <70% retention (30%+ churn)
- Rule: if 3+ out of 10 people leave monthly, retention is the highest-ROI fix

## The 13 Retention Tactics

### 1. Track Joins vs. Cancels Monthly
Label as growing / flat / declining. Force honest assessment.

### 2. Use Retention Benchmarks
80% platform avg, 90%+ best-in-class. Know where you stand.

### 3. Churn Drops Dramatically with Time
- Month 1–3: 20%+ churn (highest)
- Day 90: drops to ~10% (first major cliff)
- Month 6: drops to ~2% (5x LTV increase)
- Tactic: focus on *"how do I get people past day 90?"* not *"how do I lower churn?"*

### 4. Ask Cancelers Why They Left
DM churned users. Tag reasons in spreadsheet (price, overwhelm, missing feature, wrong expectations). Review top patterns every 20 cancellations. Fix top 1-2 reasons.

### 5. Ask Best Members Why They Stayed
Identify longest-tenured, most engaged. Ask *"what made you stay?"* Then do more of that one thing. Subtraction beats addition.

### 6. Tier Pricing Based on Evidence
Price objections → create lower tier for non-ideal customers. If non-ideal say it's expensive, ignore (price acts as filter).

### 7. Overwhelm Is the #1 Churn Driver
> "You didn't just add features — you gave them more reasons to cancel."
- Planet Fitness case: stripped to 10–15% of facility, charged less, beat full gyms
- Community deleted content → churn went from 30% → 5% (6x LTV)
- Ask: *"If I had to delete everything, what one thing would you fight for?"*
- Metric: **value per second, not seconds of value**

### 8. Design for Extremes
- Busy path: weekly digest, can get value from a distance
- Power user path: optional deep dives and side quests

### 9. Move the Ladder Closer to the Front
Front-load the sticky experience. If calls are stickiest, put the best call replay in onboarding. Pin fast wins so new members hit value within 24 hours.

### 10. Show Up Daily
Owner presence = significantly higher retention. Daily alarm, clear DMs, read complaints every morning. Delete cancers (toxic members).

### 11. Weekly Call + Recording
Live voice/face deepens relationships. Recordings let busy people keep up. Default cadence: one big thing per week.

### 12. Add Annual Plan with Discount
Annual buyers churn less (decide once vs 12 times). Standard discount: 16–17% (buy 10 get 2). Implement immediately — expect meaningful chunk to take it.

### 13. Build Belonging: 10 True Regulars + 1-on-1 Relationships
- Employee with one real friend stays 5x longer at job (Leila Hormozi)
- Identify 10 model citizens → DM → spotlight → invite to contribute → matchmake with new members
- Onboarding: introduce new members to regulars like a party host

## Key Quotes

> "The businesses that win simply make all the things that suck about achieving the good thing go away."

> "People who are using your services are not full-time using your stuff. Think about value per second, not seconds of value."

> "Most people think growth looks like a growth curve. It really just looks like a flat retention curve — and then it keeps growing."
