# Dhamma-Cakka: The Turning of the Wheel of Dhamma

## Source Metadata

- **Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Discourse on the occasion of Dhamma-cakka-pavattana pūrṇimā (the full moon of the first discourse)
- **Significance:** Most systematic exposition of the three-level Dhamma framework (pariyatti/patipatti/pativedha) and the three types of paññā; explains why intellectual engagement alone is a "golden trap"

---

## Central Warning: The "Golden Trap"

> "We spend all our lives in uncertainties of religious and philosophical doctrines, debating, gossiping, refuting, elaborating, suggesting, understanding, hearing, reading, writing, speaking... we consider only this as our success in life."

The trap: *Knowing* Dhamma and *understanding* Dhamma are not fetters in themselves. But becoming *content* with knowing and understanding — mistaking intellectual proficiency for spiritual attainment — is "such a fetter that it becomes very difficult to obtain relief from it."

---

## The Three Types of Paññā

| Type | Pali | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| Acquired wisdom | *Pariyatti* | Gained by reading or listening |
| Intellectual wisdom | *Cintāmayī paññā* | Gained by reflecting and finding it logical |
| Experiential wisdom | *Bhāvanāmayī paññā* | Gained through direct experience; this alone leads to liberation |

The first two types are not useless — they motivate one toward practice. But actual benefit comes only through the third type: direct experiential knowledge that purifies the mind of craving, aversion, ignorance, envy, jealousy, ego, fear.

---

## The Three Levels of Dhamma

| Level | Pali | Role |
|-------|------|------|
| Theoretical knowledge | *Pariyatti Dhamma* | Motivates; provides direction |
| Practical path | *Patipatti Dhamma* | Walking the Noble Eightfold Path; the essential connecting link |
| Direct realization | *Pativedha Dhamma* | The goal — Nibbāna; reached only through patipatti |

> "Without this Patipatti Dhamma, the links of Pariyatti Dhamma and Pativedha Dhamma are not joined together."

The Noble Eightfold Path (*sammā diṭṭhi, sammā saṅkappa, sammā vācā, sammā kammanta, sammā ājīva, sammā vāyāma, sammā sati, sammā samādhi*) is entirely covered by sīla, samādhi, and paññā.

---

## "Vijjācaraṇasampanno" — Perfect in Theory AND Practice

The Buddha's quality of being "perfect in both knowledge and conduct" (*vijjācaraṇasampanno*) — "He preached what he practiced himself" — is what made him the Tathāgata venerated by the whole world.

> "The whole life of the Buddha was a life of practice. First of all, he himself walked on this path and thereafter taught the people this natural and easy path of Vipassana meditation."

---

## Homage to the Teaching Lineage

Goenka pays deep respect to the unbroken chain of teachers from the Buddha through Sayagyi U Ba Khin who preserved the practical aspect of Dhamma across 2,500 years.

> "With deep sense of respect and gratitude towards the entire chain of Teacher to disciple tradition extending from the Buddha to Sayagyi U Ba Khin, let us nurture and nourish the Teacher to disciple tradition."

---

## The Call to Personal Practice

The true way to honor the occasion of Dhamma-cakka-pavattana pūrṇimā:

> "Make Dhamma as a part of our life, as a part of our habit! On the eve of Dhamma-cakka-pavattana pūrṇimā this is a true way of paying homage to the Teachers."
