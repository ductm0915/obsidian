# Message by Goenkaji to Prisoners — Tihar Prison, January 1994

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Message recorded by Goenka and played for participants of Vipassana courses held at Tihar Prison, New Delhi, January 1994
- **Audience:** Tihar Prison Vipassana course participants
- **Length:** Short — a concentrated blessing and exhortation (~400 words)
- **Key theme:** The mental prison of defilements is greater than any physical prison; liberation from defilements is the true freedom

---

## Summary

This brief but concentrated message was designed to be played at the beginning of Tihar Prison courses. It opens with acknowledgment: "A life of bondage is full of sorrow. There is no greater suffering than bondage and no greater happiness than freedom."

But immediately Goenka reframes the situation: there are two jails. The outer physical jail is known and visible. But the inner jail — the prison of one's own negativities — is universal. Everyone, inside and outside prison walls, is imprisoned by their own anger, hatred, greed, craving, clinging, passion, attachment, and ego. These defilements overpower us instantly when they arise, making us their prisoners.

The message: the prisoners at Tihar are uniquely fortunate to have the opportunity to learn this technique. People outside prison "have the time" but never actually go for a course — some problem always comes up. The prisoners can go for a course "comfortably."

Liberation from defilements produces a natural state of "infinite love and compassion; full of joy, sympathetic joy; and full of equanimity, perfect equilibrium of mind." This is the fruit promised to those who practice diligently.

---

## Key Claims

1. **The mental prison is universal and worse than physical imprisonment.** "We have become the slaves of our own anger, hatred, ill will, animosity; slaves of our defilements."
2. **Defilements overpower us instantly.** "Any defilement that arises in our minds overpowers us — makes us its prisoner so quickly!"
3. **Liberation from defilements = universal happiness.** Whether Hindu, Muslim, Jain, Buddhist, Christian, Jew, Sikh, Parsi — anyone freed from defilements experiences peace and harmony.
4. **The technique is scientific, result-oriented, non-sectarian.** "So scientific, so result-oriented, so non-sectarian."
5. **The prisoners are fortunate.** They have the concrete opportunity to take a course. People outside with freedom often never make time for it.
6. **A pure mind is naturally full of mettā.** When freed from impurities, the mind naturally fills with "infinite love and compassion."

---

## Notable Quotes

> "A life of bondage is full of sorrow. There is no greater suffering than bondage and no greater happiness than freedom."

> "We have become the slaves of our own anger, hatred, ill will, animosity; slaves of our defilements of craving, clinging, greed, passion, attachment, ego."

> "Anyone who is imprisoned in the bondage of defilements is bound to suffer. And anyone who comes out of this bondage starts to enjoy peace and harmony."

> "May a new era start in your lives. May Dhamma bring you full liberation. May you all enjoy real peace, real harmony."
