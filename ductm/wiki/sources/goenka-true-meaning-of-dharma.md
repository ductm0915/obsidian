# The True Meaning of Dharma

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Article; connects Sanskrit/Pali concepts with Sikh scripture (Guru Granth Sahib)
- **Series:** Discourses on Dhamma, the Law of Nature
- **Key Theme:** Dharma = the universal law of nature; "saddhamma" (true Dharma) transcends all sects; parallels between Buddha's teaching and Guru Nanak's path

---

## Core Argument

The word "Dharma" has been misused in India for 1,500–2,000 years because people began using it to mean "sect" or "religion." The original meaning was the universal law of nature. The Buddha always added only the adjective "truth" — calling it *saddhamma* (true Dharma) — which prevented it from becoming sectarian.

> "The Buddha never added any sectarian adjective to Dhamma. The only adjective that he added was 'truth.' Therefore, he used the term *saddhamma* (true Dharma) for his teaching."

---

## Historical Blunder: "Dharma-nirpeksh"

When India became independent, the constitution used the term *Dharma-nirpeksh* (non-Dharmic) to mean "secular." Goenka personally drew the attention of Seth Govinddas (a major Hindi writer who helped translate the constitution) to this error. The correct word should have been *panth-nirpeksh* (non-sectarian), not *Dharma-nirpeksh*. Eventually the correction was made, though many leaders still use the wrong term.

**Lesson:** Confusing Dharma with sect corrupts both language and governance.

## Saddhamma and Satanāma

The Buddha is known as *saccanāma* — "one whose name means truth," "one whose mind is always immersed in truth." Later, *saccanāma* became *satanāma*, used by Bhakti saints as a synonym for God:

- Kabirdas: *"Hota punīta japai satanāmā..."*
- Dariya Sahab: *"Kahai dariyā satanāma bhajana binu..."*

Guru Nanak used *satanāma* in its truest meaning: one who *practices* truth, who has attained the state of *satanāma* through their own efforts.

## Parallels with Guru Nanak's Teaching

Goenka draws extensive parallels between the Buddha's Dhamma and Guru Nanak's path:

| Guru Nanak's Teaching | Buddhist/Vipassana Parallel |
|----------------------|----------------------------|
| *Kiva saciyārā hoīye* — become truthful at the level of personal experience | Direct experience as the basis of paññā |
| *Socai soci na hovaī* — ultimate truth cannot be obtained by thinking alone | Cannot attain via *suta-maya* or *cinta-maya paññā* |
| *Cupai cupa na hovaī* — silence at vocal level ≠ silence of mind | True silence requires Vipassana, not just external quiet |
| *Bhukhiyā bhukha na utari* — craving cannot be satisfied by indulging | The Buddha: be *bhattamattaññū* (know exactly how much food you need) |
| *Hukamai andari sabhu ko* — the law is within every individual | Dhamma is realized only through direct personal experience |
| *Haumai ehā jāti hai* — ego is the origin of karma and of birth | Ego (*ahaṅkāra*) as the root of saṅkhāras and rebirth |
| *Haumai dīrgha rogu hai* — ego is a dangerous illness, remedy found within | The remedy lies in inner observation, not external |

## The Concept of Khalsa

Guru Gobind Singh: *"Khālisa tāhi nakhālisa janai"* — one who knows the ultimate truth is known as *Khalsa*. The word *Khalsa* = *sekkha* in Pali = one who trains in the path.

*Wāhaguru* = the true guru who trains such pure-minded Khalsa disciples.

## Mind-Matter and No-Self

When one observes the truth of mind-matter objectively:
- One experiences clearly that the mind-body phenomenon is impermanent — arising and passing away every moment
- One realizes this ephemeral mind-matter cannot be "I," "mine," or "my soul"
- Without the false notion of "I" and "mine," craving and aversion cannot arise
- This leads to liberation from the snare of illusion (*māyā*)

---

## Key Pali Terms Introduced

- **Saccanāma / Satanāma:** One whose name/mind is truth; "the truthful one"
- **Saddhamma:** True Dharma — without sectarian adjective
- **Sekkha / Sikkha:** One who is training on the path to liberation
- **Yathā bhūta:** As it is — the actual truth as it manifests in the present moment
- **Saccakhanda:** The truth pertaining to the entire mind-matter aggregate within oneself

---

## Notable Quotes

> "Whenever a sectarian word is added to Dharma, it corrupts its meaning."

> "The entire path of liberation is based on truth. Truth is eternal and not dependent on external conditions."

> "When one understands the true nature of mind and matter by personal experience, one does not generate craving or aversion, which is dependent on the false notion of 'I' and 'mine'."
