# Source Summary

Alex Hormozi's deep-dive into **Reframing** — the 1–3 sentences said after a prospect says anything but "yes" that increase the likelihood the next thing you say gets them to buy. This is an expanded and detailed treatment of the [[aaa-method]] (3A Framework) with 5 ethical rules for use.

## Key Claims & Arguments
- The most productive salespeople (highest close rate) ask for the sale more times than anyone else, but do so without decreasing rapport.
- **Reframing** is the core skill that allows repeated asks without feeling combative.
- The person asking the questions controls the conversation — never answer a trap question directly; reframe it first.
- The 3A Framework (Acknowledge, Associate, Ask) is the structure for executing reframes.
- Five rules for ethical use:
  1. **Never answer a question you don't know the answer to** — reframe instead.
  2. **Never answer an unasked question** — don't volunteer objections.
  3. **The nastier the prospect, the nicer you get** — escalate kindness, not combativeness.
  4. **Match or exceed emotional intensity** — validate at the level of their concern before reframing.
  5. **Retain childlike curiosity at all times** — seek to understand, not to win.
- Universal response: "What are you most afraid of?" — gets to the heart of the issue fastest.
- "You don't close sales by being right. You close sales by making the prospect right."
- Labeling technique: give the prospect a positive identity label ("you seem like a smart person") then associate closing with that label.
