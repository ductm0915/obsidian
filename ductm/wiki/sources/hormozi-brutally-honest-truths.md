## Summary

A 15-point distillation of Hormozi's mindset philosophy — drawn from personal experience building Acquisition.com (portfolio companies generating hundreds of millions in aggregate revenue). Heavy on agency, pain tolerance, and redirecting psychological energy from avoidance to action.

---

## The 15 Truths

### 1. Pain is the Price of Progress
Growth requires stretching past your comfort zone — physically, organizationally, emotionally. Elite athletes don't get stronger during easy workouts. "You cannot both want progress and live an easy life."

### 2. Happy But Not Satisfied
You're allowed to be happy *before* hitting your goal — just not satisfied (complacent). Haitian proverb: *"Behind mountains are more mountains."* The work works on you more than you work on it. Hormozi's thesis from his retirement year: **hard work is the goal itself**, not the outcome it produces. → [[frustration-tolerance]]

### 3. Ignore Critics
"Most people are fat, poor pansies — don't listen to them." The average person tries to keep you average. Importantly: people want to see you fail because your failure **justifies the risk they chose not to take**. Listen to people closest to your goals, not closest to you. "Your critics are going to eventually die and their opinion won't matter then — which means it probably doesn't matter now."

### 4. Selective Productivity
Commitment = elimination of alternatives. Focus = doing the fewest non-essential things possible. The productivity fallacy: people want to *add* things to become more productive, but focus is about *getting rid* of everything that isn't the thing. Wall/ladder analogy: spreading effort across 4 ladders = 4 incomplete rungs; concentrated effort = one ladder over the wall.

### 5. Fear vs. Regret
Change is scary but so is regret. **The life you live depends on the fear you fear most.** More successful version of you has this: fear of regret > fear of rejection. Hormozi took 6 months to quit his consulting job; the thing that got him over: "I knew that if I never took the jump, I would be ashamed of myself." Plan B decomposition → [[fear-vs-regret]]

### 6. Persistence Creates Timing
"You can time everything perfectly if your intention is to never stop." Opportunities are distributed randomly across your lifeline. If you work the whole time, you'll always be ready. Perfect timing = myth. Perfect preparation = achievable. Starting *is* the perfect condition. → [[persistence-creates-timing]]

### 7. Envy vs. Effort
"If people worked for their goals as hard as they envy others for achieving them, they would have already achieved them." Three hard truths: (1) no one is doing as well as you think, (2) you win by growing into your potential, not beating others, (3) your biggest threat is a mediocre version of you. → [[envy-vs-effort]]

### 8. Hard Conversations Create Opportunities
"Everything you want is on the other side of a few hard conversations you've been putting off." People either grow into their potential or live the same six months over and over — the difference is hard conversation frequency. Short pain vs. long pain matrix: fear = short pain → fulfillment = long gain. Regret = short gain (comfort) → long pain. → [[hard-conversations]]

### 9. Endure
"The fastest way to become the person you want to be is to put yourself in a situation where you have no choice but to become them." Hormozi signed a $5K/month gym lease with $5K in savings — necessity is the mother of invention. "When the only choice was die or endure, humans tend to endure."

### 10. Results > Excuses
Your excuses may be valid. Doesn't matter. Quote from Leila: *"It's not your fault, but it is still your problem."* Excuses = a "permission slip for mediocrity" — wanting respect without the outcome. "I will do what is required" — not your best, what's *required*, because required may be better than your best right now. → [[my-fault-philosophy]]

### 11. The Hard Way Is the Easy Way
"The hard way is the easy way because the easy way never gets you there." All shortcuts that actually work get immediately adopted by everyone and stop being shortcuts. "It's rarely one big thing" — it's the neverending onslaught of small difficulties. Paul Graham: *"If you want to make a million dollars, you have to endure a million dollars worth of pain."* → [[the-hard-way-is-easy]]

### 12. Don't Give Away Your Power
"What offends you controls you." Whatever you blame is what holds power over your life. Example: attributing relationship failures to a parent → that parent controls your love life. Shaq analogy from Hormozi's father: *"Do you think Shaq, when he was 7 feet tall, was like 'It wouldn't be fair for me to play basketball'? Play the cards you're dealt."* The day you choose to control yourself = the day you divorce your results from the viable reasons you have not to win. → [[my-fault-philosophy]]

### 13. Rejection vs. Regret
"Losers fear rejection, winners fear regret." Greatness rejects all first-time applicants. Getting a job = many interviews, many rejections = one sale. Business works identically. Champions don't love winning — they **hate losing more**, and they expected to win because of preparation level. → [[fear-vs-regret]]

### 14. Consistency Beats Talent
"You can beat most people at anything if you just stick with it for a year." You can become competent at nearly any skill in 20 hours — most people wait 10 years to log the first 20 hours. "What makes things hard isn't complexity, it's consistency." Method for overcoming self-doubt: habituation, not affirmation. Expose yourself repeatedly until the nervous system adapts. → [[volume-negates-luck]]

### 15. Have No Shame
Distinction: **guilt = breaking your own rules; shame = breaking other people's rules.** "If you have no money and want to make money, you should have no shame — knock, call, email, text, DM, ask." Shame was "invented by people who have to prevent people who have not from taking action." Fear only exists in the vague — spell out the specific downside and it loses power. Asymmetric risk-reward of trying: worst case = no + experience; best case = result. → [[shame-vs-guilt]]
