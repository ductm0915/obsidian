# Experiential Wisdom — Paññā

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Discourse on paññā (wisdom) in the Vipassana path
- **Series:** Discourses on Paññā (Wisdom)
- **Key Theme:** How paññā through Vipassana practice eradicates dormant defilements (*anusaya kilesa*), leading through progressive stages to full liberation

---

## Core Argument

Paññā (*direct knowledge*) is not book knowledge or intellectual understanding. It is knowledge gained through direct personal experience. Through constant Vipassana practice, paññā eradicates dormant defilements (*anusaya kilesa*) at the deepest level, leading through progressive stages of liberation, culminating in arahantship.

> "Paññā is direct knowledge. It is the excellent knowledge that leads us to liberation."

---

## The Dormant Defilements (Anusaya Kilesa)

A crucial distinction:
- **Surface defilements** can be eradicated by various meditation techniques
- **Dormant defilements** (*anusaya kilesa*) — the roots of unwholesome saṅkhāras leading to lower realms — lie dormant in the *deep unconscious mind*

Even if surface defilements are cleared, one does not attain *total liberation from the lower worlds* unless the anusaya kilesa are also eradicated.

---

## How Paññā Eradicates Anusaya

The mechanism:
1. Meditator maintains *objective awareness* toward all bodily sensations
2. Develops progressively stronger ability to be free from craving and aversion
3. Experiences the truth: every sensation is impermanent and constantly changing (*anicca*)
4. Does not generate craving or aversion toward sensations — equanimity strengthens
5. Deep-rooted anusaya start surfacing one by one
6. Meditator remains equanimous as they surface → they get *uprooted and eradicated*

> "Uppajjitvā nirujjhanti, tesam vūpasamo sukho" — When saṅkhāras arise and get eradicated, one experiences the happiness of their cessation.

---

## Stages of Liberation

| Stage | What Happens | Result |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Stream-entry (*sotāpatti*) | First experience of nibbāna; liberation from lower-world anusaya completed | Maximum 7 more rebirths; all in higher worlds |
| Subsequent stages | Further eradication of remaining saṅkhāras | Fewer rebirths; higher states |
| Arahantship | Complete liberation from all saṅkhāras | Last birth declared |

### The Arahant's Declaration:
- *"Ayam antimā jāti"* — This is my last birth
- *"Natthidāni punabbhavoti"* — Now there shall be no more rebirth

Quote: *"Visankhāra gatam cittam tanhānam khayamajjhagā"* — The mind becomes free of all saṅkhāras and is totally purified.

When *tanhā* is eradicated: *"Khīnam purānam navam natthi sambhavam"* — All old saṅkhāras eradicated; new saṅkhāras cannot be generated.

---

## Self-Reliance: No Liberation from Outside

> "Liberation cannot be bestowed by any god, goddess, Brahmā or God Almighty."

The Buddha's own words: *"Tumhehi kiccam ātappam, akkhātāro tathāgatā"* — You have to strive for your liberation; the Buddhas only show the path.

A true spiritual teacher is only a "path-giver, not a giver of liberation."

*"Attā hi attano nātho"* — one is one's own master.
*"Attā hi attano gati"* — one makes one's own destiny.

---

## Practical Insights That Arise Through Practice

1. **On addictions:** Attachment is not to intoxicants, gambling, or sexual misconduct themselves — but to the *sensations* they produce. Observing those sensations objectively frees one from addiction.

2. **On revenge:** 
*"Pubbe hanati attānam, pacchā hanati so pare"* — A thought of revenge first destroys one's own peace and harmony before it can harm the other. The meditator's body immediately starts burning when generating a revenge thought.

This direct experiential knowledge transforms the person: "In place of hatred and ill-will, he generates love and goodwill."

---

## Notable Quotes

> "By practicing Vipassana a meditator gains complete liberation from the saṅkhāras of the lower worlds."

> "When all the dormant defilements (leading to the lower realms) have come to the surface and have been eradicated, one attains full liberation from the lower worlds."

> "Such a person is known as an arahant; one who is totally liberated from rebirth."

> "His mind is suffused with loving kindness and compassion. His life is filled with peace and happiness. His life becomes fruitful."

> "This is the invaluable legacy which Buddha gave to the suffering world."
