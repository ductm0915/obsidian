# If You Want 2026 to Be the Best Year of Your Life

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** Very long-form video/compilation transcript (~600K chars)  
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-10

## Summary
An extended compilation of Hormozi content covering his early poverty/struggle era, foundational mindset shifts that drove his exit from minimum-wage gym ownership to $250M+ in revenue. The central philosophy: **accountability is the foundation** — "my fault" precedes every other skill. Alongside: brute force work ethic when you have no leverage, the impossibility of blaming others while trying to improve, and practical survival strategy when poor.

## Core Themes

### "My Fault" as Foundation
The bedrock principle before any tactic or skill. Two words: **It's my fault.** 
- Whatever you blame is where you cast power. "I can't succeed because of X" means X controls you.
- You cannot simultaneously desire improvement and maintain blame. Choose one.
- "The first rule of business is to own everything." Not ownership of assets — ownership of agency.
- → [[my-fault-philosophy]]

### Brute Force When You Have No Leverage
When broke, you have only time. No money, no leverage, no audience, no team. The only variable you can turn up: **sheer volume of hours and effort**.
- "You have to will yourself out of it."
- Two lives simultaneously: normal job to eat today + side hustle to eat tomorrow.
- Pulling the future toward you through glass — painful at first, gets less coarse over time.

### Delay Pain, Not Gratification
Poor people delay pain (take pleasure now, suffer later). Successful people delay gratification (take pain now, enjoy later). 
- The body/bank account you have now is the result of pain you postponed from the past.
- "Always tomorrow" → a pattern that guarantees stagnation.

### Victim Frame Taken to Extreme
Hormozi's technique: take the victim narrative to its logical conclusion. "You're right — you'll never succeed. Now what?" The absurdity of being right vs. being rich forces a choice.
- He used this live on a woman at a conference who listed 6 reasons his framework wouldn't work for her business.

### Anger as Fuel
Early on, Hormozi used pure anger at the prospect of being wrong, at the people who doubted him, as fuel — not passion or purpose. Passion came later. The fuel in the early stage was: *"I refuse to let them be right about me."*

## Notable Quotes
- *"Whatever you cast blame to is where you also cast power."*
- *"You can either be right or you can be rich."* (Zig Ziglar paraphrase)
- *"One cannot both desire to get better and maintain blame. You have to pick."*
- *"The sacrifices that I encourage people to take are not sacrifices I did not make."*
