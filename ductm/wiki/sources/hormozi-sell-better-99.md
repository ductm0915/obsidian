## Key Claims

### Philosophy
- Selling is "a transference of belief over a bridge of trust" — belief AND trust are both required.
- People want to buy; the salesperson's job is to help their logical brain justify what their emotional brain already decided.
- Selling happens during the whole conversation; **closing** is what happens after you ask for the sale.
- "No" is not failure — it is expected. If they could decide alone, they wouldn't need you.
- "The person who cares the most about the prospect wins the sale" — including the prospect themselves.
- Closing is a dance, not a fight. Sell from the back foot.

### On Objections
- All objections come from **three core distortions** (from Dr. Albert Ellis's CBT): Circumstances, Others, Self. See [[blame-distortions]].
- These manifest as five surface objections: **Time, Money/Value, Fit, Authority, Avoidance**. See [[aaa-method]].
- **Obstacles** (before you ask for the sale) are easier to handle than **objections** (after you ask). Kill zombies early.
- "The reason you're telling yourself not to do this is the reason you should do it."

### On Performance
- The single greatest predictor of total sales output is **total available time slots** (availability > all other factors).
- Top salespeople listen **twice as much as they talk**.
- Only three things should come out of a salesperson's mouth on a call: **questions, restatements, anecdotal stories**.
- "Conviction will correct your tone" — belief fixes tonality faster than training.
- Referral close rates run at 80–90%; simply asking "who do you know?" can increase sales by 50%.
