# The 6 Levels of Making Money

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** YouTube video transcript  
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-10

## Summary
Hormozi maps all ways of making money onto a 6-level hierarchy based on a single variable: **how much risk you bear**. Compensation is directly proportional to perceived risk. The highest earners are those who bear risk that others *perceive* as high but that they know is manageable — i.e., mispriced bets.

## The 6 Levels

| Level | Structure | Who | Risk Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | I work, then you pay | Employee (W2) | Lowest risk, most reliable; trade risk for certainty |
| 2 | You pay as we go | Independent contractor | Parallel payment; 5x annual turnover vs employee |
| 3 | You pay, then I work | Service business with upfront pricing | Higher leverage; surgeons, lawyers with retainers |
| 4 | When X happens, you pay | Revenue share, equity, outcome-based | Divorced from time; payoff on skill, not hours |
| 5 | When nothing happens, you still get paid | Insurance model | Buy and sell risk itself |
| 6 | No matter what, you pay | Government/tax collector | Monopoly on force; control the money flow |

## Key Claims
- The average business owner doesn't make significant money; median profit ≈ minimum wage in California.
- Employees have 3.9-year average tenure vs. 3–12 months for independent contractors → 5× annual turnover for vendors.
- "Mispriced bets" — Howard Marks made billions buying distressed debt that others perceived as risky but he knew was not.
- Level 4 (outcome-based): decouples compensation from time invested, which only works when you're skilled.
- Level 5 (insurance) is "reverse lottery" — everyone pays in for the bad outcome so the company profits during normal times.
- Payment processors always get paid because they **control the money flow** — practical version of Level 6 for businesses.
- Royalties = "paid to the royals" = come off the top line; always superior to profit share since profit is manipulable.
- Bezos: "Humans overestimate the downside and underestimate the upside." Asymmetric bets favor the risk-taker.

## Notable Quotes
- *"You will be compensated in proportion to the risk you're willing to take — specifically the perceived risk you're willing to take."*
- *"The people who do this well have it be very risky for other people and not risky at all for them."*
- *"Outsized returns come from betting against conventional wisdom."* (Bezos)
- *"In baseball, the most you can score is 4 runs. In business, every once in a while you can score 1,000 runs."*
