# Hatred Never Ceases through Hatred — Post-9/11 Message, 2001

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Message written in the immediate aftermath of the September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks in the USA
- **Audience:** Vipassana community and general public
- **Length:** Short — a focused message (~400 words)
- **Key theme:** The proper response to hatred is mettā (loving-kindness), not counter-hatred; the Dhamma provides a concrete path

---

## Summary

Goenka begins by acknowledging the tragedy and expressing compassion for all affected. He frames the attacks as an "attack on the right of men and women to live in peace and safety." But rather than calling for retaliation, he argues for a Dhamma-based response.

The central teaching: On September 11, "ordinary superficial reality was stripped away and we saw the harsh truth" — the three characteristics (dukkha, anicca, anattā) were suddenly undeniable. Vipassana trains one to face exactly this kind of reality with balanced mind.

Goenka urges that mettā be extended even to the perpetrators — not as moral superiority, but because they have already harmed themselves most deeply: "They have thrown away their precious lives for a mistaken conception of religion." Counter-hatred would compound the tragedy.

He explicitly defends Islam against conflation with terrorism, citing the Quran's prohibition of compulsion in religion and quoting Surah Ha-Min on responding to vice with virtue — demonstrating his commitment to finding universal ethical common ground across traditions.

---

## Key Claims

1. **The Dhamma law: hatred cannot quench hatred.** "Never in this world is hatred quenched by hatred. By love alone is it quenched; this is an eternal law." (Quoting Dhammapada 5)
2. **The terrorists harmed themselves most.** By generating extreme hatred and throwing away their lives, the perpetrators are the primary victims of their own negativity.
3. **September 11 as a teaching of the Three Characteristics.** The sudden destruction revealed dukkha (suffering), anicca (impermanence), and anattā (lack of control) with undeniable force.
4. **Government measures are necessary but insufficient.** Security measures give only short-term benefits; "Goodwill and compassion alone can remove the roots of all such acts of hatred."
5. **Islam is not to blame.** "It would compound the tragedy to regard their distorted views as being representative of Islam." True followers of Islam are peace-loving.
6. **The Quran supports the same teaching.** Goenka quotes the Quran: "Virtue and vice can never be the same; therefore repel vice with virtue... enemies will then become friends, and hatred forever will disappear."
7. **A pure mind is invulnerable.** "With such a mind we can weather even the greatest vicissitudes of life. We can live without fear, and in peace with all others."

---

## Notable Quotes

> "Never in this world is hatred quenched by hatred. By love alone is it quenched; this is an eternal law." (Buddha, Dhp. 5)

> "On September 11, within a few moments, ordinary superficial reality was stripped away and we saw the harsh truth: that suffering is inescapable, that everything we cling to is bound to pass away, that we have no real control over what happens."

> "Our mettā must extend even to those very deluded beings who perpetrate such attacks. They have thrown away their precious lives for a mistaken conception of religion, and in harming others they have much more seriously harmed themselves."

> "The Quran tells us, 'Virtue and vice can never be the same; therefore repel vice with virtue. In the end this will prove better, for enemies will then become friends, and hatred forever will disappear.'"
