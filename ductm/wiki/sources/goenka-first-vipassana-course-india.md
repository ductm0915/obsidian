# First Vipassana Course in India

A personal memoir by [[sn-goenka]] recounting the events surrounding the first Vipassana course held in India (July 3–13, 1969 in Bombay), after approximately 2,000 years without the technique on Indian soil.

## Summary

On June 20, 1969, Sayagyi U Ba Khin appointed Goenka as a Vipassana teacher and sent him to India to revive the lost technique in its country of origin. The journey from Myanmar to India was fraught with obstacles: Goenka's immediate family in India had mostly joined the Ananda Marg movement, his Burmese-refugee relatives were destitute, and the atmosphere was filled with despair. He nearly returned to Myanmar without holding a single course.

The emotional core of the article is a powerful meditation experience in which, surrounded by darkness and doubt, Goenka made a total surrender to Dhamma: "What is to be will be. I am dedicated to Dhamma. Let Dhamma do as Dhamma wishes." Immediately after, the despair dissolved and a young man named Vijay Adukia appeared, offering a venue at the Pancayatiwadi Dharmashala in Bombay.

One by one, students committed to the course — including Goenka's own parents, who had been caught between filial loyalty and the Ananda Marg path of their other sons. The first course ran July 3–13, 1969, with fourteen students including Goenka's parents. His mother remained for all ten days and "benefited greatly."

The article closes by noting that students who experienced the first course immediately began organizing subsequent ones, and "after 2,000 years, the pure stream of Dhamma again began to flow in India."

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **The "clock of Vipassana has struck"**: U Ba Khin's prophecy — that at this moment many people with abundant pāramitā have been born in India and will naturally be drawn to Vipassana — is repeatedly invoked to explain obstacles and their eventual resolution.
- **Earthquake as Dhamma omen**: When Goenka landed in Calcutta, a tremor was felt across northern India, which he recalls as a possible sign of the country's joy at regaining Dhamma.
- **Equanimity under despair**: The pivotal meditation experience — total surrender to Dhamma — is described as the moment at which the darkness dissipated and practical solutions began appearing. This embodies [[continuity-of-practice]] and the equanimity teaching.
- **Dhamma works through people**: "Just as a site had been found, a few people would also be prepared to participate" — each logistical need was met as it arose, interpreted as Dhamma finding its own path.
- **Family resistance as compassion test**: Goenka's mettā meditation toward his Ananda Marg brothers, sending them goodwill without expectation, preceded the course going forward without opposition from them.
- **Debt to parents**: Repaying the debt to his aged parents by placing them on the path of Dhamma was a central motivation for Goenka.

## Notable Quotes

> "'The clock of Vipassana has struck.' Its revival is bound to come in India and it will happen now." — Sayagyi U Ba Khin

> "What is to be will be. I am dedicated to Dhamma. Let Dhamma do as Dhamma wishes. If I am a worthy vessel of Dhamma and if I have a sufficient store of previous pāramitā, the darkness will dissipate."

> "Both parents participated in the first course from July 3 to 13, 1969, along with twelve other students. My mother remained for the full ten days of the course and benefited greatly."

> "These difficulties were only for the first course. Immediately afterwards, students who had taken part and experienced the benefits began to organise and to serve courses, one after another."

> "May the Dhamma-Ganges of Vipassana gain more and more strength, and continue to benefit people throughout the world."

## Key Dates

- June 20, 1969: Goenka appointed as Vipassana teacher by U Ba Khin
- June 21, 1969: Goenka arrives in India (Calcutta; earthquake tremor noted)
- July 3–13, 1969: First Vipassana course in India, Bombay, 14 students
