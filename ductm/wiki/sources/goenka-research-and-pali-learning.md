# Words of S.N. Goenka on Research and Pāli Learning

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker/Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Short address/statement on the role of Pāli learning and research in the Vipassana movement
- **Series:** Discourses on Tipiṭaka — Pāli
- **Format:** Very brief — about 200 words; a concentrated statement of principle
- **Closes with:** *Bhavatu sabba maṅgalaṃ* — "May all beings be happy"

---

## Core Argument

The words of the Buddha (pariyatti) spread so that they can support meditation practice (patipatti) — not as an end in themselves. The primary aim must always remain patipatti: walking the path step by step. Pāli learning is recommended for every Vipassana student, but only as a support to deepen practice. The ultimate goal remains personal liberation from suffering.

---

## Key Claims and Arguments

### Pariyatti Must Serve Patipatti

> "The pariyatti [theory], the publication of pariyatti, the research in pariyatti should not become our main aim. Our main aim will always be patipatti."

Being satisfied only with reading the Buddha's words without taking steps on the path is self-harm — the same as knowing a medicine exists but not taking it.

### The Role of the Buddha's Words

The words of the Buddha, when encountered alongside active meditation practice:
- Encourage
- Guide
- Inspire

But they must be accompanied by personal meditation. Without the experiential context, even inspiring words remain an intellectual exercise.

### Pāli as Personal Inspiration

> "I recommend that every student of Vipassana learns at least basic Pāli, the words spoken by Buddha. I speak from my own experience. Every word of the Enlightened One is so inspiring, provided you continue your meditation practice."

Personal research into the truth inside — about the interaction of mind and matter, how ignorance causes reaction and wisdom enables liberation — is how the Buddha's words are properly used.

### The Medicine Metaphor

Suffering is all around. The Buddha's Dhamma is a medicine that cures. The words point to the medicine, but the medicine must actually be taken:

> "May this wonderful medicine of the Buddha help the suffering people to come out of their illness, to come out of their misery. May the light of Dhamma spread around the world, dispelling the darkness of ignorance."

### The Loss and Recovery of the Words

An implicit historical thread: the Buddha's words were lost in many countries. Gratitude is due to those countries (Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Thailand, etc.) that maintained them in pristine purity. This preservation enables the current revival.

---

## Notable Quotes

> "If we remain satisfied only with reading the words of the Buddha, but do nothing to take steps on the path he taught, then again we have started harming ourselves."

> "You have to make your own research of the truth inside, research about the interaction of mind and matter inside — how out of ignorance one keeps on reacting, how in wisdom one comes out of it."

> "Bhavatu sabba maṅgalaṃ" — closing blessing
