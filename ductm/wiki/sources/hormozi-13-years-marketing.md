# 13 Years of Marketing Advice in 85 Mins

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** Marketing masterclass — 13 frameworks  
**Context:** 9 companies built and sold; last company sold for $46.2M; 1M+ books sold  

## Overview

Comprehensive marketing masterclass. Core philosophy: *"Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction."*

## Key Frameworks

### 1. Start Low / Free
Get flow first. Donate-to-charity model got Hormozi first 13 clients. Gym Launch: flew out to gyms for free, fronted all costs → 5,000+ licensed locations. Raise prices 20% every 5 customers until conversion × price peaks.

### 2. [[more-better-new]] Framework
Three-stage scaling sequence:
1. **More** — volume first. Most entrepreneurs do 1/100th the volume needed.
2. **Better** — optimize once flow exists. ROI of "better" > one unit of "more."
3. **New** — only when more + better are maxed. Cost of change is guaranteed; return is not.

> *"90% of your time should be doing more and better of what already works."*

### 3. Optimize Front to Back
80% of advertising value = headline / first 5 seconds. David Ogilvy: *"After the headline, you've spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar."*

- Dean/Larry King: $250K failed ad → re-recorded first 30 seconds → $100M in sales
- Changed one landing page headline → 62% more booked calls
- **50 hooks per session:** 40 proven + 10 experimental
- **Clear beats clever. Deletion beats explanation.** Target 3rd-grade reading level.

### 4. LTV:CAC — The Only Metric
- **LTV** = lifetime *gross profit* per customer (not revenue)
- **CAC** = all-in acquisition cost (sales + marketing + software)
- **Minimum:** 3:1 ratio; *"stupid money"* comes from 30:1–200:1 windows
- Rule: collect CAC back within first 30 days of customer relationship
- Starbucks: $14K LTV → can justify massive spend per customer

### 5. [[kaleidoscope-process]] — Squeeze Winners
When a winning ad is found: change backgrounds, props, re-enact, reorder, add filters/effects, change pacing, add music, use same hook with different body. 10 variations from one winner.

> *"Squeeze the living hell out of the winners."*

### 6. [[four-ways-to-advertise]]
Only 4 channels:
- **Warm Outreach** (1:1, knows you)
- **Cold Outreach** (1:1, strangers)
- **Content** (1:many, knows you)
- **Ads** (1:many, strangers)

Revenue scaling:
- $0–6 figures: 1 product, 1 avatar, 1 channel
- 7 figures: same (consistently)
- 8 figures: 1 avatar, 1 channel, 2 products
- Multi-8 figures: 2 channels, 1 avatar, 2 products

### 7. State the Facts and Tell the Truth
Track results → improve results → use real stats as "truthful clickbait."
Measurement itself improves performance (measurement-as-intervention effect).
4 variables: **Who × What × When × Conditions** (fewer conditions = more compelling).

### 8. Say What Only You Can Say
*"Do epic stuff. Talk about what you did."* "How I" beats "How to."
Demo in advance. [[value-per-second]]: same result in half the time at twice the price.

### 9. Expand Market (Five Ways)
1. Up market, 2. Down market, 3. Adjacent, 4. **Narrower** (usually best), 5. Broader

Vista PE case: identify top 20% customers → make them 100% of business → 5x revenue same size.

> *"Better creates bigger. Bigger creates bloat."*

### 10. Value Equation
See [[value-equation]]. Dream Outcome × Likelihood / (Time Delay × Effort).

### 11. Give Away Secrets, Sell Implementation
Make free content better than competitors' paid content. CTA = personalization ("apply this to your business"), not "more stuff." 99% of free consumers never buy — they build your reputation.

### 12. All Advertising Works
It's efficiency, not platform. [[five-levels-of-awareness]] (Eugene Schwartz):
Unaware → Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware → Most Aware.

> *"The size of the plane is correlated with the length of the runway."* Cold/expensive audiences need more advertising runway.

### 13. Remind More Than Teach
Human condition unchanged for thousands of years — no truly new content. Henry Ford: *"When are we stopping this ad? I'm sick of it." "We haven't started yet."* Same concept, different formats: book, video, story, case study.
