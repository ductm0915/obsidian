# Sam Ovens — How To Make Decisions

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]]  
**Type:** YouTube video (22-point framework)  
**Core theme:** Your life is the sum of your decisions. Making better decisions requires reducing decision load, thinking critically, and applying mental models.

---

## Summary

Sam presents a 3-part, 22-point decision-making system.

**Part 1: Reduce Load**
The first step in making better decisions is making fewer of them. Decision fatigue is real — cognitive energy depletes on mundane choices, leaving none for critical ones.

Five tactics:
1. **Make less decisions** — sacrifice deliberately; say no more than yes
2. **Do less stuff** — eliminate activities; true sacrifice is saying no to things you genuinely want
3. **Make one decision that removes future decisions** — wear the same clothes daily (Sam: identical t-shirts in different colors, same shorts, same socks, one pair of shoes); cook weekly instead of daily
4. **Automate decisions with heuristics** — simple rules that eliminate contemplation: "if X happens, I always do Y"
5. **Routinize everything possible** — fixed wake/sleep/gym/meal/work schedule eliminates thousands of daily micro-decisions

**Part 2: Think Critically**
Once load is reduced, apply rigorous thinking:
- Weigh options on short-term and long-term timelines (the "effect horizon" from [[sam-ovens-long-term-thinking]])
- Map pros and cons for each option across time frames
- Identify the actual goal, then work backward from it
- Beware first-order thinking (see only immediate consequences)
- Always ask: what are the second-order, third-order consequences?

**Part 3: Apply Mental Models**
Mental models are cognitive lenses that reveal patterns invisible to unequipped minds. Sam uses:
- Long-term vs. short-term (effect horizons)
- Systems thinking (inputs → processes → outputs → feedback)
- Inversion (find failure modes, then flip them)
- First principles (break down to constituents, reassemble optimally)
- Second-order consequences
- Supply/demand, game theory, opportunity cost

Heuristics examples:
- "Never check email before 12pm" eliminates hours of reactive morning sessions
- "If I'm going to sleep later than 1am, just sleep and do it tomorrow" — don't sacrifice sleep for one night's work
- Dress code: same items daily → zero wardrobe decisions

---

## Key Claims

1. **"Your life at this moment is the sum total of all your choices."** Decision quality = life quality.

2. **Decision fatigue on trivial things depletes the capacity for critical ones.** Most people exhaust their cognition on socks and sandwiches.

3. **True sacrifice is required.** "If you say yes most of the time and you're looking for opportunities to say yes, you suck at sacrifice."

4. **Heuristics (simple rules) eliminate the need to think about entire categories of decisions.** Sam recommends developing a personal library of them. They make decision-making "rapid" because you're not contemplating — you're executing a rule.

5. **One uniform decision removes future decisions forever.** Same-shirt wardrobe, batch-cooked weekly meals, fixed routine — these compress infinite future decision points into one past decision.

## Notable Quotes

> "If you have more than five options for a decision, you haven't broken it down into small enough pieces."

> "You're good at sacrifice when you say no more than yes — and you're truly good when you say no to things you genuinely want."

> "I have heuristics — little rules in my head for dealing with things — and I never disobey the rule. Decision-making becomes rapid because I just use it like a default."
