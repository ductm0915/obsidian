# If I Wanted to Become a Millionaire In 2024 — Full Blueprint

## Summary
A structured "how to become a millionaire" blueprint organized into 3 levels: (1) Fundamentals of wealth creation, (2) Tactics for first million, (3) Staying and getting richer. The most detailed compilation of Hormozi's financial frameworks in one place. Covers: earn vs. own, focus vs. diversification, the 5-stage opportunity cycle, hungry crowd selection, 1-avatar-1-product-1-channel, the value equation, 8 ways to advertise, the rule of 100, paying yourself, and scientific goal-setting.

## Level 1: Fundamentals

### What Is a Millionaire?
Net worth (assets minus liabilities) > $1M *excluding primary residence*. Distinction: liquid net worth (cash/tradable) vs. net worth (includes illiquid business equity).

### Earn vs. Own
- **Earn your way:** $200K/year × 10 years, saving 100% = $1M. Slow, taxed, drains living expenses.
- **Own your way:** Build a business to $250K/year profit → sell at 4× multiple = $1M. Time is an ally, not an enemy.
- Hormozi: first cash million at 27; crossed $100M net worth at 31.

### Focus Over Diversification
- Diversification = "a hedge against ignorance." (Warren Buffett)
- Most people spread across 7 income streams simultaneously — nothing fills.
- The wealthiest people went all-in on one thing → overflowed → then diversified.
- You make wealth by focusing; you maintain wealth by distributing.
- → fits [[problems-vs-missed-opportunities]] and existing ideas about focus

### 5 Stages of Opportunity
(Same cycle as in other Hormozi sources)
1. Uninformed Optimism
2. Informed Pessimism
3. Valley of Despair ← most quit here
4. Informed Optimism
5. Achievement

Most newcomers cycle through 1-2-3-1-2-3 forever, never reaching 4-5.

### Long Game — Build Right, Not Fast
Stack blocks slowly vs. building quickly and hitting a ceiling. Fast vertical builds can't scale past a certain height. Sustainable businesses need a wide foundation of satisfied customers and referrals before accelerating marketing.

## Level 2: Tactics

### Find a Hungry Crowd
"It's not about the hot dogs — put your stand in front of a starving crowd." Four required conditions:
1. **In pain** (desperate need, not just want)
2. **Can buy** (purchasing power)
3. **Easy to target** (findable)
4. **Growing market** (tailwinds, not headwinds)
- → [[hungry-crowd]]

### 1 Avatar + 1 Product + 1 Channel
Simplest formula to $2M+. Don't add complexity early. If under $1M: don't add products or channels — just sell more of the same thing to more of the same person.
- → [[one-avatar-product-channel]]

### The Value Equation (same as $100M Offers)
- [[value-equation]]: (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) / (Time + Effort)
- The "most expensive" part of an offer is often not money but hidden time/effort costs.

### 8 Ways to Advertise
Four you do yourself (Core 4): Warm Outreach (1:1 warm), Content (1:many warm), Paid Ads (1:many cold), Cold Outreach (1:1 cold).
Four others do for you: Referrals, Employees, Agencies, Affiliates.
- Rule: pick ONE for your first million. Don't add channels before you've maxed your first.

### Rule of 100
100 primary actions per 100 days = first desired result. "Count in hundreds." Focus on input metrics, not output metrics.
- → [[rule-of-100]]

### Paying Yourself
- 33% model: 33% to self, 33% to growth (new locations/hires), 33% reserve.
- Watermark model: keep a minimum in the account; distribute the rest above watermark.
- Taking cash out *forces* cash flow discipline and improves decision-making quality.

### Scientific Goal Method
1. What problem are we solving? (Identify constraint)
2. Hypothesis: "If X, then Y"
3. How do we measure X and Y?
4. Did we do it? Did it work?

Make inputs the goal, not outcomes. Inputs are 100% controllable.
