# May Sayagyi's Dhamma Mission Be Fulfilled

A discourse delivered by [[sn-goenka]] at the opening address of the Centenary Seminar "Dhamma Joti" in Burma (January 9, 2000), honouring the centenary of Sayagyi U Ba Khin's birth. Representatives from approximately thirty countries attended.

## Summary

The discourse traces the full lineage of Dhamma transmission from the Buddha to Sayagyi U Ba Khin, with particular attention to Myanmar's role as custodian of pristine Dhamma over the centuries. Goenka presents the historical chain of transmission through six key figures before arriving at Sayagyi:

1. **Tapassu and Bhalluka** — first lay disciples, brought relics and Dhamma to Myanmar
2. **Arahant Gavampati** — taught Dhamma to King Singharaja (Siharaja) of Suvannabhūmi, 7 years after parinibbāna
3. **Arahants Sona and Uttara** — post-Third Council; established both pariyatti and patipatti
4. **Arahant Dhammadassi (Ashin Arahan)** — 800 years ago; recognized corruption in northern Myanmar (the Aris), convinced King Anorata (Anuruddha) to preserve the Tipitaka; later lived and taught at Sagaing Hills
5. **Ledi Sayadaw** — 150 years ago; opened Vipassana to laypeople; trained Saya Thetgyi
6. **Saya Thetgyi** — trained Sayagyi U Ba Khin
7. **Sayagyi U Ba Khin** — held the vision of Dhamma returning to India and spreading globally; sent Goenka as his representative

Goenka recounts the emotional reality of Sayagyi's wish: wanting to go to India himself but being unable; training Goenka as his representative; Goenka's own adhitthāna (strong determination) to receive Indian citizenship or a passport endorsement within ten years. The dramatic resolution: Indian citizenship received three days before the ten-year deadline, passport issued exactly on the tenth anniversary of Goenka's arrival in India.

The central appeal: the best expression of gratitude to Sayagyi is not praise but living and serving as his representative — teachers should always feel "The Dhamma is being given by Sayagyi U Ba Khin; I am simply a representative."

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Gratitude expressed through living purity, not praise**: "Merely singing his praises does not express it rightly. Even if we spend an entire lifetime singing his praises, we will still not fulfill a proper expression of gratitude."
- **The teacher-disciple transmission is a continuous Dhamma river**: From Gotama Buddha → arahant disciples → Dhammadassi → Sagaing teachers → Ledi Sayadaw → Saya Thetgyi → U Ba Khin → Goenka → current teachers.
- **Goenka as representative, not originator**: "People should not have the wrong impression and think I am giving the Dhamma. No, my Teacher is giving Dhamma to the world; I am simply a representative." This framing prevents ego from inflating.
- **Adhitthāna as Dhamma tool**: The sacca kiriyā determination — "at the completion of ten years of Dhamma service in India, may I receive Indian citizenship" — is presented as a precise Dhamma mechanism that was fulfilled to the day.
- **Shwedagon as Dhamma vibration center**: The relics of the Buddha enshrined by Tapassu and Bhalluka continue to benefit all meditators who sit there.

## Notable Quotes

> "When I went, all was darkness before me. I wondered, 'How will people have enough confidence to stay with me for ten days?' But with Sayagyi U Ba Khin's blessing, the first ten-day course was held within one month of my arrival in India."

> "I request all those whom I have trained as teachers to have this same feeling whenever they give Dhamma: The Dhamma is being given by Sayagyi U Ba Khin; you are all simply his representatives."

> "The only way to really pay respects to Sayagyi U Ba Khin is to live the life of pure Dhamma and to be a good example to others."

> "May the Dhamma vision, the Dhamma mission of that great teacher Sayagyi U Ba Khin be fulfilled. May more and more people around the world benefit by this wonderful Dhamma."
