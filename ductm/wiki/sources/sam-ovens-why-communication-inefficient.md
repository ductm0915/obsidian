# Sam Ovens — Why Communication Is Inefficient

**Author:** [[sam-ovens]]  
**Type:** YouTube video  
**Core theme:** Communication lines grow exponentially as teams grow linearly. This kills efficiency and destroys net productive hours.

---

## Summary

Sam discovered this problem when Consulting.com grew to 40+ employees and paradoxically slowed down versus when it had fewer people. The counterintuitive truth: more people can mean less output.

**The Formula:**
Communication lines = n × (n-1) / 2

Examples:
- 1 person: 0 lines
- 2 people: 1 line
- 3 people: 3 lines
- 5 people: 10 lines
- 6 people: 15 lines
- 10 people: 45 lines
- 11 people: 55 lines → 0% net efficiency
- 14 people: 91 lines

**The math at 50 hours/week per person, 90% of lines = communication hours:**
- At 1 person: 100% efficiency, $38/net hour
- At 5 people: 82% efficiency
- At 10 people: 18% efficiency
- At 11 people: 0% efficiency — zero net productive hours, all time spent communicating

This means in a team of 11, you could pay 11 people and get zero actual work done. The chart shows a catastrophic drop-off at the 5–6 person mark.

**Sam's engineering team example:** When they had too many engineers, adding one more engineer slowed everything down because the communication cost of integrating them exceeded their contribution.

**The solution:** Minimize the number of people, maximize the quality of each person, and minimize coordination requirements between them. Structure the organization so people can work independently with minimal dependencies.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Headcount is a vanity metric; communication lines is the real cost.** A 10-person team has 45 lines. At some communication overhead per line, you've consumed all productive time.

2. **There is a drop-off cliff at 5–6 people.** Below that: manageable. Above that: exponentially harder. Most companies don't know this and keep hiring.

3. **More people can mean less work done.** Sam experienced going slower with 40+ employees than with a small team. This is a predictable mathematical outcome.

4. **The antidote is not better communication tools — it's fewer people who need to communicate.** Slack and email don't solve the problem; they make it more efficient at the margins.

5. **Independent, high-quality people with clear ownership = optimal structure.** Minimize dependencies between people.

---

## Notable Quotes

> "Somehow, some way, we were going considerably slower with more people than we were with less. I couldn't get my head around it."

> "By the time you get to 11 people, you're at zero percent efficiency. All time is absorbed by communication."

> "What you really have to watch out for isn't the number of people — it's the number of connections between the people."
