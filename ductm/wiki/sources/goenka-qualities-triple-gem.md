# Qualities of the Triple Gem

## Source Metadata

- **Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Format:** Short expository article; densely packed Pali terminology with explanations
- **Significance:** Comprehensive breakdown of the specific qualities of the Buddha, Dhamma, and Sangha as defined in the Pali Canon — moves taking refuge beyond devotional act to understanding of qualities

---

## Central Thesis

When taking refuge in the Triple Gem (Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha), one should remember the specific qualities each represents — and work diligently to develop those qualities in oneself. The Triple Gem is defined by universal qualities, not by sectarian identity.

---

## The Buddha: Qualities and Epithets

The Buddha is defined not as a person or name but as a set of qualities:

| Pali Term | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| *Bhagava* | Exalted one; having vanquished craving, aversion, and delusion |
| *Araham* | Conqueror of enemies (mental impurities) |
| *Sammā Sambuddho* | Perfectly enlightened by own rightful efforts |
| *Vijjā-caraṇa-sampanno* | Perfect in both wisdom and conduct |
| *Sugato* | Gone to the ultimate truth; pure in body, speech and mind |
| *Loka-vidu* | Knower of worlds through personal experience |
| *Anuttaro purisa-damma-sārathi* | Unsurpassed charioteer of tameable men |
| *Satthā deva-manussānaṃ* | Teacher of gods and men |

Additional epithets: *jina* (conqueror), *mahāvīra* (valorous), *sabbaññu* (omniscient), *tathāgata* (truth-discoverer), *maha-karuṇiko* (highly compassionate), *vita-rāgo/doso/moho* (free from passion/aversion/delusion), *khinasavo* (exhausted all defilements).

**Key point:** "Anyone who acquires these qualities will become a Buddha. And whoever is a Buddha will have these qualities." Buddha is a quality-set, not a proper name.

---

## The Dhamma: Six Attributes

| Pali Term | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| *Svakkhato* | Well-explained |
| *Sanditthiko* | Can be experienced in this life |
| *Akaliko* | Gives immediate results |
| *Ehi-passiko* | Invites people to come and see |
| *Opanayiko* | Every step takes one toward full liberation |
| *Paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhī* | To be experienced by each person of average intelligence, for oneself |

The Dhamma is called by many non-sectarian names: *esa dhammo sanantano* (eternal dhamma), *ariyo atthangiko maggo* (noble eightfold path), *saddhammo* (true dhamma), *sukka dhammo* (stainless dhamma), *aggo dhammo* (foremost dhamma), *purano dhammo* (ancient dhamma).

---

## The Sangha: Universal Qualities

The Sangha defined by the Buddha consists of those who, practicing Dhamma, have attained one of the four stages: stream-enterer (sotāpanna), once-returner (sakadāgāmī), non-returner (anāgāmī), or fully enlightened (arahat). Not an assembly of ordinary people.

Qualities of the Sangha:
- *Ahuneyyo* — Worthy of invitation
- *Pahuneyyo* — Worthy of hospitality
- *Dakkhineyyo* — Worthy of offerings
- *Anjali-karaṇīyo* — Worthy to be saluted with folded hands
- *Anuttaraṃ puñña-kkhetaṃ* — Field of merit par excellence
- *Danto, santo* — Temperate, tranquil
- *Virajo, vimalo* — Free from passion, spotless
- *Nippapañco* — Composed, not diffused

---

## The Teaching Conveyed

Such a Buddha teaches: "This is sīla, this is samādhi, this is paññā. After sīla is perfected, samādhi proves highly beneficial. After samādhi is perfected, paññā proves highly beneficial. After paññā is perfected, the mind becomes free from all impurities."
