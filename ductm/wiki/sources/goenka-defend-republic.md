# How to Defend the Republic! — S.N. Goenka

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Essay/discourse on the Buddha's seven instructions to the Vajji (Licchavī) republic and their relevance to modern India
- **Key theme:** Internal disunity — not external enemies — is the primary threat to a republic; the Buddha's practical governance wisdom; Vipassana as a tool for social integration

---

## Summary

This discourse presents the story of the Vajji republic (Licchavīs of Vesālī) and the Buddha's "Seven Instructions for an Unassailable Republic" — practical governance wisdom that kept the Vajjians invincible as long as they followed it, and led to their destruction when they abandoned it through internal discord.

The historical framing: The Buddha knew that King Ajātasattu of Magadha was planning to attack the Licchavīs. His response was to recite the seven principles publicly (through his attendant Ānanda) knowing that Ajātasattu's minister Vassakāra was present — ensuring the enemy heard that as long as the Vajjians maintained unity, they could not be conquered. Vassakāra then spent years sowing internal dissension before Magadha could attack. The moral: internal discord is the fatal weakness.

Goenka applies this to contemporary India, identifying two "lethal poisons" — sectarianism (treating one's sect as Dhamma) and caste discrimination (assigning value by birth rather than conduct). Both divide society and undermine the republic's strength.

The solution: universal non-sectarian Vipassana, which dissolves caste and sectarian barriers at the experiential level. People of all backgrounds sit and eat together during courses without discrimination.

---

## The Seven Instructions for an Unassailable Republic

1. **Maintain unity and regular assembly.** Alert rulers who meet regularly can repel external threats.
2. **Meet together, rise together, act in unity.** During crisis, every member rushes to the assembly immediately, and decisions are unanimous.
3. **Do not transgress ancient principles of governance and justice.** No arbitrary taxes; seven levels of appeal before any criminal punishment — people's cooperation depends on their confidence in the justice system.
4. **Revere and honor elders.** Benefit from the experience of retired leaders; don't ignore their wisdom.
5. **Protect women; do not abuse power over them.** Rulers who abuse power over women create enemies who will betray the republic in times of danger.
6. **Protect places of worship of all sects.** Give adequate support and protection to all religious groups; people whose places of worship are protected become allies, not enemies.
7. **Protect and support saints and arahants.** Where saints are respected, true Dhamma teaching flourishes; where they are disrespected, immorality increases and the country weakens.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Internal discord destroys republics; external enemies cannot.** Vassakāra's deceit succeeded only because the Licchavīs let it. A united republic is unconquerable.
2. **Sectarianism corrupts social harmony.** "The people of different sects can live together harmoniously in any country as long as they do not consider any sect as Dhamma."
3. **Caste by birth is lethal poison.** "The demon of belief in the caste system has also been dangerous... This is the lethal poison that has filled the blood vessels of the nation."
4. **Status by conduct, not birth.** If a person's social standing is determined by morality rather than birth, society can continuously improve; people have incentive to become good.
5. **Vipassana dissolves sectarian and caste barriers.** Universal non-sectarian practice — sīla, samādhi, paññā, and mettā — creates actual integration, not merely principled statements.

## Notable Quotes

> "Sects may be different and may have different aspects; but Dhamma is always indivisible. Let the knowledge of the true nature of universal indivisible Dhamma spread among the people."

> "One should not be considered high or low just because of being born from the womb of one's mother. If a person commits unwholesome actions, if he is immoral, then he is wicked, unrighteous."

> "The poison flowing through the blood vessels of the country since such a long time will turn into nectar." (On replacing caste discrimination with conduct-based status)

> "Like the belief in sectarianism, the demon of belief in the caste system has also been dangerous to the security of the country in the past, it is dangerous today also and will continue to be dangerous in the future."
