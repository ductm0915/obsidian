# Yoga — As Seen in the Light of Vipassana — S.N. Goenka (Bombay 1990)

**Type:** Academic lecture  
**Speaker:** S.N. Goenka  
**Date:** April 30, 1990  
**Venue:** Kaivalyadham Yoga Academy, Bombay  
**File:** `raw/articles/Vipassana/S.N Goenka/Yoga - as seen in the light of Vipassana.md`

---

## Summary

Goenka's deepest intellectual exposition: arguing that Patanjali's Yoga Sutra is essentially Vipassana, and that modern yoga (asanas + pranayama as therapy) is a profound degradation of Patanjali's actual teaching. Dense historical and philosophical analysis. Bridges the Yoga and Buddhist Vipassana traditions.

---

## Core Thesis

**Patanjali Yoga Sutra = Vipassana (minus 10-15 anti-Vipassana sutras)**

> *"If you discrete just ten or fifteen sutras out of 155 sutras of Patanjali's treatise, the remaining sutras narrate about pure Vipassana and nothing else."*

And inversely:
> *"Only practice of Vipassana will reveal the real meaning of each and every word of Patanjali Yoga Sutra to the practitioner."*

---

## Key Arguments

### 1. Modern Yoga Is a Degradation

- Modern yoga = asanas + pranayama = **therapeutic exercises**, not spirituality
- Same distortion has happened to Vipassana (used as acupressure/acupuncture in some places)
- Poor Patanjali: reduced from "master of highest spiritual knowledge" to "circus trainer teaching 84 postures"
- Patanjali's actual text: barely 5 sentences on asanas/pranayama; 190+ sutras on *something else entirely*
- Patanjali's actual definition of asana: *"the posture in which one can sit for a long time, steadily and with ease"* — not a gymnastic routine

### 2. The 8 Jhanas (Meditative Absorptions)

Buddha mastered the 8-jhana system from two teachers before discovering Vipassana:
- **Teachers:** Alar Kalam (7 jhanas) → Uddaka Ramputta (8th jhana)
- **Problem:** Even the 8th jhana doesn't eliminate **anusaya kilesa** (dormant defilements)

**Components of jhana (honey bee metaphor):**
| Component | Description | Honey Bee Analogy |
|---|---|---|
| Vitakka | Mind leans toward object via sense organ | Bee flies toward lotus |
| Vicara | Mind sustains on object | Bee hovers searching for honey |
| Piti | Rapture when honey found | Bee feels pleasure of proximity |
| Sukha | Pleasure of tasting | Bee penetrates and tastes |
| Ekagatta | Concentration, complete absorption | Bee trapped in lotus at sunset |

**8 jhana levels:**
1. Vitakka + Vicara + Piti + Sukha + Ekagatta
2. Piti + Sukha + Ekagatta (vitakka/vicara dropped)
3. Sukha + Ekagatta (piti dropped)
4. **Upekkha** + Ekagatta (sukha replaced by equanimity)
5–8. Higher formless absorptions (infinite space → infinite consciousness → voidness → neither-perception-nor-non-perception)

**Critical problem with jhana alone:**
> *"Even attainment of the eighth meditation did not make him free of the fetters lying in the form of defilements over his psychic level."*

Jhanas bury anusaya kilesa deep — like volcanoes dormant but ready to erupt at future conditions.

### 3. Anusaya Kilesa — Dormant Defilements

**Etymology:** anu (follow) + saya (dormant) = *defilements that follow you in dormant state*

- Lie in unconscious mind, travel across rebirths
- Not eliminated by jhana (even 8th) — just buried temporarily
- Why Vipassana is necessary: it **uproots** anusaya, not just suppresses them
- See [[sankara-mental-volition]] for the accumulation mechanism

> *"Those defilements which are lying dormant on our unnoticed part of psychic level and go on flowing along with it without any knowledge to us. They remain as such along with us even after our death and in successive births."*

### 4. Sampajanna — The Key Innovation

**Sampajanna** = simultaneous awareness = the ingredient that makes the difference:
- "In sampajanna, one learns to be witness of each and every phenomenon appearing before him without projecting any explanation to it based on his imagination"
- Watch body sensations exactly as they are, without evaluating good/bad
- The same as Goenka's practice: observe natural breath, observe sensations
- Directly corresponds to Patanjali's observation of natural breath
- When sampajanna is fully integrated: "sampajanna na rincati" (sampajanna is present at all times — sleeping, walking, eating)

**Sampajanna + 4th jhana** = the combination Buddha found that eradicates anusaya kilesa
- Trance without sampajanna = suppresses defilements (jhana)
- Trance with sampajanna = **eradicates** defilements (Vipassana proper)
- Result: asampajanna samadhi = the samadhi of emancipation (nibbana/kaivalya) — "beyond sampajanna" because sampajanna is no longer needed

### 5. Rtambhara Prajna = Bhavanamaya Panna

Patanjali's **rtambhara prajna** (wisdom attained from one's own experience of universal laws) = Buddha's **bhavanamaya panna** (experiential wisdom via practice):
- Both are distinct from sruta-panna (heard wisdom) and cinta-panna (inferred wisdom)
- Both require direct experience — not scripture, not logic
- Only this wisdom permanently changes the psyche
- See [[bhavana-maya-panna]] for deeper analysis

### 6. Kalapa Physics

- Via deep Vipassana practice: gross body → net of finest atomic particles constantly emerging and vanishing
- Sub-atomic particles = sheer vibrations
- Ancient Indian sages called them **kalapa**
- Modern quantum physics: "quanta" — same insight
- *"Sabbo pajalito loko, sabbo loko pakampito"* = the whole universe including body and mind is vibrations only

| Rt (universal law) | Dhamma | Law of nature, timeless |
| Anusaya | Anusaya kilesa | Dormant defilements |

## The Universal Claim

Regardless of tradition name (yoga, vipassana, dhamma, rt):
- **If practiced in purest form → same results**: gradual melting of false notions, increasing revelation of pure reality
- **Why both degenerate**: practitioners stopped doing it and started commenting on it
- Philosophers created hypothetical debates (soul's size, God's nature) instead of experiencing truth
- True method = experience reality "as it is" — not prove preconceived notions

## Notable Quotes

> *"Vipassana means to experience the truth in its ultimate reality. Vivekena pasyatiti vipasyana — to see the truth with a rational outlook."*

> *"Without applying this technique one cannot explore absolute reality. Apparently we can experience only conventional truth. When conventional truth is viewed through Vipassana, absolute truth appears."*

> *"A deep practitioner is successful in experiencing the reactions of mind and body when the sense objects encounter their respective objects."*
