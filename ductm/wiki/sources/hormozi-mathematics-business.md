# The Mathematics of Business, Explained

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** YouTube video/live session transcript  
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-10

## Summary
12 rules of thumb for reading a business's health through paired metrics. Core thesis: business decisions are always about relationships between two numbers — price/close rate, LTV/CAC, rule of 100, lead response time, calendar utilization, payback period, and more. These ratios tell you where you are, where problems live, and where to invest attention.

## The 12 Rules of Thumb

### Rule 1: Close Rate ↔ Pricing Ladder
If you sell via a salesperson/sales call, your close rate is a diagnostic of whether your price is right:

| Close Rate | Likely Underpriced By |
|---|---|
| 80%+ | 3–4× |
| 60–80% | 2–3× |
| 50–60% | 1.5–2× |
| 40–50% | 1.25–1.5× |
| 30–40% | Appropriately priced (with proper sales motion) |
| <30% | Avatar or sales motion problem — fix before touching price |

For SaaS: lower price → higher growth rate, but lower gross margin. Balance both.
→ [[close-rate-pricing-ladder]]

### Rule 2: LTV:CAC by Business Model
The traditional "3:1 is healthy" applies only to fully automated businesses (SaaS with no humans). As you add humans:

| Human Presence | Target LTV:CAC |
|---|---|
| All automated (SaaS) | 3:1 |
| One human in loop (salesperson OR delivery) | 6:1 |
| Two humans (sales + delivery) | 9:1 |
| All human (attraction + sales + delivery) | 12:1 |

Padding rationale: every time you hire a new person, they underperform initially, which temporarily tanks your ratio. You need buffer to absorb the ramp period.

Gym Launch first year: **100:1**. Hormozi has hit 30:1+ four times in his career.

→ Updates [[ltv-to-cac]]

### Rule 3: Rule of 100
100 primary actions, for 100 straight days = first result.
- Apply at launch of any new channel (content, ads, outreach).
- 95% of businesses < $1M → this is almost always the fix.
- Feast/famine cycles = symptom of insufficient volume, not inconsistent lead flow.
- Hormozi's output: ~450 pieces of content/week (~25K/year) vs. 1-person businesses at 365/year.
→ [[rule-of-100]]

### Rule 4: Lead Response Time
Call leads within **60 seconds** of opt-in. Letting leads cool costs 4× more per customer (lower close rates + worse gross margins from competitive shopping).

### Rule 5: 70% Calendar Utilization
Sweet spot for sales team calendars: 60–75% booked (never above 85%).
- Fully booked → lower conversion rates (no time for pipeline follow-up, inconvenient slots, commission breath).
- Under 30% → momentum collapses; every sale feels like life or death.
- At 70%: salespeople do pipeline work between calls, closing the "six-inch putts."

### Rule 6: Payback Period
**Target: 30 days.** Why: most businesses have access to 30-day interest-free credit cards. If CAC is recovered in 30 days, you can grow infinitely without capital.
- Gym Launch: started at 100:1 LTV:CAC; payback period was extremely short.
- → [[payback-period]]
