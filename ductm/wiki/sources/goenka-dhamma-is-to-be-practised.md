# Dhamma is to be Practised

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** First in a series of 44 public talks broadcast on Zee TV (India)
- **Audience:** General Indian public
- **Series:** Discourses on Dhamma, the Law of Nature
- **Key Theme:** Dhamma is useless unless practised; purification of mind is the essence of Dhamma

---

## Core Argument

The central thesis is blunt: Dhamma discussed but not practised is worthless. Like a coat that warms only when worn, medicine that heals only when taken, water that quenches only when drunk — Dhamma benefits only when practised.

> "Kīyai hoī, o kīyai hoī." — One gets the benefit only when one practises Dhamma.

**The simple definition of Dhamma:** development of a pure mind. Impure mind = anti-Dhamma. Pure conduct arises naturally from a pure mind ([[sila-samadhi-panna]]).

---

## Key Claims

1. **Dhamma ≠ ritual.** People confuse Dhamma with fasts, festivals, ceremonies, attire, social customs, and philosophical beliefs. The real meaning is purification of mind.

2. **Universal nature of Dhamma.** Dhamma belongs to everyone. Its benefits are the same regardless of religion, nationality, language, or colour. "Whether one calls oneself Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, Christian, Muslim, Sikh, Parsi or Jew it does not matter."

3. **Dhamma is eternal.** Any person in any period — past, present, or future — who purifies the mind and acts from a pure mind brings happiness to themselves and others.

4. **Impurity punishes immediately.** "As soon as we defile the mind, we have moved away from Dhamma and we receive the punishment — we become miserable immediately."

5. **The technique is Vipassana.** Goenka identifies Vipassana as the Buddha's discovery — the specific scientific technique for purifying the mind at depth, where conditioning takes place.

6. **Sectarianism corrupts Dhamma.** When people fight in the name of religion ("My religion is right, your religion is wrong"), they prove they have not understood Dhamma — because Dhamma, when present, produces only compassion and peace.

---

## Notable Quotes

> "If one does not practise it, how can one derive benefit from Dhamma? Without practice, Dhamma will only remain a subject of intellectual discussion, debates and arguments."

> "Dhamma is always universal, it belongs to everyone; it is boundless and immeasurable. When Dhamma is made limited and confined to a particular sect or group, it is not true Dhamma."

> "It is not possible for one to generate anger within oneself and to experience peace and harmony at the same time."

> "As soon as the mind becomes pure and filled with compassionate love, it will become filled with peace and happiness."

---

## The Coat Analogy

Goenka offers four vivid analogies to illustrate the futility of discussing Dhamma without practising it:
- A man shivering with cold who praises his coat but does not wear it
- A thirsty woman who salutes the water but does not drink it
- A hungry man who praises the food but does not eat it
- A sick person who extols the medicine but does not take it

All four = talking about Dhamma without practising = "great misfortune."

---

## The Sanskrit Saint's Verse

Goenka quotes an unnamed Indian saint:

*"Kathai na hoī, badai na hoī, suṇai na hoī, Kīyai hoī, o kīyai hoī."*

— Merely talking does not help. Merely speaking does not help. Merely listening does not help. One gets the benefit only when one practises.
