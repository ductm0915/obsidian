# Inner Peace for World Peace — UN Millennium World Peace Summit, August 29, 2000

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Address to the Millennium World Peace Summit — 1000 world religious and spiritual leaders, General Assembly Hall of the United Nations, August 29, 2000, under Secretary-General Kofi Annan
- **Audience:** Religious and spiritual leaders from all major traditions
- **Reception:** "Received with repeated ovations"
- **Key theme:** True conversion is from misery to happiness; religion must be defined by non-violence and inner peace

---

## Summary

Goenka addresses 1000 of the world's religious leaders assembled at the UN under Kofi Annan, with the explicit potential for serious disagreement among them. His strategy: identify what all traditions share — and prioritize that.

He redefines "conversion" as the central issue: not conversion from one organized religion to another, but "from misery to happiness... from bondage to liberation... from cruelty to compassion." This reframing disarms most objections from religious leaders concerned about proselytization.

The core argument: world peace requires individual peace. An agitated mind full of anger and ill will cannot contribute peace to the world. "First find peace within yourself." This is not metaphor — it is physics. When one person is peaceful, their surroundings become more peaceful. When they are agitated, the atmosphere becomes poisoned.

He invokes Emperor Ashoka's Rock Edict 12 — a 2300-year-old message of religious tolerance that remains urgent today — and uses it to argue that honoring one's own religion while condemning others ultimately harms one's own tradition.

The most provocative line: "Religion is religion only when it unites; when it divides us, it is nothing."

---

## Key Claims

1. **The needed conversion is internal, not religious.** From misery to happiness, from bondage to liberation, from cruelty to compassion. No "organized religion to organized religion" conversion needed.
2. **World peace is impossible without individual peace.** "If I have an agitated mind, always full of anger, hatred, ill will and animosity, how can I give peace to the world? I cannot because I have no peace myself."
3. **The self-observation technique is a pure science.** The technique of observing mind-matter interaction is non-sectarian, scientific, results-oriented. Not magic or miracle.
4. **The vibration effect.** "When someone generates peace in the mind, the entire atmosphere around that person is permeated with the vibration of peace."
5. **Ashoka's wisdom: honor all religions.** Rock Edict 12: "One should not honor only one's own religion and condemn other religions... his actions injure his own religion more gravely."
6. **Religion = non-violence.** "Let us declare together that we shall abstain from killing, that we condemn violence." This should be the definition of any religion worthy of the name.
7. **Propose "universal religion" at the UN.** A concept of universal religion or non-sectarian spirituality based on non-violence should be endorsed by the UN.

## Notable Quotes

> "Religion is religion only when it unites; when it divides us, it is nothing."

> "Far from being opposed to conversion, I am in favor of it — but not conversion from one organized religion to another. No, the conversion must be from misery to happiness. It must be from bondage to liberation. It must be from cruelty to compassion."

> "If there is no peace in the mind of the individual, I do not understand how there can be real peace in the world."

> "A sage once said, 'A balanced mind is necessary to balance the unbalanced mind of others.'"

> "Someone who honors his own religion and condemns other religions may do so out of devotion to his religion thinking 'I will glorify my religion,' but his actions injure his own religion more gravely." (Ashoka, Rock Edict 12)
