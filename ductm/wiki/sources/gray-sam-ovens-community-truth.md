# The Real Truth About Building a Community — Sam Ovens on Matt Gray Show

Interview on the Matt Gray Show. [[sam-ovens]] (founder of [[skool-platform]]) discusses community design, the transition from [[consulting.com]] to Skool, info vs. software business tradeoffs, and his product philosophy. Hosted by [[matt-gray]].

---

## Key Claims

### Community ≠ Audience
- Social networks were renamed "social media" — they became one-to-many (creator → followers), not interactive
- A real community's strength is measured by **member-to-member relationships**, not creator-to-member relationships
- "Audience builders provide answers; community builders ask questions"
- Mistake: posting constant "value" leaves no room for members to interact — that's why high-posting groups have low engagement

### Fun > Adding Value
- "Adding value" = lecturing people what to do without knowing them — neither fun nor effective
- Get the right people together; fun (and learning) will happen organically
- *"Happy hour > adding value"* — the bar analogy: you go to connect, not to be told a PDF was dropped
- Dentists talking to dentists will naturally discuss interesting dentistry — no prompting needed

### Community Curation is the Product
- **Quality > quantity**: big follower numbers don't mean good community
- Dentist community example: 80,000 practicing dentists, 96% monthly actives, grows via word of mouth only, no marketing
- Key success factors: clear framing (dentists only, verified), no pitching, protected integrity
- Rapid growth without curation = community falls apart

### Global Philosophy + Local Cluster (Religion Model)
- Religion's durability = global value system + local physical church
- Most churchgoers don't go for the priest — they go for their neighbors
- Apply to communities: shared worldview/passion + empowered local leaders/superfans to host meetups
- In-person value will *increase* as things become more digital

### Superfan Empowerment
- Identify top contributors; give them visibility and agency
- Example: Andrew Kirby was doing interesting things with Skool → Sam had him create a course and shouted him out
- Subsidize leaders to run local meetups; reward contribution publicly
- When members see others get rewarded, everyone wants to contribute

### Info Business vs. Software Business
- **Info** (courses, masterminds): fast to start, high-ticket right away, no engineers needed, profitable quickly — but ceiling because you = the product
- **Software**: slow, expensive, needs a whole team, can't charge much early — but can scale to billions of users
- Every dynamic is inverted: info wins short-term; software wins long-term potential
- Key insight: info is *selling yourself through leverage*; ultimately hits a ceiling at scale

### Sam's Transition: consulting.com → Skool
- Started Skool ~3.5 years before this interview
- Leaned down consulting.com (more profitable, smaller, low maintenance) → used cash flow to fund Skool
- Exit decision: spreadsheet of prorated refund cost vs. opportunity cost of brain space freed up
- The math was obvious once modeled

### Intuition vs. Signal Noise
- Social media and strong internet opinions pollute the intuition signal
- Sam avoids Twitter/social because strong opinions → anxiety → losing the thread of what he actually wants to build
- Makes most product decisions from intuition: "psychic connection to the user"
- The intuition signal is *delicate* — constant information input drowns it

### Product Design Philosophy (Tesla / Henry Ford / Steve Jobs)
- Tesla settings menu: everything in one menu, one button → inspired Skool's settings architecture
- Henry Ford: customer asked for a faster horse → Ford identified the real need (A to B faster) → Model T
- Steve Jobs: customers said they'd never pay $1,000 for a phone → they hadn't seen the iPhone
- Pattern: listen to all feedback → find the *root need* → innovate on their behalf, not just fulfill requests

### Skool's Mission
- **1 billion people find community** via **1 million creators** earning full-time livings building communities online
- Economic model: subscription membership (like Substack for writing, OnlyFans for creators)
- 1M creators × average 1,000 members each = 1B people in communities

## Notable Quotes

> "Audience builders provide answers; community builders ask questions."

> "Happy hour > adding value." — fun leads to learning; learning doesn't necessarily lead to fun.

> "You don't go to a bar because some dude dropped a PDF of value nuggets. You go to hang out and have a good time."

> "I'm terrible at doing something I don't want to do. But if I do what I love, discipline doesn't even come to mind — it's just so good I don't want to stop."

> "The intuition signal is quite delicate. Most people have lost theirs."

> "If I'd asked the customer what they wanted they would have said a faster horse." (Henry Ford, cited by Sam)
