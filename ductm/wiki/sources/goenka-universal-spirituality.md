# Universal Spirituality for Peace — UN Millennium World Peace Summit, 2000

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Address to the United Nations Millennium World Peace Summit — 1000 world religious and spiritual leaders. This is a different text from "Inner Peace for World Peace," also delivered at the same Summit.
- **Audience:** Religious and spiritual leaders at the UN
- **Key theme:** The inner essence of all religions is the same; non-violence as the definition of religion; yoga and Vipassana as India's two universal gifts to the world

---

## Summary

This address takes a somewhat different angle from "Inner Peace for World Peace" (also delivered at the Millennium Summit). Where that address redefines "conversion," this one focuses on the shell vs. essence of religion, the universal teaching of non-violence, and a specific call to action: propose a UN definition of "universal religion" or "non-sectarian spirituality" based on non-violence.

Goenka begins with a simple observation: every religion has an outer shell (rites, rituals, ceremonies, beliefs, myths, doctrines) and an inner core (morality, charity, disciplined mind, love, compassion, goodwill, tolerance). These outer shells differ; the inner core is universal. Religious conflict arises when people prioritize the shell over the core.

He cites Emperor Ashoka's Rock Edict 12 — "One should not honor only one's own religion and condemn other religions" — delivered by the King of Oman as a contemporary example of the same wisdom in Islamic context.

India's two gifts to the world: physical yoga (asanas and pranayama) for the health of the body, and Vipassana for the health of the mind. Both are universal and non-sectarian — accepted by people of all faiths without requiring conversion.

Environmental protection is linked to inner purification: "What prevents us from acting on this understanding [to protect the environment] is the stock of mental pollutants, such as ignorance, cruelty or greed."

The concrete proposal: "Under the aegis of the United Nations, let us try to formulate a definition of religion and spirituality highlighting non-violence, and refusing to countenance violence or killing."

---

## Key Claims

1. **Every religion has outer shell and inner essence.** The essence — morality, disciplined mind, love, compassion — is universal. Only shells differ.
2. **Give importance to essence, not shell.** "If proper importance is given to the essence of all religions and greater tolerance is shown for their superficial aspects, conflict can be minimized."
3. **Ashoka's model:** Honor all religions' essence; condemning others damages your own tradition.
4. **India's two universal gifts:** Physical yoga (body health) + Vipassana (mind health). Both non-sectarian, non-proselytizing.
5. **Mind pollution = environmental destruction.** The same ignorance, cruelty, and greed that destroy the environment destroy interpersonal peace. Purifying the mind addresses both.
6. **Leaders must set an example of inner peace.** "A balanced mind is necessary to balance the unbalanced mind of others."
7. **Call for UN definition of religion = non-violence.** A formal definition would exclude violence in the name of religion from being classified as religion at all.

## Notable Quotes

> "Religion is religion only when it unites; when it divides us, it is nothing." (Opening line)

> "Every religion has an outer form or shell, and an inner essence or core... there is an inner core common to all religions: the universal teachings of morality and charity, of a disciplined and pure mind full of love, compassion, good will and tolerance."

> "Ancient India gave two practices to the world. One is the physical exercise of yoga postures (Asanas) and breath control (Pranayama) for keeping the body healthy. The other is the mental exercise of Vipassana for keeping the mind healthy."

> "What prevents us from acting on this understanding [to protect the environment] is the stock of mental pollutants, such as ignorance, cruelty or greed."

> "_Religion sets us not apart; it teaches peace and purity of heart._"

> "Let us declare together that we shall abstain from killing, that we condemn violence. I also urge political leaders to join in this declaration."
