# How to Become a Storytelling Genius (The Addiction Framework)

**Author:** [[matt-gray]]  
**Format:** YouTube video  
**Topic:** 6-part framework for turning stories into profit

---

## Summary

Matt Gray breaks down a six-part storytelling framework focused on making stories addictive and converting them into revenue. Core argument: storytelling is not just a communication tool — it's the full sales funnel (Attention → Trust → Belief → Action). Most founders underuse their own story as a competitive moat.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Open loop = retention.** The Garnic Effect: brains hate unfinished business. Open a loop, delay the closure → people can't leave. Intrigue drives the click; tension drives retention inside the content.

2. **Story is the funnel.** Real buying path: Attention → Trust → Belief → Action — not traffic → calls → sales. Stories are the only mechanism that moves people through all four stages.

3. **Story = unfair moat.** Skims vs. generic shapewear: same product, same factories, Skims wins because of Kim's story. The moat generic brands can't copy is an authentic founder story.

4. **Five-Line Story Framework** — simplified hero's journey for business: [[five-line-story-framework]]
   - The Mirror (reflect their current situation)
   - The Friction (personal cost, not just business problem)
   - The Realization (epiphany no one tells them)
   - The Shift (your transformation)
   - The Invitation (natural CTA)

5. **Tightly coupled CTA.** Generic CTAs jolt people out of the story. A good CTA is inevitable — story opens a loop, lead magnet closes it. See [[coupled-cta]].

6. **Content Waterfall.** Don't create new content daily — waterfall winners. See [[content-waterfall-system]].

## Matt Gray's Personal Story (Bitmaker)

Used as proof of story-as-moat:
- Built Bitmaker (coding bootcamp) → $200K/month in 7 months
- Government raided the business (two agents, Globe and Mail article, accused of running unregistered private career college)
- Faced: 1-year jail + $1M fine
- Response: stayed up 48 hours, emailed 3,000 people personally telling the story
- Result: story went viral, government backed off within 1 week, got the only exemption of its kind in Canada
- 1 year later: trained 2,000 software engineers, business acquired for a life-changing sum
