# How to START a Personal Brand (Full Course)

[[caleb-ralston]]'s course specifically for those starting or restarting a personal brand. In the first 10 months of his own brand: 200K+ followers, 40K email subscribers, 750+ consulting applications.

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Define the Goal First**: Gary Vaynerchuk told Caleb: "Make sure this is what you really want. You might be able to build a great business without ever making content." Not everyone needs a personal brand — define your desired outcome first.
- **Brand Journey Framework**: Same 4-question reverse-engineering process. If making content doesn't accelerate your desired outcome, don't do it.
- **Expert vs. Student (Revisited)**: Students build an Interest Bank (testing/learning). Experts build a [[credibility-bank]]. Pretending to be an expert when you're a student burns trust forever.
- **Build a Brand You Love**: Burnout happens when you build a brand you hate. The "Stoked Filter" — if you wouldn't text the video to your friends, you've built a caricature.
- **Customer > Competitor**: Optimize around your customer's painful problems, not what competitors are doing. Whatever group you optimize for, you attract more of.
- **[[contrarian-belief]] as the 80% Lever**: Three levers to stand out: (1) Contrarian belief (the biggest), (2) Content format, (3) Personality. Use the [[two-column-approach]] to find contrarian takes.
- **Contrarian ≠ Controversial**: Contrarian = genuine belief going against the grain. Controversial = designed to trigger. Top brands avoid controversial people "like the plague."
- **Test and Double Down**: Start with multiple contrarian takes, observe which resonates most, then double/triple down on the winner. Caleb had 3 takes; "optimize for trust over virality" caught fire.

## Notable Quotes

> "If you do more epic stuff you're going to have more epic stuff to talk about."

> "Whatever group you optimize for is the group that you're going to attract more of."

> "Build the brand that excites you... if you wouldn't text your YouTube videos to your friends, you've built a countdown clock to quitting."
