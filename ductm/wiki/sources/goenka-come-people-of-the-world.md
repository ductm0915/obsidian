# Come People of the World

A collection of Hindi couplets (dohas) chanted by [[sn-goenka]] during 10-day Vipassana courses. Originally composed in Hindi, the booklet presents the verses in Roman transliteration alongside English translations. One doha is presented for each of the eleven days of a standard 10-day course (Days 1–11 including Day 0 discourses).

## Summary

The dohas are poetic distillations of core Dhamma teachings used as inspirational recitations throughout a Vipassana course. Each verse encapsulates the essence of whatever is being taught that day, weaving together the practical (breath observation, sensation awareness) and the philosophical (universality of Dhamma, law of nature, karma). The collection is framed as an invitation — "Come, people of the world! Let us walk the path of Dhamma" — making it explicitly non-sectarian and universalist.

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Dhamma is non-sectarian**: "Dhamma is not Hindu or Buddhist, not Sikh, Muslim, or Jain; Dhamma is purity of heart, peace, happiness, serenity." (Day 1) — connects directly to [[universal-dhamma]]
- **[[sila-samadhi-panna]] as the triple foundation**: Sīla is the foundation, samādhi forms the walls, paññā is the roof — a complete structure for liberation (Day 7). The three are described as three rivers converging at a sacred confluence (Days 4, 5).
- **Observing impermanence (anicca) through sensation**: "Observing breath after breath the truth reveals itself. Observing truth after truth you come to Ultimate Truth." (Day 2) — connects to [[anicca-impermanence]]
- **Mind as the root of all action**: "Deeds of body and speech are offspring of the mind. As is the volition of the mind, such will be the fruit." (Day 5) — connects to [[karma]]
- **Equanimity as the art of living**: "Not dancing when pleasure comes, not wailing when in pain, keeping equilibrium with both — this is real happiness." (Day 9) — connects to [[continuity-of-practice]]
- **Egoism as the root of suffering**: "Self-centeredness — this is the root of birth, decay, and death. Unless egoism is removed, the torment and fear of becoming will not end." (Day 6)
- **Mettā radiating outward**: The Day 10 and Day 11 verses culminate in universal goodwill: "May all beings be happy! May each man dwell in Dhamma, may each woman follow Dhamma."

## Notable Quotes

> "Come, people of the world! Let us walk the path of Dhamma. Dhamma is not Hindu or Buddhist, not Sikh, Muslim, or Jain; Dhamma is purity of heart, peace, happiness, serenity." (Day 1)

> "As you observe breath after breath the truth reveals itself. Observing truth after truth you come to Ultimate Truth. Sectarianism is not Dhamma; Dhamma raises no walls. Dhamma teaches oneness, Dhamma teaches love." (Day 2)

> "Sīla, samādhi and paññā — three streams have joined and flow together. By plunging into their confluence one crosses the ocean of suffering." (Day 4)

> "The mind can be wicked or gentle, the mind can be a foe or friend. If the mind is transformed, all is transformed, so make your mind truly pure." (Day 5)

> "Sīla is the foundation of Dhamma, samādhi forms the walls, paññā is the roof — the auspicious dwelling of happiness." (Day 7)

> "There is no disease like craving, there is no frailty like aversion. There is no folly like ignorance, there is no sanity like Dhamma." (Day 8)

> "By plunging deep within, the entire being has become so saturated with the colour of the Dhamma that no other colour can impinge." (Day 10)

> "I pardon all, may all pardon me. All are my friends, I have no enemies." (Day 11)
