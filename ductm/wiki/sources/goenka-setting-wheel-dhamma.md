# Setting the Wheel of Dhamma in Motion

## Source Metadata

- **Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Dhamma letter dated July 15, 1968 — on the occasion of Āsādha-pūrṇimā (the full moon day commemorating multiple key events in the Buddha's life)
- **Significance:** Explores the three-fold significance of Āsādha-pūrṇimā; frames the Buddha's first discourse as a cosmological event reversing the wheel of suffering

---

## Three Occasions Commemorated on Āsādha-pūrṇimā

1. **Conception** — The Bodhisatta descended from the Tusita Deva Realm to enter the womb of Mahāmāyā. (Goenka notes this is as important as birth: the Vinaya Piṭaka calculates the 20-year minimum age for bhikkhu ordination from conception, not birth.)

2. **Renunciation** — Prince Siddhārtha left the household life in search of Enlightenment. Without this going forth, the seed of enlightenment could not have been sown.

3. **First Discourse** — The Buddha set the Wheel of Dhamma in motion for the benefit, happiness, and liberation of countless beings.

---

## The Three Dhamma Births

One is "born" three times:
- At the moment of **conception**
- At the moment of **physical birth**
- When one takes **Dhamma birth** through the practice of Vipassana — by tearing open the veil of ignorance

---

## The Buddha's Joy After Enlightenment

> *"Anekajāti samsāraṃ, sandhāvissaṃ anibbisaṃ, Gahakārakaṃ gavesanto, dukkhā jāti punappunaṃ. Gahakāraka diṭṭhosi, puna gehaṃ na kāhasi, Sabbā te phāsukā bhaggā, gahakūṭaṃ visaṅkhataṃ, Visaṅkhāragataṃ cittaṃ, taṇhānaṃ khayamajjhagā."*

> "I have taken countless births running in search of the creator of this house [body], but all I achieved was misery in every birth. O creator of this house, now you have been seen. All your building materials (saṅkhāras) have been destroyed; another house (birth) can no longer be built by you. The mind has become totally purified."

**Interpretation:** The "creator" is the mind itself — which keeps building new life forms based on saṅkhāras (deep-rooted conditioning). Nibbāna breaks this cycle.

---

## Why the First Discourse Was Difficult

Setting the Wheel of Dhamma in motion required reversing the direction of the fast-rotating wheel of suffering. Goenka uses the analogy of reversing the earth's rotation — causing mountains to crumble, rivers to change course, earthquakes. The Buddha used the unique strength of his ten pāramis to accomplish this reversal.

---

## The Basic Principle of the Wheel of Dhamma

> "If there is misery, then there is a cause of this misery. If there is a cause, then it can be eradicated. If the cause can be eradicated, then there is a way to eradicate it."

All saṅkhāras are impermanent — they arise and pass away. Knowing this, the deathless state of nibbāna — where nothing arises, nothing passes away — can be experienced in this very life.

---

## The Context: Why Brahmā Sahampati Had to Intervene

After seven weeks of bliss following enlightenment, the Buddha hesitated: this subtle Dhamma may be impossible to teach to people so immersed in sensual pleasures and ignorance. Brahmā Sahampati intervened — pointing out that some beings have very thin veils of ignorance and would grasp the Dhamma immediately. The Buddha agreed and set out for Sarnath.
