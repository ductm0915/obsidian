# Vipassana Comes to the West

A personal memoir by [[sn-goenka]] recounting the dramatic sequence of bureaucratic obstacles and last-minute resolutions that enabled him to conduct the first Vipassana courses in Western countries (France, Canada, and England) in the summer of 1979.

## Summary

After years of Vipassana spreading through India and attracting large numbers of Western students, Goenka faced a fundamental legal barrier: his Myanmar passport was not endorsed to travel outside India, and repeated appeals to the Myanmar government (including General Ne Win) were unsuccessful. Western students were increasingly urgent, and a more pressing crisis emerged: some of Goenka's American students, before being fully ripened in the technique, had set up an unauthorized Vipassana centre in the USA — abandoning sīla as the foundation, commercializing teaching (against the tradition), and mixing Vipassana with other techniques, thus polluting it.

This created two urgent reasons to go West: students needed proper Dhamma, and an incorrect version was spreading.

Goenka made a precise adhitthāna (strong determination) via sacca kiriyā (a vow based on truth):

> "If Dhamma wants me to carry on my Dhamma duties only in India then may I remain a Myanmar citizen, and if Dhamma wants me to travel the world for the spread of Vipassana, then at the completion of ten years of my Dhamma service in India, may I receive Indian citizenship."

He applied for Indian citizenship — but the application stalled in the Indian Intelligence Department, which suspected a possible connection to Ananda Marga (the political movement that several of Goenka's brothers had joined). The deadline of the adhitthāna — June 22, 1979 (exactly ten years after his arrival in India) — passed without resolution. Courses in France were already booked and fully paid for.

Then on June 27, 1979, just five days after the deadline, citizenship papers for both Goenka and his wife Ilaichi arrived. By June 28 they had passports. By June 29 they had French visas and cleared all departure formalities. On June 30 they reached Paris; on July 1 the first course in France began — exactly as scheduled.

The behind-the-scenes resolution: Goenka's son Murari and daughter-in-law Vatsala contacted family friend Radhe Shyam Murarka (a Congress MP close to Prime Minister Morarji Desai), who personally appealed to Desai, who immediately cleared the application.

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Pollution of Dhamma by premature/unauthorized teaching is a crisis**: Three specific violations — abandoning sīla, commercializing teaching, and mixing techniques — are cited as threats requiring urgent correction. This establishes a clear standard for what constitutes authentic Vipassana transmission.
- **Adhitthāna works through Dhamma**: The precision of the resolution (citizenship just days after the ten-year deadline, passport within 24 hours, visas and clearance all in a single day) is interpreted as Dhamma choosing a medium and working through multiple people simultaneously.
- **"I am only a medium"**: Goenka's recurrent self-understanding: "It is only Dhamma that is working. Dhamma has to choose some medium to get the work done." This prevents ego inflation and maintains the teacher-disciple humility seen in [[goenka-sayagyi-dhamma-mission]].
- **Dhamma has no geographical barriers**: "The geographical boundaries between countries were merely for political and business purposes. Dhamma cannot have geographical barriers."
- **Myanmar and India both honoured**: Even after taking Indian citizenship, Goenka reframes both nations as partners: one is where Dhamma originated, the other where it was preserved.
- **Three Muraris as Dhamma instruments**: Goenka names the resolution's human chain — Murari, Vatsala, Murarkaji, Morarji Desai — as each receiving Dhamma inspiration at the right moment, demonstrating how Dhamma "uses different people as mediums for different purposes."

## Notable Quotes

> "Due to their own weakness in Dhamma, these students had very wrongly stopped giving due importance to sīla, the foundation for Vipassana. Secondly, they had made teaching the Dhamma a profession, a means of livelihood, and this is totally against the tradition of pure Dhamma teaching. Thirdly, they had started mixing Vipassana with other techniques."

> "My confidence in Dhamma was strengthened by these wonderful events, and also my belief that I am only a medium — it is only Dhamma that is working. Dhamma has to choose some medium to get the work done."

> "May they both share all the merits that I acquire by spreading Dhamma in the world. May they both be happy, may they both be peaceful, may they both be liberated." (on Murarka and Morarji Desai, who had passed away)

## Key Dates

- June 22, 1969: Goenka arrives in India (start of ten-year adhitthāna period)
- June 22, 1979: Ten-year adhitthāna deadline
- June 27, 1979: Indian citizenship granted for Goenka and his wife
- June 28, 1979: Passports issued
- June 29, 1979: French visa and all departure formalities completed
- July 1–11, 1979: First Vipassana course in France (Guyon)
- July 14–24, 1979: Second course in France (Plage)
- July 26–August 6, 1979: Course in Montreal, Canada
- August 9–20 & 21–September 1, 1979: Courses in Godalming, England
