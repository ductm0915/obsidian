# Returning the Gem of the Dhamma to India

A short personal memoir by [[sn-goenka]] about the moment of his departure from Myanmar (Burma) to India in 1969, told through an exchange with a customs official at Rangoon Airport.

## Summary

As Goenka was preparing to board his flight to India after the nationalization of trade and industry in Burma (which forced many Indian residents to return to their homeland), he passed through the customs counter. The official, knowing departing Indians often tried to smuggle gems out of the country, jokingly asked whether Goenka was carrying any valuables. Goenka replied: "Yes, I am carrying a gem."

After carefully searching all his belongings and finding nothing, the nervous official pressed him. Goenka explained that the gem he was taking would repay Burma's ancient debt to India — it had originally come from India, was sorely needed there, and by taking it, Burma would not be impoverished. "I am taking the jewel of the Dhamma."

The official laughed, gave his blessing, and thereafter "always felt very happy whenever he heard about the success of my work."

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Dhamma as an inexhaustible gem**: Unlike material gems, the Dhamma cannot be depleted by being taken away. Burma loses nothing; India regains something it had lost for ~2,000 years.
- **Debt and gratitude across nations**: The framing of "Burma repaying its debt to India" echoes the theme of [[goenka-all-gratitude-to-myanmar]] — that India gave the Dhamma to Myanmar via the Buddha, and Myanmar is now returning it.
- **Playful integrity**: The story works on two levels simultaneously: Goenka literally has no material gems, yet he is truthful in claiming to carry a gem. The honesty is both literal and metaphorical — consistent with the sīla theme running throughout his teachings (see [[goenka-dhamma-decision]]).
- **The teacher's wish as mission**: Goenka frames the return to India explicitly as fulfilling his teacher Sayagyi U Ba Khin's wish.

## Notable Quotes

> "My friend, the gem that I am taking from here will be used to pay back a debt of Burma to India. It originally came from India, and is sorely needed there today. By my taking it from here, Burma will not become any poorer. I am taking the jewel of the Dhamma."

> "Please go ahead — take this gem with you! I am very happy that you will use it to repay this debt." — The customs official
