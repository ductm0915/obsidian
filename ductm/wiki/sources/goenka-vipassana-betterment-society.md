# Vipassana for the Betterment of the Society — IIT Delhi Seminar, April 1994

## Source Metadata

- **Speaker:** S.N. Goenka
- **Context:** Excerpts from opening talk at the International Seminar "Vipassana: Its Relevance to the Present World," Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, April 15-17, 1994
- **Audience:** Academic and intellectual audience at IIT Delhi
- **Key theme:** Vipassana at mass scale; Tihar Prison 1,000-person course as historical turning point; the four-part mind mechanism; individual transformation as the prerequisite for social change

---

## Summary

This is one of Goenka's most wide-ranging addresses, delivered to an intellectual audience at IIT Delhi shortly after the landmark 1,000-person Vipassana course at Tihar Prison. It covers: the social application of Vipassana; historical precedent of Ashoka; the detailed mechanism of mind (four-part model); the non-sectarian nature confirmed at Tihar (Muslims, Sikhs, Hindus, Christians, foreigners from 15 countries all benefiting equally); and the precise technical explanation of how vedanā functions as the "missing link."

The Tihar course is presented as proof that Vipassana at scale is possible. Sayagyi U Ba Khin's prophecy ("one day he will teach a course for one thousand students") was fulfilled. When Goenka arrived at Tihar, over 1,000 prisoners sat before him with "so much sadness, worry and anxiety on their faces... the anger and hatred of these people was palpable." By the last day, "their faces were glowing. There was so much peace on their faces."

The address is particularly strong on the four-part mind model and vedanā as the "missing link":
- Viññāna (consciousness): merely cognizes the event
- Saññā (perception): recognizes and evaluates
- Vedanā (sensation): a neutral vibration becomes pleasant or unpleasant based on evaluation  
- Saṅkhāra (reaction): reacts to the sensation — craving or aversion

The insight: at the apparent level, we react to sense objects. At the actual level, we react to *vedanā*. The reaction happens at the level of sensation, which is why merely understanding intellectually ("I should not react") does not prevent the reaction.

---

## Key Claims

1. **Individual is the key to social change.** "Society is made up of individual human beings. To reform it, each member of society has to be reformed."
2. **Mind creates all pollution.** "As long as the mind remains impure, it will continue to generate unhealthy vibrations, making the entire atmosphere full of misery."
3. **Apparent truth vs. actual truth.** You think you're miserable because of an insult. Actual truth: you're reacting to the unpleasant sensation produced by your saññā's evaluation.
4. **The four-part mind explains why intellectual understanding fails.** Saṅkhāra reacts to vedanā. Only observing vedanā with equanimity interrupts this at the root.
5. **Tihar Prison course (1,000 people) is proof of scale.** Vipassana has already been tested at mass level — both in Ashoka's time and now.
6. **Non-sectarian universality confirmed by diverse participation.** 15 nationalities, all Indian religious communities — same results, same law of nature.
7. **Beliefs about God and Soul are irrelevant.** Myanmar (80% no soul/no creator) and India (80% soul + creator) — people in both countries generate craving and aversion and become miserable. Beliefs don't change the mechanism.
8. **"Optimism" is intrinsic to the path.** "When you practise Dhamma, the law of nature, and apply it in your life, the benefits come here and now: akāliko, sandiṭṭhiko."

## Notable Quotes

> "Society is made up of individual human beings. To reform it, each member of society has to be reformed. Hence, the individual is the key."

> "We don't understand what is happening inside. When anger arises, we give all our attention to the outside object — the person, the situation — we try to rectify things outside."

> "Accepting things only at the devotional level... does not help. It is only one's own wisdom, one's own vijjā at the level of vedanā, one's own experience, that can liberate one — nothing else."

> "It is true that they are in prison. But the fact is that every individual in the entire world is in prison. We are all prisoners of our own behaviour patterns, in the prison of our own negativity."

> "They say that they feel so light. Why? Because the burden of impurities, the burden of accumulated negativity, has gone away."
