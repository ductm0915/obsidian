# This is the Way to End Dukkha

## Source Metadata

- **Author:** S.N. Goenka
- **Format:** Essay-length exposition of the fourth Noble Truth
- **Significance:** Step-by-step technical description of the Vipassana method as the practical path to end dukkha — connects theory of craving/aversion to the actual practice

---

## The Core Principle

> "If a thing arises due to a certain cause, it can certainly be eradicated by eradicating the cause."

Suffering arises because of craving and aversion. If these are completely eliminated, suffering is also eliminated. But this must be experienced in practice, not just accepted theoretically.

---

## Where Craving and Aversion Arise

The crucial discovery: craving and aversion *always arise whenever there is a sensation.* And sensation arises whenever there is contact of a sense-object with a sense-door.

Therefore, to eradicate craving and aversion, one must work **at the level of sensation** — where they actually arise. To do this, one must:
1. Develop the ability to be **aware** of all sensations throughout the body
2. Develop the faculty of **equanimity** toward all sensations (pleasant, unpleasant, neutral)

When equanimity is maintained, sensation arises but no craving or aversion is generated.

---

## The Three-Stage Method

### Stage 1: Sīla (Morality — the Base)
Abstain from actions that contribute to mental agitation:
- No lying, harsh words, slanderous talk, idle gossip
- No killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, intoxicants
- Honest livelihood

With this base, begin developing awareness of sensations — starting with the breath (below the nostrils, above the upper lip).

### Stage 2: Samādhi (Concentration)
Sit quietly, observing inhalation and exhalation at the nasal entrance. When the mind wanders, bring it back. This develops:
- Ability to keep mind fixed on one object
- Heightened sensitivity to experience sensations in that area
- The sharpened mind can now begin experiencing sensations throughout the physical structure

### Stage 3: Paññā (Wisdom)
Systematically move attention through the body, strengthening awareness of sensations and increasing the ability not to react. With this training:
- Pattern of thought begins changing: from craving/aversion/ignorance to dhamma (the way out of suffering)
- One starts realizing experimentally that sensations are manifestations of mind-matter contact — impermanent and ephemeral
- One realizes "this is not 'I', not 'mine', not 'my soul'"
- Real wisdom (*Paññā*) arises; emergence from illusions and delusions

---

## Layer-by-Layer Eradication

Each time equanimity is maintained and no new reaction is generated, the old stock of suppressed reactions rises to the conscious level — and is eradicated. Layer after layer of past conditioning comes to the surface.

> "By maintaining equanimity, one gets them eradicated, and thus finds relief from misery."

The process continues until the entire accumulation of past reactions has been eradicated and the habit of reacting itself has disappeared. Then:

> "Freed from all craving, aversion, ignorance — past, present and future — one experiences total freedom from suffering."

---

## Personal Effort Required

> "Simply reading articles, or engaging in discussions or debates, will not suffice. The only way to achieve liberation is by one's own efforts — the effort to observe the reality of oneself, within the framework of one's body, without reacting to it."
