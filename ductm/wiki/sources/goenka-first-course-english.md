# First Vipassana Course in English Language

A personal account by [[sn-goenka]] describing the breakthrough of conducting the first Vipassana course entirely in English, held at Shanti Kutir in Dalhousie (Himalayas) for a group of Western students.

## Summary

After establishing courses in India (1969–), Goenka initially gave Dhamma talks in Hindi, with only brief 5–10 minute English summaries for Western participants. A group of Western tourists in Dalhousie specifically requested an all-English course, but Goenka refused, citing his inability to give hour-long discourses fluently in English without reading from a text. The students complained directly to Sayagyi U Ba Khin, who then telephoned Goenka and ordered him to go, saying: "Whatever English you know will be enough. You will be successful. Dhamma will help. All my mettā is with you."

Goenka went to Dalhousie. For the first two days he was struck by inexplicable suffocation during discourses, barely managing 15–25 minutes. On the third day he discovered the cause: one student had a human skull and blood-stained kukri (curved Gurkha knife) in his room, kept at the instruction of a local tantric guru. After persuading the student to discard them, the anti-Dhamma vibrations cleared.

That evening, "Dhamma vibrations flooded my being through the top of the head" and Goenka found himself speaking English "as fluently as I speak Hindi" for a full hour. The course was a success. After this, all courses became bilingual — Hindi discourses in the morning, English in the evening.

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **The teacher's mettā as transmitter of Dhamma power**: U Ba Khin's blessing from Myanmar — despite physical distance — is described as the direct enabling force behind the English discourse becoming possible.
- **Dhamma vibrations and environmental purity**: The article gives concrete form to the idea that negative/impure practices in a meditation environment create tangible obstacles to Dhamma work. The tantric objects are specifically identified as "anti-Dhamma vibrations."
- **U Ba Khin's prophetic confidence**: "Whatever English you know will be enough" — the teacher's certainty preceded the miracle of fluency.
- **Himalayan setting as Dhamma-resonant**: Goenka describes a special affinity with the Himalayas, citing U Ba Khin's words: "Who knows how long in how many lives we have meditated in the serene caves of the Himalayas."
- **Bilingual delivery as a structural pivot**: Making all courses bilingual (Hindi + English) was the mechanism that enabled Western students to receive the full benefit of the discourses, which in turn enabled Vipassana's global spread.

## Notable Quotes

> "Whatever English you know will be enough. You will be successful. Dhamma will help. All my mettā is with you." — Sayagyi U Ba Khin

> "Just a few moments before the start of the discourse, Dhamma vibrations flooded my being through the top of the head. I began the discourse and I found I spoke English as fluently as I speak Hindi. I spoke for an entire hour."

> "It was Sayagyi's mettā that gave me the Dhamma strength to turn what seemed impossible to possible."

> "In a few years, thousands from about eighty countries came for Vipassana courses... The time had come for the prophecy and Dhamma wish of Sayagyi to come true."
