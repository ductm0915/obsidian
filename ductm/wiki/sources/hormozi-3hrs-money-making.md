# 3 Hours of Money Making Advice You Needed to Know Yesterday

**Author:** [[alex-hormozi]]  
**Type:** Business advice compilation — 8 tactical lessons from top Skool community owners  
**Context:** Hormozi spent 8 hours with top 10 Skool Games winners sharing money-making strategies  

## Overview

Compiled learnings from the highest-performing Skool community builders. Covers lead generation, pricing, differentiation, community engagement, team building, and avatar selection.

## Key Sections

### 1. Getting More Leads with Free Skool Groups
Use free Skool group as lead magnet: content → DM → link in bio/description → free group → paid upsell. Optimize School discovery ranking. Skool organic traffic (profile visits) is underutilized.

### 2. Pricing: The Strongest Lever on Profit
Pricing is often the highest-ROI lever — a 10% price increase may produce 50%+ profit increase if margins are tight. Price signaling affects quality perception. Most people underprice due to fear, not data.

### 3. Beating Competition by Being Different
Differentiation beats optimization. Find what the market does uniformly (industry tropes) and do the opposite ([[contrarian-belief]]). Being the same but better is a slow road; being categorically different is faster.

### 4. Community Engagement: What Actually Works
- **Overwhelm kills retention.** Simplicity wins. Delete content to reduce churn.
- Value per second beats seconds of value.
- Show up daily. Owner presence is the single highest-retention lever.
- Weekly call + weekly post = sufficient cadence for most communities.

### 5. Building a Community Network (Not Guru Model)
Peer-to-peer models outperform expert-to-student models at scale. Position the community as the value, not just yourself as guru. Facilitating connections between members creates stickier retention than content alone.

### 6. Strategy: Finding the Next Lever to Pull
Use [[what-who-when]] execution format. Identify the single highest-ROI lever (pricing, retention, lead gen) and go all-in before switching. Switching too early is the most common strategic mistake.

### 7. Growing Beyond You: Hiring and Delegating
Transition from operator to owner requires: hiring before you're ready, documenting processes before delegating, and measuring output not hours. [[discretionary-effort]] is unlocked by trust and autonomy, not command-and-control.

### 8. Client / Avatar Selection: Saying No to Wrong People
The wrong customers cost more than lost revenue — they drain time, erode culture, produce refunds, and generate negative reviews. The right 20% should become 100% of focus (Vista PE model from [[hormozi-13-years-marketing]]).

## Key Quotes

> "The pricing is often the single most leveraged thing you can change, because a small change in price can produce a disproportionate change in profit."

> "Most people think retention is about adding more. It's almost always about subtracting."
