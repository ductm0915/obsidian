# Saya Thetgyi

**Saya Thetgyi** (1873–1945) — Myanmar lay farmer and Vipassana teacher; student of [[ledi-sayadaw]] and direct teacher of [[sayagyi-u-ba-khin]].

---

## Background

- Born 1873 in Burma; died 1945
- Worked throughout his life as a **farmer** — he never became a monk or professional religious teacher in any formal sense
- Trained directly by [[ledi-sayadaw]], who opened Vipassana practice to laypeople

## Attainment

- Saya Thetgyi is described in Goenka's accounts as an **anāgāmī** — a non-returner, the third of the four stages of liberation in Theravāda Buddhism
- This is a remarkably advanced attainment, especially for a layperson living and working as a farmer
- His attainment stands as evidence that [[ledi-sayadaw]]'s experiment of bringing Vipassana to laypeople was not a dilution — full liberation remained possible outside the monastery

## The Lay Tradition He Embodied

Saya Thetgyi represents the clearest early proof of concept for the **lay Vipassana lineage**:

- Lived an ordinary working life (agriculture) while cultivating deep meditative realisation
- Taught from his own attainment, not merely from intellectual or scriptural authority
- His example inspired [[sayagyi-u-ba-khin]] — also a layman in government service — to pursue and then transmit the same practice

## Place in the Lineage

```
[[ledi-sayadaw]] → Saya Thetgyi → [[sayagyi-u-ba-khin]] → [[sn-goenka]]
```

Saya Thetgyi is the crucial middle link: he received the democratised teaching directly from its innovator and transmitted it to the teacher who would eventually send Vipassana to the whole world through [[sn-goenka]].
