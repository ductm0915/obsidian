# Cô Quế Minh

Teacher (Cô) at [[wit-organization]]. Full name: Lê Quế Minh (based on reference "cô giáo Lê Quế Minh" in session transcripts). Senior teaching staff with program-wide oversight responsibility.

## Role at WIT

Cô Quế Minh occupies a distinctive multi-role position at WIT:

- **Subject teacher**: Specializes in Phẩm Chất (Character Virtues) and Nguyên Lý (Principles) — the foundational inner architecture modules
- **Program overseer**: For Mentor K07, she oversaw the entire program from initial interviews through graduation, including all physical activity (thể chất), calligraphy (thư pháp), and social sessions
- **Quality guardian**: The only teacher in the WIT ecosystem who openly holds students to account — other teachers are described as gentle; she is described as willing to correct students directly, even publicly on Zoom
- **Personal development mentor**: She has personal disclosures in sessions that reveal deep integration of WIT philosophy in her own life journey

## Teaching Specializations

### Phẩm Chất (Character Virtues) — Primary Domain

Teaches the five Confucian virtues as applied in modern WIT context:
- **Nhân** (Benevolence)
- **Lễ** (Propriety/Ceremony) — see [[le-礼-respect-ceremony]]
- **Nghĩa** (Righteousness/Duty) — linked to tánh Danh, Tu thân — Tề gia — Trị quốc — Bình thiên hạ
- **Trí** (Wisdom/Intelligence)
- **Tín** (Trustworthiness) — giữ lời và đúng giờ

Her framing: "Phẩm chất nằm ở ngay trung tâm của mọi thứ — nó vừa là nhân, nó vừa là quả." Virtue is both cause and effect.

### 7 Bố Thí (7 Life Donations) — Referenced specialty

Students reference "7 Bố Thí" as part of her teaching domain. This refers to WIT's framework of seven forms of giving (donation/generosity) that generate merit and are integrated into daily life. The seven include: giving safety/fearlessness, giving smiles, giving kind words, giving time/presence, giving physical assistance, giving a seat, and giving room/space (variants across Buddhist traditions).

### Nguyên Lý (Principles)

Her subject domain in the Mentor curriculum's structured progression: Laws (Quy Luật) → Principles (Nguyên Lý) → Keys (Chìa Khoá). She delivers the Nguyên Lý module.

## Character and Teaching Style

Cô Quế Minh is known for:

- **Directness**: "Trong hệ sinh thái WIT, ngoài Minh ra hình như không có ai la các anh chị hết." (Within WIT's ecosystem, she seems to be the only one who directly challenges students.) She views this as care, not harshness.
- **Personal disclosure**: She shares her own life journey openly, including a period of wanting to die, and the conversation with her own teacher that turned her around (about taking responsibility for herself before she could serve others).
- **Liêm chính nội tâm**: Insists on internal honesty — saying what you actually think, not what sounds good.
- **Anti-thao-túng**: She explicitly states that no amount of gifts, status, or emotional appeals can influence her decisions — modeling the anti-manipulation principle she teaches.

## Personal Story (Self-Disclosed in Teaching)

In Session 9, Cô Quế Minh disclosed: she once wanted to die, and when she asked her teacher if she could join his work, he asked: "If you could do everything you want to do, would you still want to die?" She said no. He said "Then you can join." Her teacher used this to confirm she had begun to develop Nghĩa (responsibility) toward herself — the prerequisite for serving others.

She chose to remain single for years believing it gave her more time to serve — but her teacher told her: those who don't dare to take responsibility for a family will not be trusted with organizational responsibility. She was assigned only "general assistant" (tổng trợ lý) work until she could take on full commitment.
