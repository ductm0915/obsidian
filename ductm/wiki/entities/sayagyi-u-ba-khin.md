# Sayagyi U Ba Khin

**Sayagyi U Ba Khin** (1899–1971) — Myanmar government official and Vipassana master; direct teacher of [[sn-goenka]] and founder of the International Meditation Centre (IMC) in Rangoon.

---

## Background

- Born 1899 in Burma; passed away January 19, 1971
- Served as Accountant General of Burma — a senior government post — while practicing and teaching Vipassana
- Learned Vipassana from [[saya-thetgyi]], the lay meditator-farmer trained by [[ledi-sayadaw]]
- Founded the **International Meditation Centre (IMC)** in Rangoon in 1952 — one of the earliest formal centres for lay Vipassana practice

## Relationship with S.N. Goenka

- Accepted Goenka as a student in the 1950s despite Goenka's initial scepticism as a successful Burmese-Indian businessman
- Taught Goenka over fourteen years; authorised him to teach in 1969
- Goenka brought Vipassana to India in 1969 — fulfilling U Ba Khin's long-held **adhitthāna** (Dhamma wish) to see Vipassana return to its land of origin

## The Adhitthāna (Dhamma Wish)

- U Ba Khin made a formal adhitthāna that Vipassana would spread back to India, Buddha's homeland
- This wish was articulated approximately 23 years before Goenka's first course in India (1969)
- U Ba Khin died on January 19, 1971 — before witnessing Vipassana's full spread into the West, yet after Goenka had already begun teaching in India

## Character and Integrity

- Renowned for extraordinary personal integrity: refused to accept any personal gifts or material benefit from students
- Lived as a demonstration that Dhamma practice and worldly responsibility (high government service) are fully compatible
- Embodied the lay Vipassana tradition that [[ledi-sayadaw]] initiated — Dhamma as universal law, not monastic privilege

## Place in the Lineage

```
[[ledi-sayadaw]] → [[saya-thetgyi]] → Sayagyi U Ba Khin → [[sn-goenka]]
```

The lineage from Ledi Sayadaw through U Ba Khin to Goenka is the direct chain that brought Vipassana from Myanmar to the world at large.
