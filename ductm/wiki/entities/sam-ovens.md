# Sam Ovens

New Zealand-born entrepreneur and founder of [[skool-platform]]. Grew Consulting.com from his parents' garage (0 revenue, no college degree) to $30M+ per year in roughly 5–6 years, primarily via paid traffic. Co-founder of Skool alongside [[alex-hormozi]] and others.

---

## Background

- Grew up in New Zealand; moved to New York, then Venice Beach CA, then back to New Zealand
- Started Consulting.com — an online education business teaching people to start their own consulting businesses
- Built the business to $18M/year as a solo operator before hiring his first team
- Experienced catastrophic operational debt in 2016–2017 while scaling 2M → 18M → briefly retreating → 34M+
- Co-created and runs [[skool-platform]], a community platform with 50,000+ communities, 15M+ users, and $1B+ in earnings at time of recording

---

## Core Philosophy

Sam Ovens is a systems thinker and contrarian. His worldview:

1. **Full-stack thinking** — Success requires developing every layer of the mind: Mental Awareness → Mental Cognition → Business Principles → Business Disciplines → Processes/Tools. Most people only learn tools; billionaires master the full stack.
2. **Long-term orientation** — Every decision should be evaluated on its "effect horizon": immediate upside with long-term downside = wheelchair path; short-term pain with long-term gain = hill path. Always take the hill.
3. **Do less, not more** — Energy is finite. Focus it on one thing. Eliminate before optimizing. Social media, email, notifications = death by 1,000 cuts.
4. **Garbage in, garbage out** — Outputs derive from inputs. Obsess about quality of information, mentors, and environment you consume.
5. **Systems thinking over component thinking** — See everything as interconnected inputs → processes → outputs → feedback → environment. Every part affects the whole.
6. **Monk-like discipline** — Winners rely on discipline, not motivation. Motivation runs out; discipline doesn't. Build routine, reduce decisions, protect attention ruthlessly.

## Community Philosophy

Sam's most distinctive contribution to community design (from [[gray-sam-ovens-community-truth]]):

1. **Community ≠ audience** — Followers don't interact with each other. True community = member-to-member relationships. Creator facilitates; doesn't dominate.
2. **Fun is the real product** — "Happy hour > adding value." Fun leads to learning; learning doesn't lead to fun. Get the right people together; value emerges automatically.
3. **Curation over growth** — Quality > volume. The dentist community (80K dentists, 96% MAU, word-of-mouth only) grew slowly with deliberate curation and no promotions.
4. **Global philosophy + local cluster** — Religion's architecture: shared worldview (global) + physical meetups (local). Most people go to church for the other members, not the priest.
5. **Superfan empowerment** — Identify top contributors; give them a platform and agency. When members see others get rewarded for contributing, everyone wants to contribute.

## Product Design Philosophy

- Innovate on users' behalf rather than taking feature requests literally (Henry Ford / faster horse)
- Tesla settings menu → one menu for everything → directly inspired Skool's settings UX
- Steve Jobs: customers said they'd never pay $1K for a phone — hadn't seen the iPhone yet
- Cross-disciplinary learning (Da Vinci model): study biology, religion, architecture, software — always with the lens "what can I bring back to my work?"

## Skool Mission

- Help **1 billion people find community** by empowering **1 million creators** to earn full-time livings building communities online
- Economic model: subscription membership (Substack for writing, but for any passion)
- Math: 1M creators × 1,000 members each = 1B people in community
