# Skool (Platform)

## Overview

Community-building platform that [[alex-hormozi]] made his first-ever brand deal with (negotiated in 2023, announced January 2024). Positioned at the intersection of education, media, community, and business — a vehicle for creators and business owners to monetize expertise through paid communities.

## Key Metrics

- Millions of users on the platform
- Average paid community earns **$1,360/month**
- Hormozi considers it one of the best investments he's ever made

## Strategic Fit for Hormozi

Hits multiple pillars simultaneously:
- Education (his core expertise)
- Media (content creation)
- Community (social/IRL connection)
- Business opportunity vehicle (low-risk way for people to start a business)
- Operational leverage (scalable platform)

## Lessons from School (via Hormozi)

1. **CRO at high optimization is hard** — 16 major split tests on the School Games page; only 2 won. The more optimized something is, the more likely a change results in a decrease.
2. **Overwhelm = #1 cancellation reason** — addition rarely improves things; deletion and simplicity do. Maximize **value per second**, not seconds of value.
3. **Customers above everything** — Paul Graham: "You can solve almost every problem in a business by talking to your customers more."
4. **Flywheel effect** — Make media → attract business owners → do deals → get outcomes → make media about outcomes → repeat.

## Use Cases

- Every gym owner should have a School community
- Every realtor, HOA, e-commerce brand can use it for customer retention and feedback
- E-commerce: 100% reachability vs email deliverability issues; better product feedback, higher repeat purchase rates

## Origin Story (Sam Ovens)

- [[sam-ovens]] started making courses 10 years before founding Skool
- Observed: students ignored the LMS content portal but were extremely active in the Facebook group
- Noticed: success stories always thanked *community members*, not the course content
- Noticed: LMS platforms (Kajabi, Thinkific) were adding website customization features — moving in the wrong direction
- Noticed: Facebook's perception degrading + courses going mainstream → opportunity
- Decision: start Skool ~3.5 years before interview; used consulting.com cash flow to fund it

## Founding Vision

- **Mission**: Help **1 billion people find community** by empowering **1 million creators** to earn full-time living building communities online
- Economic model: **subscription membership** — the new internet business model (Substack for writing, OnlyFans for creators — but for all passions)
- 1M creators × avg 1,000 members each = 1B people

## Product Philosophy

- Skool settings menu modeled after Tesla (everything in one menu, one button)
- Product decisions: listen to all feedback → identify root need → innovate on behalf of users, not just fulfill literal requests
- Deletion-first design: maximize value per second, not seconds of value (aligns with [[value-per-second]])
- Team at time of interview: ~15 people (lean for the scale)

## Community Design Principles (per Sam)

See [[community-vs-audience]] and [[community-fun-first]] for full frameworks. Key Skool-specific example:
- Created "How are you using Skool?" category → members share use cases → community becomes the teacher
- Spotted Andrew Kirby using Skool creatively → had him create a course → shouted him out to email list → community became member-driven
