# Dan Koe

## Who He Is

Writer, entrepreneur, and philosopher. Koe's work sits at the intersection of entrepreneurship, philosophy, and self-development — drawing heavily from consciousness studies (Ken Wilber), ancient wisdom (Alan Watts, Stoicism), and practical business building. Known for the "one-person business" model and writing as the foundational skill.

## Background

- Started as a freelance web designer, then fitness YouTube, then writing/content
- Built a substantial following by documenting his philosophy and life operating system
- Age 26 when most of this content was recorded; positions himself as learning in public
- Teaches writing as the meta-skill underlying all other business skills

## Core Philosophy

Koe's worldview:
1. **Attention is the primary resource** — whoever controls your attention controls your life. All suffering stems from mismanaged attention ([[flow-state]], [[life-as-video-game]])
2. **Entropy is the default enemy** — without deliberate system-building, life declines into chaos ([[entropy-principle]])
3. **Clarity through negation** — you find your path faster by defining what you *don't* want ([[anti-vision]])
4. **Mastery over misery** — boring fundamentals aren't boring; they're the path to unconscious competence ([[frustration-tolerance]])
5. **Bring the future into now** — don't wait to practice tomorrow's habits; start tiny today ([[ideal-future-now]])

## Business Model

The path Koe prescribes (and walked himself):
1. Develop a high-income skill (writing, design, code)
2. Offer as a service → build early income and proof
3. Productize → move from client-time to scalable leverage
4. Build audience alongside → content as customer acquisition
5. Compress to [[four-hour-workday]] → asymmetric leverage

## Intellectual Influences

- **Ken Wilber** — integral theory, holism, consciousness levels; calls him "the most conscious man alive"
- **Alan Watts** — philosophy, Zen, non-dual awareness
- **David Deida** — masculinity, *Way of the Superior Man* (Koe refers to as "David Data" in transcript)
- **Mark Manson** — *The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F\*ck*; documented Wilber's rise and fall
- **Tim Ferriss** — *4-Hour Workweek* misread as "4-hour workday," became the kernel of Koe's productivity philosophy
