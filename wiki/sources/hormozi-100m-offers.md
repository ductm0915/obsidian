# Source Summary

Alex Hormozi's flagship book on creating "Grand Slam Offers" — offers so good people feel stupid saying no. The core thesis: most businesses compete on price because their offer is undifferentiated. By engineering the offer itself (not just the marketing), you can charge premium prices while making acquisition easier.

## Key Claims & Frameworks
- **Grand Slam Offer**: An offer that combines a dream outcome, high perceived likelihood of achievement, minimal time delay, and minimal effort/sacrifice.
- **Value Equation**: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Maximize the numerator, minimize the denominator.
- **Pricing**: Charge based on perceived value delivered, not cost of goods. The goal is to be in a "category of one" where price comparison is impossible.
- **Offer Stacking**: Bundle bonuses, guarantees, scarcity, and urgency to amplify perceived value far beyond the price point.
- Businesses fail at marketing because their offer is weak — the offer is the foundation everything else rests on.
