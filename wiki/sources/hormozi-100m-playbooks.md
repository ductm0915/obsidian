# Source Summary

A collection of tactical playbooks by Alex Hormozi (Acquisition.com, 2025). Each playbook distills one operational lever into actionable steps. Together they form a comprehensive business operations system.

## Playbooks Included

### Fast Cash Playbook (29 pages)
The fastest way to extract cash from an existing business. Focus on immediate revenue actions before growth initiatives — "stop the bleeding before growing."

### Hooks Playbook (26 pages)
How to double marketing results in under 5 seconds. Hooks are the first thing a prospect sees/hears that determines if they engage. Covers verbal hooks (headlines, opening lines) and nonverbal hooks (visual patterns, thumbnails). Volume testing of hooks is the highest-leverage marketing activity.

### Lead Nurture Playbook (21 pages)
Systematic follow-up sequences to convert warm leads who didn't buy immediately. Most businesses lose 80%+ of revenue by failing to follow up. Structured cadences across multiple channels (email, SMS, phone, retargeting).

### Lifetime Value Playbook (31 pages)
Strategies for maximizing the total revenue per customer over time. Core levers: increase purchase frequency, increase average order value, extend customer lifespan. LTV > CAC is the fundamental equation of profitable growth.

### Marketing Machine Playbook (52 pages)
A full system for building a predictable marketing engine. Covers the four ways to get customers (paid ads, content, outbound, referrals) and how to systematize each into a "machine" that runs without the founder.

### Price Raise Playbook (28 pages)
Step-by-step process for raising prices without losing customers. Key insight: low prices attract worse customers, create thinner margins, and prevent reinvestment. Raising prices is often the single fastest path to profitability.

### Pricing Playbook (61 pages)
Three models of pricing and instant ways to make more profit. Tactical plays include: switching from monthly to 28-day billing cycles, tiered pricing, and value-based pricing. Rules for pricing follow the principle: "charge as much as you can while maintaining the volume you need."

### Retention Playbook (35 pages)
How to get customers to keep buying. At 15% monthly churn, a business loses 83% of customers per year. Solving retention uses a DOD consulting framework: ask experts, get all answers, find common threads. Churn is the silent killer of business growth.
