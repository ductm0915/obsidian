# How We Built My Personal Brand to 265,000 Followers in 1 Year

[[caleb-ralston]] and his content director [[trevor-odum]] walk month-by-month through building Ralston's personal brand from zero to 265,000 followers and 44,000 email subscribers in one year.

## Key Claims and Arguments

- **Start with AI Database**: Trevor transcribed all existing Caleb content (only ~3 podcasts) into an AI database to map talking points and brand pillars before creating anything new.
- **Systems Before Content**: Set up project management (Notion board), Google Drive structure, and file naming conventions from day one — even when there was no content yet. Built for scale.
- **Introduction Video First**: First YouTube video was a 50-minute career walkthrough. Assume nobody knows who you are. Frame how the audience will view you by sharing credentials + applicable lessons.
- **6.5-Hour Free Course as Second Video**: Filmed "How to Build a Personal Brand" in an LA warehouse (Pier Space) over 3 days with just 2 people. From idea to published in under 2 months. Hit 750K+ views.
- **Bullet Points over Scripts**: Caleb is a poor reader. Heavily scripted intros, then bullet-point outlines for everything else. Adapted in real-time after day 1 of filming.
- **Scene Changes Re-Engage**: Different sets/outfits per section refresh the talent's energy and help viewers re-engage in long-form content.
- **Motion Graphics over Whiteboard**: Hired a motion designer (Michael) to add visual explanations instead of hand-drawn whiteboard — differentiation from industry standard.
- **Rson Select (Agency)**: Launched consulting firm during the year. Content drives leads; clients often come through private channels (DMs, texts) not public comments.
- **Platform Strategy**: YouTube (primary, innovation focus), Instagram + LinkedIn (maintenance mode). Eye of Sauron approach — one platform gets the innovation energy at a time.

## Notable Quotes

> "We came from teams that are much bigger... it sounds silly that we're going to build our project management board for content that doesn't exist yet. But in the back of our heads, we knew we're going to want to stay organized."

> "From idea to posted was less than two months. I started it on February 1st and we published it on April 4th."
