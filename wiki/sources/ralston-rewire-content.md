# In 47 Minutes, I'll Rewire How You Think About Content

[[caleb-ralston]] delivers a live talk debunking three common content lies, with practical exercises. A condensed version of his core philosophy delivered in a speaking/workshop format.

## Key Claims and Arguments

### Lie #1: All Views Are Good Views
- Not all awareness is positive. Pepsi's 2017 Kendall Jenner ad got 60M+ views — all negative. Views from bad pairings hurt more than help.
- Going on untrustworthy podcasts consistently = audience lumps you with that crowd. "In high school, if you sat at the goth table, you're automatically a goth."
- **Exercise**: Write 4 ideal brand associations — 2 things you're FOR, 2 things you're AGAINST. These become your north stars for content repetition.

### Lie #2: I'll Figure It Out As I Go
- Most creators have been posting for years with no clarity on why. Without a defined goal, "it doesn't matter which way you go" (Alice in Wonderland).
- **Brand Journey Framework**: 4 questions reverse-engineering from desired outcome to today. Provides your northstar and "why."
- **Content as Fitness**: Build a sustainable system first, like a good personal training program. Don't try Chris Bumstead's workout on day one.

### Lie #3: If I Just Keep Posting, It Will Work Eventually
- Posting without tracking = throwing bricks hoping they land straight. Quality is determined by the audience, not the creator.
- **[[accordion-method]]**: Expand (high volume) → find what audience wants → Contract (optimized, fewer posts). Use quantity to inform quality.
- **Outlier Tracking**: Grade content against your 90-day benchmark. A 10x outlier is more meaningful than raw view counts. Tool recommendation: oneof10.com for YouTube outlier detection.
- **Quality is Audience-Defined**: "I made a high quality video and nobody watched it" is a contradictory statement. If nobody watched, it wasn't quality.

## Notable Quotes

> "I do not believe all views are good views. I believe there are many cases where more awareness around a bad pairing hurts more than helps."

> "If you don't know where you want to go, how do you know what steps to take?"

> "You use quantity to inform what quality is."
