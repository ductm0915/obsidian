# How to Lead a Creative Team (Full Blueprint)

[[caleb-ralston]] shares a philosophy and tactical blueprint for leading creative/media teams, drawn from building an 18-person in-house team that produced 350 pieces/week generating billions of impressions.

## Key Claims and Arguments

### Philosophy: 5 Mindset Shifts
1. **Training ≠ Control**: The more you control the creative process, the more you stifle innovation. A brilliant manager who generates all ideas creates a single point of failure. Challenge them to step back; let the team ideate.
2. **Managing ≠ Leading**: Managing = telling what to do. Leading = casting vision, setting expectations, equipping with tools, giving ownership. Leader's job is empowerment, not task assignment.
3. **IC → Leader Identity Shift**: Moving from Individual Contributor to leader requires changing where you derive value. Your output is now the team's output. Ask "What did my team get done today?" not "What did I get done?"
4. **Fear/Micromanagement Kill Creativity**: "We Love a Good Flop" — celebrate experimentation failures. If creatives fear failure, they stick to safe uninspired work. Nobody remembers a post 30 seconds after scrolling past it.
5. **Creatives Care About Business Results**: They care deeply *when informed* how their work impacts revenue. Once an editor saw her documentary sell out a seminar, the whole team started thinking about driving sales.

### Tactical Blueprint
- **Platform Ownership**: Assign one person per platform. They own ideation + strategy + execution.
- **Checking On vs. Checking In**: "How are you doing? Any roadblocks?" vs. micromanaging lighting/framing.
- **Ownership Chunks**: Give ownership at varying levels — a whole campaign, or just the audio monitoring. Adjust up/down based on individual readiness.
- **Delegate to Breed Leaders**: Delegating short-form review to Trevor → he took more ownership → promoted to manager at 22 → freed Caleb to think 3-6-12 months ahead.
- **Share the Data**: Show how organic content drives revenue, not just platform metrics. Visibility into business impact = engaged team.
