# Source Summary

Jeremy Miner's NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning) framework — a question-based selling methodology that uses psychology-driven question sequences to guide prospects through their own decision-making process, rather than pitching or pushing.

## Key Claims
- Traditional sales (pitching features/benefits) triggers fight-or-flight resistance.
- Questions bypass psychological defenses by making the prospect think and feel rather than react.
- NEPQ structures questions in a specific sequence to lead prospects to self-discover why they need to buy.
- Focuses on emotional engagement before logical justification.
