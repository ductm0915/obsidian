# Optimize for Trust

**Optimizing for Trust** is a content strategy that prioritizes the depth of relationship and perceived credibility over the breadth of reach (virality).

## Core Principles
- **Customer > Follower**: It is better to have 1,000 people who trust you deeply (and might buy) than 1,000,000 who just find you entertaining.
- **Proof of Work**: Use the [[credibility-bank]] to prove you have achieved results.
- **Psychological Safety**: Creative work (and brand building) thrives when the environment feels safe to take risks, not when it is dictated by algorthmic fear.
- **Qualifiers**: Use qualifiers to prove expertise to a "cold" audience in every piece of content.

## Trust Definition
Caleb defines trust as: "How much you believe that someone or something will meet your expectations in the future based on how they have behaved in the past." Build it by repeatedly solving painful problems for your audience.

## 4-Step Trust Content Formula
1. Identify the painful problems your customer faces.
2. Define your unique solution.
3. Set expectations for what this piece of content will solve.
4. Make it easy to take action.
Repeat → trust compounds over time.

## The Viral Trap
Optimizing for virality requires wider content → dilutes message → attracts non-buyers → alienates existing customers. Views go up, sales go down. "It's like a whiskey and coke when you really want the full-proof whiskey."

## Effectiveness
This strategy is particularly effective for **high-ticket, narrow-market service providers**. In these niches, the conversion often happens in private channels (DMs/email) because the public content established a "Trusted Figure" status rather than a "Creator" status.
