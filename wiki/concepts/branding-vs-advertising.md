# Branding vs Advertising

Alex Hormozi distinguishes strictly between Advertising and Branding:
- **Advertising** is the act of letting people know about your stuff.
- **Branding** is the deliberate pairing of things through an outcome. 

Good branding pairs your business, product, or self with things that your IDEAL customers already like, yielding a positive behavioral change. Good advertising can be bad branding if it exposes your product to many people but associates it with something your ideal customers dislike.

## Core Properties
1. **Influence:** Does the brand change someone's behavior?
2. **Direction:** Does it drive them towards or away from the business?
3. **Reach:** How many people does it affect?

## The 70/30 Rule (New Balance Story)

New Balance's CMO flipped advertising spend from 70% bottom-of-funnel (buy shoes) / 30% top-of-funnel (brand awareness) to **30% bottom / 70% top**. First 18 months: revenue decreased. Month 19 onward: explosive, compounding growth. This corroborates the **3:1 ratio** (studied across TV and radio): at least 3 value-giving impressions for every 1 ask. Story shared by [[ben-francis]] and Gymshark's head of brand.

**Implication:** Brand building must be measured in years, not months. Expect an 18+ month lag before ROI materializes.

## B2B Branding Strategies

For B2B businesses (over half of all businesses), three paths to build brand:

1. **Help them accomplish their goals for free** — content, education, direct assistance
2. **Help someone like them** — case studies, social proof showing results for similar businesses
3. **Accomplish the aspirational outcome yourself** — extreme results get "regressed down" by prospects (Northface survives Everest → keeps me warm; Hormozi's $46M exit → his advice probably helps my $2M business)
