# Two-Column Approach

The **Two-Column Approach** is [[caleb-ralston]]'s exercise for discovering your [[contrarian-belief]].

## How It Works

| Left Column | Right Column |
|---|---|
| Things people in your space **say** that you disagree with | Your unique view on it |
| Things competitors **do** that you disagree with or think your customers hate | What you would do differently |

## Output

The result is a list of contrarian takes — beliefs or actions that differentiate you from the dominant industry tropes. Test them in your content; double down on the one that resonates most with your ideal customer.

## Ralston's 3 Starting Takes

1. "Always try to go viral" → **Optimize for trust, not virality** (winner)
2. "Post on every platform daily" → **Build a sustainable system you'll actually stick with**
3. "Your media team should fear you" → **Create psychological safety or the creative work suffers**

"Optimize for trust" caught fire — the audience quoted it back, DMs spiked, and clients referenced it. He doubled down.
