# Sawdust Business

## Definition

A business or revenue stream created from **excess capacity or waste products** that already exist within an operation. Named after lumber mills that turned sawdust waste into plywood by mixing it with glue.

## Origin Story

Lumber mills stripped trees into planks and swept up mountains of sawdust daily. A foreman realized: mix the sawdust with glue → plywood. An entirely new industry born from waste.

Similarly: a fur business owner cut jackets from pelts, used the snippets to make earmuffs → sold or gave as bonuses.

## Modern Examples

- **Airbnb:** Spare bedrooms (unused capacity) → rental income
- **Uber:** Idle cars and drivers → ride service
- **Gym after hours:** Facility is closed 8:30 PM–4:30 AM — that's paid-for space generating zero revenue
- **Acquisition.com workshops:** Unused office desks + portfolio team with spare bandwidth → 2-day workshops for potential portfolio companies. Result: deal flow + cash flow + content + talent flow from one already-paid-for resource.
- **SOPs as lead magnets:** Internal checklists and processes → repurposed as free downloads that generate leads

## How to Find Sawdust

1. Write down with detail everything you do on a regular basis (calls, conferences, content, meetings)
2. Ask: "Is there a way to recombine these things into something valuable at no extra cost to me?"
3. Look for moves that check **multiple boxes** simultaneously (content + deals + cash + talent)

## Principle

> "I don't want a single box check. I want four boxes checked."

The best sawdust plays use an existing cost basis to create value across multiple dimensions. The resource is already paid for — you're just finding additional uses.
