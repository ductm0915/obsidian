# Six Horsemen of Business Stagnation

## Definition

Six recurring "rock and hard place" scenarios that cause businesses to plateau. Each requires choosing short-term pain over indefinite stagnation.

## The Six Horsemen

### 1. Underpriced
- **Symptom:** Full capacity, not making money.
- **Fear:** Raising prices → losing customers.
- **Fix:** Use the **5+20 rule** — every 5 sales at current price, bump price 20%. Repeat until net revenue decreases. Fewer customers at higher price = more profit + less delivery cost.

### 2. Overcompensation
- **Symptom:** Employee comp (e.g., revenue share) scales faster than profit.
- **Fear:** Renegotiating → they leave.
- **Fix:** Shore up infrastructure (hire backup), then renegotiate with a transition period (keep current pay for 2–3 months, then shift to new structure).

### 3. Infinite Avatars
- **Symptom:** Serving too many different customer types. Can't nail messaging or offers.
- **Fear:** Narrowing → selling fewer customers.
- **Fix:** 80/20 your customer base. Identify top 20%, build messaging and offers exclusively for them, price for the value you deliver to that niche.

### 4. Overextension
- **Symptom:** Opened location 2/3 before location 1 is truly self-running. Profit drops.
- **Fear:** Closing a location = admitting failure.
- **Fix:** Either hire an impressive operator (may cost your current income) or prune back and reconcentrate. Run a **6-month test**: business must maintain or grow without your direct involvement.

### 5. More Than One Business
- **Symptom:** Running 2–3 businesses, none growing meaningfully.
- **Fear:** Sunk cost of abandoning one.
- **Fix:** Pick one. Zuckerberg didn't have a side hustle. The opportunity is in the problems you need to solve in your one business, not in the next shiny idea.

### 6. No Data
- **Symptom:** Can't diagnose what's wrong because you don't measure anything.
- **Fear:** None really — just distraction by shinier tasks.
- **Fix:** Start collecting data. **Test for relevance:** if this metric goes up or down, does it change our behavior? If not, don't track it.

## Pattern

All six share the same structure: **short-term pain (finite) vs. long-term stagnation (infinite)**. The entrepreneur's job is to choose the finite pain.
