# AAA Method (3A Reframing)

## Definition
The AAA Method is a framework for handling objections via **Reframing** — the 1–3 sentences said after a prospect says anything but "yes" that increase the likelihood the next thing you say gets them to buy. The person asking the questions controls the conversation.

## The Three Steps
1. **Acknowledge**: Validate the prospect's concern without agreeing that it prevents a purchase. Repeat what they said back to them — this demonstrates active listening and buys you time to think.
2. **Associate**: Connect their concern to a broader context, a past experience, or another person. This gives the prospect a positive **identity label** to live up to (e.g., "you seem like a smart person"). That label can be brought back at the close to create alignment.
3. **Ask**: Redirect the conversation by asking a targeted, qualifying question. Universal go-to: *"What are you most afraid of?"* — this gets to the heart of the issue fastest.

## 5 Rules for Ethical Use
1. **Never answer a question you don't know the answer to** — reframe instead of guessing.
2. **Never answer an unasked question** — don't volunteer objections the prospect hasn't raised.
3. **The nastier the prospect, the nicer you get** — escalate kindness, not combativeness.
4. **Match or exceed emotional intensity** — validate at the level of their concern ("That's a totally reasonable concern") before reframing.
5. **Retain childlike curiosity at all times** — seek to understand, not to win. Say "Huh, that's so interesting…" to buy time and shift tone.

## Key Principle
> "You don't close sales by being right. You close sales by making the prospect right."

The goal is to understand, not to beat them. If they feel you won an argument logically, you've lost the sale.
