# Kind Not Nice

## Definition

A cultural principle that distinguishes between **kindness** (truth-seeking, giving people the information they need to improve) and **niceness** (seeking approval, avoiding discomfort, withholding feedback).

## Criticism vs Insult

This is the operational core of the concept:

| | Criticism | Insult |
|---|---|---|
| **Definition** | The gap between desired and actual | Assigning a negative label to a person |
| **Example** | "Expectation: arrive by 9:00. You arrived at 9:05. Here's what needs to change." | "You're lazy." |
| **Effect** | Factual, actionable, no emotional charge | Emotionally charged, shuts down learning |
| **Content example** | "Channel average is 100K views. Your content averages 75K. Let's break down what to change." | "Your content sucks." |

## Framework for Giving Feedback

1. **State the gap** — desired vs actual (factual observation)
2. **Tell them what to do instead** — not just "don't do this" but "do this instead of this"
3. **Break down the skill** — sometimes lateness isn't laziness, it's poor time estimation. Teach: "Do you estimate based on best-case or worst-case travel time?"

> "No one can operate on a negative. Give them directions."

## Cultural Frame

Reed Hastings (Netflix): *"We are a professional sports team trying to win the World Championships. We are not a family."*

- You're not going to fire your kids, but you will release a player who isn't performing.
- The kindest thing = clear feedback + opportunity to improve. If they don't improve → opportunity to work somewhere they fit better.
- In a high-performance culture, the team itself should police standards. If someone isn't pulling weight, the culture removes them — it shouldn't have to come from the top.

## Anti-Pattern: "No Man Left Behind"

Treating a business like a government program where everyone is accommodated regardless of performance. This:
- Drains resources from high performers
- Signals that standards are negotiable
- Creates an environment where the best people leave first (they have options)
