# We Love a Good Flop

**"We Love a Good Flop"** is [[caleb-ralston]]'s cultural principle for creative teams that normalizes experimentation failure to unlock innovation.

## The Principle
- When creatives are afraid of failing, they stick to safe, uninspired work → output stagnates.
- Celebrating experimentation failures (not celebrating poor work) signals that taking risks is valued.
- "If you have something that flops, guess what? Nobody gives a shit. 30 seconds after they scroll past it, they've already forgotten."
- A 7-day streak of underperformance IS a problem to investigate. But individual experiments flopping is expected and healthy.

## Implementation
- Praise and celebrate the team when experiments don't work (not just when they succeed).
- Ensure all leadership is bought in — not just the top leader.
- The talent (person on camera) must also embrace this philosophy, not just the team.
