# Accordion Method

The **Accordion Method** is [[caleb-ralston]]'s content optimization framework that alternates between high-volume testing and focused optimization phases.

## How It Works

### Expand Phase (Volume)
- Post at higher volume to gather data on what your audience wants.
- The purpose is learning, not performance. More volume = more data on what resonates.
- "Quality is something you don't know. Your audience determines what quality is."

### Contract Phase (Optimization)
- Take the same effort that went into high volume → channel it into fewer, better pieces.
- Optimize based on content that performed above your benchmark (outliers).
- You're "increasing the odds that these videos will perform better because you are optimizing off of content that has performed better."

## Key Principle
**Use quantity to inform quality.** The common quality-vs-quantity debate is based on a misunderstanding. You don't know what quality is until your audience tells you through their behavior. High volume in the expand phase is the fastest way to discover it.

## Tracking
- Calculate your 90-day benchmark (avg views, likes, watch time).
- Grade all future content against this benchmark.
- A 10x outlier is the signal — not raw view count.
- Tool: oneof10.com for YouTube outlier detection.

## Relates To
- Directly complements the [[eye-of-sauron-method]]: expand phase maps to the platform under the "eye"; maintenance platforms stay contracted.
- Analogous to [[volume-negates-luck]] from [[alex-hormozi]]: high repetition reveals what works.
