# Brand Pairing Formula

The **Brand Pairing Formula** defines the mechanism by which internal branding efforts become External Brand Identity.

$$Pairing \times Consistency = Association$$

## Components
1. **Pairing**: The intentional act of linking yourself to a specific belief, outcome, or attribute (e.g., Apple pairing itself with "Design First").
2. **Consistency**: The repetition of this pairing over a long timeframe (years, not weeks).
3. **Association (The Brand)**: The byproduct. The lens through which the audience automatically views the individual or company (e.g., When you think of Apple, you automatically think of design).

## Strategic Implications
- **Negative Pairings**: The formula works in reverse. Consistently appearing with untrustworthy people or low-quality topics creates a negative association that acts as a "brand threat."
- **Brand Statements**: Use a concise statement to repeat the pairing.
- **Formula**: *"I believe [audience] who want [core desire] should [contrarian belief] not [common belief in niche]."*
