# Flywheel Effect

Jim Collins' concept of a self-reinforcing business loop where each step naturally feeds the next. The key is that once sparked, the engine runs and compounds without requiring proportional additional input. Contrasted with linear input-output growth where you must keep adding more effort to the front end.

## Core Principle
> Can you draw a circle within your business where each step must follow from the previous one?

The wheel feeds itself. The only requirement is to get it spinning.

## Examples

### Acquisition.com Flywheel
1. Make business media →
2. Attract interested business owners →
3. Do deals with those businesses →
4. Get outcomes to make media about →
5. Repeat

### Amazon Flywheel
1. Cheapest products with highest review fidelity →
2. More customers →
3. More suppliers →
4. More competition and selection →
5. Even more customers →
6. Repeat

## Application
- **Identify friction points** that prevent the wheel from spinning, then smooth them out
- **Build multiple flywheels** within a business that self-reinforce
- **Prioritize wheel-building over linear growth** — linear requires constant input; flywheels compound

## Key Distinction
A linear business: output = f(input). You stop pushing, growth stops.
A flywheel business: output = f(accumulated momentum). Energy compounds and the wheel spins faster over time.
