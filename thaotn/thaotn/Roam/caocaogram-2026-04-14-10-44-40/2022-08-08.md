    * [[Có điều gì nổi bật]] Bạn không muốn đánh mất những gì bạn học được khi đọc và nghe, vì vậy nên bạn ghi lại những nội dung chính.
      * People quit things for 1 reason: Dopamine. Well really, it's *lack* of dopamine. You see, most people set a goal and think: "I'll be happy once I achieve this goal."
      * This is the way:
        * First, stop setting goals – at least the way you're currently setting them.
          * Instead, choose an outcome you want to achieve.

Then, switch the goal to:

"I want to enjoy every second on the journey to achieving X."
        * Then, optimize for progress.Find a way to make
          * Tiny, constant gains
          * Every day or every week
          * For long periods of time

Trust me – you will get addicted to the tiny bits of progress (and stick with it forever).
        * By optimizing for progress, it's easy to stay motivated. But, motivation won't last forever. Along the way, there will be plenty of times where you don't feel like doing the things you know you need to do. Which is where the last mindset shift comes in – the Daily Leapfrog.
          * Every day you take another step on the path of monotonous consistency, picture this:

There are thousands of people who pursued a similar goal, but quit on *this exact day* on their journey.

So picture yourself *leapfrogging* all of those people.
