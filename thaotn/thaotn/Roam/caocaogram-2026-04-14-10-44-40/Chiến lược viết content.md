  * 1) Idea Generation
    * Since I only have to worry about one topic per week, my content consumption can be very targeted.
I start all of this out by researching a topic that is guaranteed to do well.
    * These validated content ideas come from:
- The most popular YouTube videos of my favorite channels
- Medium article headlines
- Previous tweets that have done well
- Something that is on my mind and just want to write about
Now I can focus on and dissect that topic all week.
  * 2) Outlining
    * At the start of each week, I fill in my content template with:
- The main topic
- Problems associated with that
- Objections to that problem
- Benefits of overcoming the problem
- Personal experiences with that topic
Then I outline the articles key points.
  * 3) Working Without Working
    * I have 3 main things that I talk about:
- Online business
- Human potential
- Creativity
In my newsletters, I try to work all of these pillars into one post.
    * Example: If I am studying spirituality and being in the present moment — I can talk about how that impacts your ability to be creative in online business.
Everything connects if you want it to connect.
In essence, I can consume ANYTHING slightly related to those.
    * When I am consuming, it is easy to catch certain parts that can be added or referenced to make the newsletter stronger.
When those gold nuggets catch my attention, I write it down in my Quick Capture notes and expand on the idea a bit relating to the newsletter.
    * This is an extremely important step (most won't realize it).
Having a constant stream of ideas flooding your mind will help you write creatively.
Plus, the pattern recognition raises those good dopamine levels in your brain (life becomes more enjoyable with this habit)
  * 4) Writing
    * Now that I have key points fleshed out with a few talking points and resources to pull from — I start writing.
I use the APAG copywriting framework I created
    * A = attention (agitating and painting a picture of the problem)
P = perspective (describing a perspective on the topic that I see as wrong and why)
A = advantage (the benefits behind seeing it from my perspective)
G = gamify (steps to overcome the problem and a challenge)
    * I created this framework by bringing the best of psychology, persuasion, and spirituality together (understanding how humans work)
I follow a general storytelling structure:
- Hook or headline
- Lead (the problem)
- Body (the transformation)
- Conclusion (call to action)
  * 5) Cross Posting
    * As I am writing the article, ideas for tweets inevitably come to mind.
I write and schedule those immediately.
Every day after writing, I cross post my best tweets to Instagram and LinkedIn. The ideas are already validated & will perform well.
    * When the newsletter is finished I:
- Send it to my list
- Upload it to my site
- Condense into a thread
- Use it as a YouTube vid script
- Go over the key points in a podcast
Now I've turned 1 validated content idea into content that will grow on all platforms.
  * 6) Content Ecosystem
    * Sales funnels are great, but content ecosystems are better.
It's like deploying tiny salesman that live on the internet and sell for you 24/7
Here is my process for selling without selling
    * This part is assuming that you:
1) Have an offer that sells
2) Have a funnel for your offers
As you are writing the newsletter, plug your offers in relevant places (and ALWAYS plug them at the end).
    * Now, the only thing you need to focus on to make sales is:
- Creating content on all platforms
- Plugging a relevant newsletter once per day on all platforms
That's it.
You grow your newsletter, make sales, and land clients without selling on the timeline.
  * To recap:
- Generate validated ideas
- Outline a newsletter
- Consume to fill in the blanks
- Cross post along the way
- Plug the newsletter to make sales
